Hedging & Correlation Shocks

Advanced24 minGeneral education

Educational content, not financial advice.

Learning Objectives
  • Recognize correlation spikes.
  • Use simple hedges & reduce exposure.
  • Measure net beta/sector risk.
Core Concepts
  • Correlation Regimes: Calm vs stress correlations.
  • Simple Hedges: Index or inverse ETFs/futures; options overlays.
  • Net Exposure: Beta‑adjusted portfolio view.
Examples
  • Hedges are cost centers; use judiciously.
Checklist
  • Cut gross & net exposure during crises.
  • Avoid over‑hedging single names.
Exercises
  1. Estimate portfolio beta.
  2. Design a crisis hedge plan.
Key Terms
TermDefinition
BetaSensitivity of asset to market moves.

Quick Quiz (self‑check)

Q1. In crises, correlations:

  • A. Drop to zero
  • B. Often rise toward one
  • C. Are unknown
  • D. Turn negative

Answer: B
