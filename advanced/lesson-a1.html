Backtesting Without Bias

Most “holy grails” die when confronted with clean data and realistic execution. Your goal is not a perfect curve but a believable one.

Bias checklist

  • Lookahead: Use only information known at bar close.
  • Survivorship: Include delisted symbols when testing equities.
  • Corporate actions: Use adjusted data consistently.
  • Slippage/fees: Model both; widen slippage in fast markets.
  • Overfitting: Fewer knobs, wider ranges, cross‑validation.
Practice: Re‑run one backtest with +50% slippage and +100% fees. If the edge disappears, it was execution‑fragile.
