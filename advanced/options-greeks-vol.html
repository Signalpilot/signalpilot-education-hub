Options Greeks & Volatility Primer

Advanced26 minGeneral education

Educational content, not financial advice.

Learning Objectives
  • Understand delta, gamma, theta, vega.
  • Relate IV to expected move.
  • Avoid common vol traps.
Core Concepts
  • Greeks: Delta (direction), gamma (convexity), theta (decay), vega (vol sensitivity).
  • IV & Expected Move: Rough move ≈ IV × price × sqrt(time).
  • Event Risk: IV crush after known catalysts.
Examples
  • Short gamma bleeds on jumps; long gamma needs movement.
Checklist
  • Avoid short naked options without experience.
  • Respect liquidity and assignment.
Exercises
  1. Compute expected move for a sample IV.
  2. Explain IV crush impact post‑event.
Key Terms
TermDefinition
GammaRate of delta change.
IVImplied volatility.

Quick Quiz (self‑check)

Q1. Theta exposure means:

  • A. Sensitivity to vol
  • B. Time decay effect
  • C. Direction only
  • D. Dividends

Answer: B
