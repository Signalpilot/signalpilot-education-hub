Kelly, Fractional Kelly & Drawdown Control

Advanced22 minGeneral education

Educational content, not financial advice.

Learning Objectives
  • Estimate Kelly and why to scale it down.
  • Balance growth vs pain.
  • Apply caps to smooth paths.
Core Concepts
  • Kelly Criterion: Maximizes long‑run growth; aggressive.
  • Fractional Kelly: Scale down to reduce drawdowns.
  • Caps: DD caps, exposure caps, timeouts.
Examples
  • Full Kelly is intolerably volatile for most.
Checklist
  • Use fractional Kelly if at all; monitor realized variance.
Exercises
  1. Compute Kelly for a 55%/1R edge.
  2. Choose a fraction and justify.
Key Terms
TermDefinition
Kelly FractionChosen proportion of full Kelly.

Quick Quiz (self‑check)

Q1. Full Kelly tends to:

  • A. Minimize variance
  • B. Maximize growth with high DD
  • C. Eliminate drawdowns
  • D. Be riskless

Answer: B
