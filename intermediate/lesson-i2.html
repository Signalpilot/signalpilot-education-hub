Volatility: ATR, Stops & Targets

Average True Range (ATR) measures typical move size. It’s not a crystal ball—it’s a tape measure. Use it to set expectations and design risk.

Applications

  • Stops: 1.0–1.5× ATR beyond structure to avoid random noise.
  • Targets: Pick reachable multiples (e.g., 1–2× ATR) given session volatility.
  • Sizing: Bigger ATR → smaller size for constant risk.

Volatility regimes

Clustered volatility means high‑vol days travel together. Expect wider whips and faster targets; trade fewer, better setups.

Practice: Track ATR on your trading TF for two weeks. Note hit rate and RR when ATR is in the top/bottom quartile of its 3‑month range.
