Strategy Anatomy: Entry, Exit, Filter, Size

A strategy is a contract with yourself: when to act, when to pass, and how much to risk. Entries get attention; exits determine reality.

Four parts

  • Entry: Your signal or setup.
  • Exit: Stop loss and profit‑taking logic; time‑based exits reduce overstay.
  • Filters: Trend/volatility/structure rules to avoid bad environments.
  • Position sizing: Fixed‑fraction or volatility‑normalized.

Prototype example

Breakout system: trade only when above yesterday’s high, ATR above median, and session range expanding. Enter on break + retest; exit at 1.5–2R or time stop end‑of‑day.

Practice: Write your current strategy in one page. If the exit section is shorter than the entry, expand it until it’s at least equal.
