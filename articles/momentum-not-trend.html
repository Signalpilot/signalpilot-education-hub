Momentum

Momentum ≠ Trend

Reading time ~7 min • Educational only

Momentum is the short‑term impulse. Trend is the longer‑term drift. You can have strong momentum against a trend (pullback) and zero momentum inside a trend (slow grind). Conflating them causes most “why did this fail?” moments.

Separate the jobs

  • Bias/trend: EMA stack / MACD+ regime / 200‑EMA slope.
  • Momentum: RSI Triad / SRSI+ flips / MACD histogram change.
  • Participation: PVA/OBV agreement.

Implications

  • In trends, take momentum with the drift; in ranges, fade momentum at the edges.
  • Never call a trend change off a single oscillator peak/trough.
  • Use structure (SD zones, anchors) so momentum has somewhere to push from.

Test it yourself

Pick one instrument. Mark trend using 200‑EMA slope. Now look at RSI Triad turns: with‑trend entries vs. counter‑trend entries. Record R‑multiples. The difference is the whole point.
