ATR & volatility (position sizing)

Reading time ~12–15 min • Educational only

Working theory: ATR is your volatility ruler. Stops are placed in multiples of ATR; position size derives from risk R divided by stop distance.

Formulas

  • True Range = max(H−L, |H−Cprev|, |L−Cprev|)
  • ATR(14) = EMA of True Range

Stops from ATR

Pick k by regime: quiet trend 1.2–1.4; noisy range 1.6–2.0. Stop distance = k × ATR.

Size from stop

Let R be dollars risked on the trade. Size ≈ R / stop_distance.

Example: Account $20k, r=0.5% ⇒ R=$100. ATR=$1.20, k=1.5 ⇒ stop=$1.80 ⇒ size ≈ 100 / 1.80 ≈ 55 units.

Choosing k

  • Trend day: shallow pullbacks → k≈1.3
  • Chop: whipsaws → k≈1.8+
  • Event risk: widen k or halve R

Edge cases

  • Gaps/huge candles: clamp ATR or use median ATR to avoid one-off spikes.
  • Futures: convert ATR to ticks, then currency.
  • No mid-trade resizing unless a written rule says so.

Checklist

  • Fix r% and k before the setup.
  • Stop outside structure and ≥ k×ATR.
  • Journal MAE/MFE to refine k by regime.
