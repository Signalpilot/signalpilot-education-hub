Risk 101: R-multiple, stop types, win% vs. expectancy

Reading time ~12–15 min • Educational only

R is the dollars you lose if the stop hits. Expectancy E in R is E = Win% × AvgWin − Loss% × AvgLoss.

Math that matters

Example: 45% × 1.9R − 55% × 1.0R = +0.305R. Lower win% can still win if payoffs are bigger.

Stop styles

  • Hard stop (resting order)
  • Soft stop (alert then market/limit)
  • Time stop (good-until, close the trade if N bars)

Sequence risk

Losses cluster. Keep r small (0.25–1.0%). If drawdown hits 5R, halve size and only take A+ setups until back above water.

Guardrails

  • Daily stop (e.g., 2R). Walk away after.
  • No martingale. Add only after confirmation.
  • Journal MAE/MFE to improve exits.
