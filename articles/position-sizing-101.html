Position Sizing 101: Risk per Trade & R‑multiples

Reading time ~9 min • Educational only

Good entries can’t save reckless sizing. Use a fixed account risk per trade (e.g., 0.5–1.0%) and compute size from your stop distance. Then evaluate outcomes in R‑multiples so different trades are comparable.

Step 1 — pick account risk

Choose a constant risk fraction. Example: 1% on a $10,000 account → $100 risk per trade.

Step 2 — define the stop from structure

Stops live below/above structure (zone/anchor). Suppose your long entry is 50, stop is 48 → $2 per share at risk.

Step 3 — compute position size

Position size = AccountRisk / StopDistance. From the example: $100 / $2 = 50 shares.

Measure results in R

  • Risk (1R) = $100 here.
  • Win of $250 = +2.5R regardless of symbol or timeframe.
  • Keep daily/weekly R‑drawdown limits to avoid death by churn.
Practice: backtest your method on 20 trades. Force risk = 1R each time. Track average R and max drawdown in R. If the method only wins with oversized bets, it’s not a method.
