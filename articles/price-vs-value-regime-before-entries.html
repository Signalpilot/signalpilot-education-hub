Price vs. Value: Regime before Entries

Reading time ~8 min • Educational only

Entries feel important. Regime is more important. If the market is ranging, trend systems bleed. If it’s trending, mean‑reversion systems bleed. Decide the state first; only then pick a play.

Quick ways to classify regime

  • EMA‑stack: 50 above 100 above 200 = uptrend; reversed = downtrend; scrambled = range.
  • 200‑EMA slope: positive/negative vs. flat is often enough.
  • ATR contraction/expansion: low and falling volatility often precedes range; expansion accompanies breaks.
  • Anchored VWAP: repeated rejections at a prior anchor suggest value is defended.

Plays by regime

Trend

  • Buy dips in uptrends / sell rips in downtrends.
  • Look for PVA Bull/Bear at structure; first ATR pullback is often the least painful.

Range

  • Fade extremes back to the mean; don’t chase breakouts without expansion + participation.
  • Let RSI/SRSI do the timing; respect the box.

A tiny checklist

  • Did regime change? (e.g., 200‑EMA slope flipped, stack crossed)
  • Is participation present? (PVA/OBV agree)
  • Where is structure? (SD zones, anchors, VWAPs)
