Risk Management 101: Sizing & Stops

Survivability comes from sizing. Decide risk in money first, then compute quantity from stop distance.

One simple framework

  • Risk per trade: 0.5%–1.0% of account for beginners.
  • Place stops beyond the invalidation level (structure or volatility).
  • Size = Risk ÷ Stop distance ÷ Value per point.

Worked example

$10,000 account, risk 1% ($100). Stop is $0.50 away. Stock moves $1 per share. Size = 100 / 0.50 = 200 shares. If filled, a stop hit loses ~$100 before fees/slippage.

Targets & R multiples

Think in R (risk units). If your target is 2R and you risk $100, your plan is to make $200 when right. This keeps expectations consistent across trades.

Practice: Build a table: stop distances from 0.25 to 2.00, account sizes from $5k to $50k. Compute size and $ risk at 1R. Print it and keep it near your screen.
