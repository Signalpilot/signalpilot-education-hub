Markets 101: Instruments & How Prices Move

Beginner20 minGeneral education

Educational content, not financial advice.

Learning Objectives
  • Differentiate spot, futures, options and CFDs at a high level.
  • Explain bid, ask, spread and slippage.
  • Recognize how sessions and liquidity affect volatility and fills.
  • Describe what splits and dividends do to charts.
Core Concepts
  • Instruments & Venues: Spot, futures, options, CFDs. Venues differ by asset class.
  • Quotes & Order Book: Best bid/ask, spread, mid, last trade.
  • Liquidity & Impact: Impact per unit rises in thin books.
  • Sessions: Stocks: pre/regular/after; FX overlapping; crypto 24/7.
  • Corporate Actions: Splits/dividends change adjusted charts.
Examples
  • Crossing the spread twice is a hidden round‑trip cost.
  • 2‑for‑1 split halves price, doubles shares—value unchanged.
Checklist
  • Confirm instrument specs before trading.
  • Check sessions/events; avoid thin pockets for size.
  • Prefer limit orders when spreads are wide.
Exercises
  1. List main sessions for one symbol you trade.
  2. Compute break‑even move for a given spread.
Key Terms
TermDefinition
Bid/AskHighest standing buy vs lowest standing sell.
SpreadAsk minus bid; transaction cost.
SlippageDifference between expected and actual fill.
LiquidityCapacity to trade size without large price impact.

Quick Quiz (self‑check)

Q1. Spread is:

  • A. Ask minus bid
  • B. Bid plus ask
  • C. High-Low
  • D. Open-Close

Answer: A

Q2. An option is:

  • A. Obligation to trade
  • B. Right but not obligation
  • C. Loan
  • D. Index

Answer: B

Q3. Adjusted charts change on ex‑date to:

  • A. Hide moves
  • B. Reflect dividend cash
  • C. Reduce taxes
  • D. Match futures

Answer: B
