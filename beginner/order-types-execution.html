Order Types & Execution 101

Beginner20 minGeneral education

Educational content, not financial advice.

Learning Objectives
  • Choose between market, limit, stop, stop‑limit.
  • Understand time‑in‑force and partial fills.
  • Avoid execution traps in fast markets.
Core Concepts
  • Market vs Limit: Fill certainty vs price control.
  • Stops: Stop‑market vs stop‑limit trade‑offs.
  • Brackets & OCO: Automate exits; avoid double fills.
  • Time‑in‑Force: DAY, GTC, IOC, FOK.
  • Queues: Price‑time priority; partial fills.
Examples
  • Limit inside the spread reduces cost but may delay fill.
  • Stop‑limit can miss in a gap.
Checklist
  • Prefer limit entries unless immediacy matters.
  • Attach brackets (stop+target).
  • Avoid obvious stop clusters.
Exercises
  1. Place a 2:1 bracket for an entry at 50 with 1R=1.
  2. Pick IOC vs FOK use‑cases.
Key Terms
TermDefinition
OCOOne‑cancels‑other.
GTCGood‑til‑canceled; broker rules vary.
Stop‑LimitTriggered limit; can miss fills in gaps.

Quick Quiz (self‑check)

Q1. Which prioritizes price?

  • A. Market
  • B. Limit
  • C. Stop‑market
  • D. MIT

Answer: B

Q2. IOC means:

  • A. All or nothing
  • B. Fill what can now; cancel rest
  • C. Persist forever
  • D. Open only

Answer: B

Q3. Risk of stop‑limit:

  • A. More fees
  • B. No execution in gap
  • C. Double fill
  • D. Latency

Answer: B
