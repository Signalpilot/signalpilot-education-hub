Risk Management 101: Position Sizing & R‑Multiples

Beginner25 minGeneral education

Educational content, not financial advice.

Learning Objectives
  • Define risk per trade and size positions.
  • Use R‑multiples to compare trades.
  • Understand drawdowns & recovery math.
Core Concepts
  • Risk‑Per‑Trade: % of equity at risk if stop hits.
  • Sizing: Size = (Acct × Risk$) / (Entry − Stop).
  • R‑Multiples: Normalize P/L to initial risk.
  • Drawdowns: Peak‑to‑trough; recovery needs larger %.
Examples
  • $10k account, 1% risk, $1 stop → 100 shares.
  • Many small losses beat one oversized one.
Checklist
  • Cap risk per trade/day/week.
  • Lower risk in high vol; avoid revenge.
Exercises
  1. Compute size for entry 200, stop 198, risk 0.8%.
  2. Sum R across a 5‑trade sample.
Key Terms
TermDefinition
R‑MultipleOutcome in units of initial risk.
Risk of RuinChance of blow‑up given risk & win rate.
DrawdownPeak‑to‑trough equity drop.

Q3. Sizing formula:

  • B. Acct / Entry
