Order Types & Execution 101

Execution is where good ideas go to die when misused. Learn how orders interact with the book so your intent becomes reality.

Core orders

  • Market: Fills now at best available price; pays spread; can slip.
  • Limit: Sets a max (buy) or min (sell) price; may not fill; sits in queue.
  • Stop: Triggers a market (or limit) order once price trades through your stop level.
  • Stop‑limit: Triggers a limit; prevents awful fills but can also miss the exit.

Queues & partials

Limits join a queue at their price level. You may get partial fills. “Time priority” matters; flashing and canceling can move you backward in line.

Use cases

  • Enter with limits at areas you’re happy to own.
  • Exit with stops where the thesis is invalidated; avoid stop‑limit for risk exits.
  • Use market only when liquidity is deep and urgency matters.
Practice: On your platform, place a tiny limit order far from price and watch the queue position. Then move it one tick—notice the priority reset.
