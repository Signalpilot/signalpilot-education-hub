Markets & Instruments 101

Markets are continuous auctions. Price prints when a buyer and seller agree. Your job is not to predict every tick but to choose where you participate and how you survive when wrong.

Key ideas

  • Instruments: Cash equities, futures, forex, crypto, ETFs/ETNs. Know the unit, tick size, and if leverage is embedded.
  • Microstructure: Bid/ask, spread, depth, partial fills. Illiquid symbols widen spreads and slip more.
  • Sessions: RTH vs extended; Asia/Europe/US for FX/crypto flow. Volatility and spreads change by session.
  • Corporate actions: Splits/dividends adjust historical prices; gaps are normal, not glitches.
  • Costs: Fees, borrow, funding, taxes. They turn edges paper‑thin.

Worked example

Buying 100 shares at 25.00 with a $0.02 spread means an instant $2.00 headwind. If you also slip $0.01, that’s another $1.00. Track these “invisible” costs; they often exceed your expected edge on lower timeframes.

Checklist

  • What is the tick size and minimum price increment?
  • What hours is this instrument most liquid?
  • What is one contract or lot worth per tick?
  • What are the explicit fees and likely slippage?

Common pitfalls

  • Trading illiquid tickers because the chart “looks clean.”
  • Ignoring contract multipliers (e.g., futures) and oversizing by accident.
  • Holding through major events without understanding gap risk.
Practice: Pick three instruments. Note tick size, tick value, typical spread, and best session. Compute the cost of a round trip at your usual size.
