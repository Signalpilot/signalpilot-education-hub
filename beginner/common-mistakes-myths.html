Common Mistakes & Trading Myths

Beginner16 minGeneral education

Educational content, not financial advice.

Learning Objectives
  • Spot destructive habits early.
  • Replace myths with evidence.
  • Write guardrails.
Core Concepts
  • Chasing: Late entries after impulse.
  • Over‑sizing: Risking too much per trade.
  • Indicator Worship: Signals ≠ certainty.
  • Myths: 'Tight stops always better', 'Low TF = more profits'.
Examples
  • Most edge loss is from sizing/discipline, not entries.
Checklist
  • Set a daily loss cap.
  • Pre‑define entry zones—avoid chasing.
Exercises
  1. List top 3 mistakes you’ve made.
  2. Write counter‑rules.
Key Terms
TermDefinition
ChasingEntering after the move is mostly over.

Quick Quiz (self‑check)

Q1. A persistent myth:

  • A. Edges are probabilistic
  • B. Tight stops are always better
  • C. Costs matter
  • D. Drawdowns happen

Answer: B
