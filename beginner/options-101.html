Options 101: Calls & Puts Basics

Beginner22 minGeneral education

Educational content, not financial advice.

Learning Objectives
  • Define calls and puts.
  • Explain intrinsic vs extrinsic value.
  • Avoid common options pitfalls.
Core Concepts
  • Rights & Obligations: Call = right to buy; put = right to sell.
  • Premium & Time: Extrinsic value decays with time.
  • Risks: Decay, assignment, volatility crush.
Examples
  • Buying options needs both directional and volatility edge.
Checklist
  • Start small; avoid short naked options as a beginner.
Exercises
  1. Identify intrinsic/extrinsic for a sample option.
  2. Explain why IV crush hurts buyers post‑event.
Key Terms
TermDefinition
Intrinsic ValueImmediate exercise value.
Extrinsic ValueTime and volatility value.

Quick Quiz (self‑check)

Q1. A call gives:

  • A. Obligation to sell
  • B. Right to buy
  • C. Dividend rights
  • D. Guaranteed profit

Answer: B
