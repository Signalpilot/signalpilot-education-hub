Options Market Microstructure: How MMs Move Markets

Market makers don't just provide liquidity—their delta hedging CREATES price movement.

When 100,000 SPY calls expire on Friday, market makers who sold those calls must unwind their hedge. That means selling millions of shares into the close—creating downward pressure REGARDLESS of fundamentals.

Part 1: Market Maker Delta Hedging

How it works:

  • Institution buys 10,000 calls from market maker
  • MM short 10,000 calls (exposed to upside)
  • MM buys underlying stock to hedge (delta × shares per contract)
  • As price moves, delta changes → MM must rehedge → creates price movement

Gamma Hedging Creates Volatility

High gamma environment: Delta changes rapidly → MMs rehedge frequently → amplifies moves

Example: SPY rallies $1 → call delta increases → MMs buy more stock → SPY rallies more → repeat

Part 2: Pin Risk & Max Pain

Pin Risk: Price gravitates toward strike with highest open interest on expiration day

Why: MMs have massive hedges at that strike. Their hedging activity pins price there.

Max Pain: Strike where most options expire worthless (maximum pain for option buyers)

Trading it:

  • Identify max pain strike (highest total OI)
  • On expiration Friday, expect price to drift toward max pain by 3 PM
  • Trade mean reversion if price far from max pain morning

Part 3: 0DTE Options Impact

0DTE (zero days to expiration): Options expiring same day

Impact: Extreme gamma → MMs hedging intraday → huge intraday volatility

Trading around 0DTE: Avoid breakout trades on 0DTE expiration days (likely to chop/pin)

Key Takeaways

  • MM delta hedging creates self-reinforcing price movement
  • Pin risk gravitates price toward max OI strike on expiration
  • 0DTE options = extreme intraday volatility from MM hedging
  • Trade mean reversion to max pain on expiration Fridays
