Fed Policy & Liquidity Cycles: The Macro Edge

The Federal Reserve controls the money supply. When they print, markets rally. When they tighten, markets fall. It's that simple.

Understanding Fed policy isn't optional for professional traders—it's the difference between trading with the macro tide or swimming against it.

Part 1: How Fed Policy Impacts Markets

Quantitative Easing (QE) = Bullish

What it is: Fed buys bonds, injects liquidity into system

Impact:

  • More money chasing same assets → prices rise
  • Lower yields → investors move to equities (TINA: There Is No Alternative)
  • Dollar weakens → commodities rally

Trading implication: Bull market bias. Buy dips. Long risk assets.

Quantitative Tightening (QT) = Bearish

What it is: Fed sells bonds, removes liquidity from system

Impact:

  • Less money available → prices fall
  • Higher yields → bonds compete with equities
  • Dollar strengthens → commodities fall

Trading implication: Bear market bias. Sell rallies. Hedge with puts.

Part 2: Fed Liquidity Cycle Indicator

Net Liquidity = Fed Balance Sheet - TGA (Treasury General Account) - Reverse Repo

Rising net liquidity: More money in system → stocks up

Falling net liquidity: Less money in system → stocks down

How to Track It

  • Fed Balance Sheet: Check FRED (St. Louis Fed data)
  • TGA Balance: Treasury Daily Statement
  • Reverse Repo: NY Fed website

Trading rule: If net liquidity rising for 3+ weeks → bias long. If falling 3+ weeks → bias defensive.

Part 3: FOMC Meeting Patterns

Before FOMC (2 days prior): Market drifts higher (institutions positioning)

During FOMC: Volatility spike, whipsaw moves

After FOMC (2-3 days): True direction emerges

Trading around FOMC:

  • Close risky positions 1 day before
  • Wait for volatility to settle (1-2 hours post-announcement)
  • Enter on direction confirmation with technical setup

Key Takeaways

  • QE = bullish, QT = bearish (Fed liquidity drives markets)
  • Net liquidity (balance sheet - TGA - RRP) = leading indicator
  • Rising liquidity for 3+ weeks = strong long bias
  • FOMC meetings create volatility — avoid or trade small
