Part 1: QE vs QT ‚Äî The Two Regimes

The Fed's Balance Sheet = Market Direction

The Federal Reserve's balance sheet is the single most important indicator for macro direction.

  • Quantitative Easing (QE): Fed buys bonds ‚Üí injects cash ‚Üí assets rally
  • Quantitative Tightening (QT): Fed sells bonds ‚Üí drains cash ‚Üí assets fall

Quantitative Easing ‚Äî The Money Printer

What the Fed Does:

  • Buys $80B-$120B in Treasuries and MBS per month
  • Balance sheet expands (from $4T in 2019 ‚Üí $9T by 2022)
  • Cash floods into the banking system

Market Impact:

  • Stocks rally: More money ‚Üí higher valuations (TINA: There Is No Alternative)
  • Bonds rally: Fed buying pushes yields down
  • Dollar weakens: Money supply increases ‚Üí dollar depreciates
  • Commodities rally: Weak dollar + inflation ‚Üí gold, oil surge
  • Crypto rallies: Risk-on sentiment + liquidity ‚Üí BTC, ETH moon

üí° Case Study: 2020 COVID QE

March 2020: Fed announces unlimited QE ($3 trillion in 3 months)

Result:

  • S&P 500: +70% from March 2020 low to Jan 2022
  • Nasdaq: +120% (tech stocks exploded)
  • Bitcoin: +1,500% (from $5K to $69K)

Lesson: QE = buy everything. The Fed prints ‚Üí you profit.

Trading Rules During QE:

  1. Buy dips aggressively ‚Äî every pullback is a gift
  2. Hold winners longer ‚Äî liquidity keeps pushing prices up
  3. Avoid shorts ‚Äî fighting the Fed = financial suicide
  4. Leverage works ‚Äî margin debt is cheap, volatility is low

Quantitative Tightening ‚Äî The Liquidity Drain

What the Fed Does:

  • Stops buying bonds (lets them mature and roll off)
  • Balance sheet shrinks ($95B/month runoff in 2022)
  • Cash is removed from the system

Market Impact:

  • Stocks fall: Less money ‚Üí lower valuations (P/E compression)
  • Bonds fall: Yields rise as Fed stops buying
  • Dollar strengthens: Money supply shrinks ‚Üí dollar appreciates
  • Commodities fall: Strong dollar + recession fears
  • Crypto crashes: Risk-off + liquidity drain ‚Üí BTC, ETH collapse

Result:

Trading Rules During QT:

  1. Sell rallies ‚Äî every bounce is a short opportunity

  4. Avoid leverage ‚Äî drawdowns are deeper and longer
Part 2: Net Liquidity ‚Äî The Leading Indicator

The Real Liquidity Formula

The Fed's balance sheet alone doesn't tell the full story. You need to account for cash sitting idle in government accounts that doesn't flow into markets.

üìä Net Liquidity Formula

Net Liquidity = Fed Balance Sheet - TGA - Reverse Repo

  • Fed Balance Sheet: Total assets the Fed owns (Treasury bonds, MBS)
  • TGA (Treasury General Account): The government's checking account at the Fed
  • Reverse Repo (RRP): Cash parked overnight at the Fed by banks

Why subtract TGA and RRP? Because that cash is not in the market‚Äîit's locked up at the Fed, unavailable to buy stocks, bonds, or crypto.

How to Track Net Liquidity

Step 1: Get Fed Balance Sheet Data

Source: FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data)

Ticker: WALCL

Current Value (as of Feb 2024): ~$7.7 trillion

Update Frequency: Weekly (every Wednesday)

Step 2: Get TGA (Treasury General Account)

Source: FRED ‚Äî TGA Balance

Ticker: WTREGEN

Current Value: ~$750 billion

What it means: Cash sitting at the Fed that the Treasury hasn't spent yet. When TGA falls, cash flows into the economy (bullish). When TGA rises, cash is drained from markets (bearish).

Step 3: Get Reverse Repo (RRP)

Source: FRED ‚Äî Overnight Reverse Repo

Ticker: RRPONTSYD

Current Value: ~$500 billion (down from $2.5T in 2023)

What it means: Money market funds park cash at the Fed overnight instead of investing in markets. When RRP falls, cash flows back into stocks/bonds (bullish). When RRP rises, cash exits markets (bearish).

Step 4: Calculate Net Liquidity

Formula: Net Liquidity = Fed Balance Sheet - TGA - RRP

Example Calculation (Feb 2024):

  • Fed Balance Sheet: $7.7T
  • TGA: $750B
  • RRP: $500B
  • Net Liquidity = $7.7T - $0.75T - $0.5T = $6.45T

Trading Signal: Track the change in net liquidity week-over-week.

  • Net liquidity rising for 3+ weeks: Strong bullish signal ‚Üí bias long
  • Net liquidity falling for 3+ weeks: Strong bearish signal ‚Üí bias defensive

üí° Pro Tip: Liquidity Leads Price by 2-4 Weeks

Net liquidity is a leading indicator‚Äîit typically moves before stock prices respond.

Example: In October 2023, net liquidity started rising (RRP fell from $1.3T ‚Üí $500B). The S&P 500 rallied +20% over the next 4 months.

Why it works: Liquidity creates demand. More cash in the system ‚Üí institutions deploy capital ‚Üí prices rise.

The Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) meets 8 times per year to decide interest rates and policy. These meetings create predictable volatility patterns that professional traders exploit.

The FOMC Trading Pattern

Before FOMC (2 Days Prior): Markets drift higher (pre-FOMC drift is real)

  • Volatility is compressed (VIX falls)
  • Institutions position ahead of expected news
  • Market makers hedge gamma exposure by buying stock

Stats: From 2011-2023, the S&P 500 gained an average of +0.5% in the 24 hours before FOMC announcements (68% win rate).

During FOMC (Day-Of): The Chaos Window

  • 2:00 PM ET: FOMC statement released ‚Üí algorithmic whipsaw
  • 2:30 PM ET: Jerome Powell press conference ‚Üí wild volatility
  • HFTs dominate the first 5 minutes (you can't compete)
  • Spreads widen to 5-10x normal (slippage kills you)

Real Example (June 2023): Fed paused rate hikes. S&P surged +1.2% initially, then Powell said "more hikes likely ahead" ‚Üí S&P reversed to -0.8%. First reaction was wrong.

After FOMC (2-3 Days): The True Move

  • Markets digest the Fed's message over 2-3 days
  • True direction emerges (either sustained rally or selloff)
  • Volatility normalizes by day 3

üí° Pro Strategy: The 2-Hour Rule

Rule: Don't trade until 2 hours after Powell's press conference ends (~4:30 PM ET).

Why: By then, institutions have positioned, headlines are priced in, and the real direction is clear.

Part 4: The Fed Put

Why the Fed Rescues Markets

The "Fed Put" is the belief that the Federal Reserve will step in to prevent major market crashes by cutting rates or restarting QE.

Why the Fed Put exists:

  • Wealth effect: Falling stocks ‚Üí consumers spend less ‚Üí economy contracts
  • Financial stability: Crashes cause pension funds, banks, and insurance companies to fail
  • Political pressure: Crashes hurt voters ‚Üí politicians demand action

Historical Fed Puts

  • 1987 Black Monday: S&P -20% in 1 day ‚Üí Fed cuts rates immediately
  • 1998 LTCM Crisis: Hedge fund collapse ‚Üí Fed orchestrates bailout
  • 2008 Financial Crisis: S&P -50% ‚Üí Fed launches QE1 ($1.7T)
  • 2018 December Crash: S&P -20% ‚Üí Fed pivots from hikes to cuts
  • 2020 COVID Crash: S&P -35% ‚Üí Fed unlimited QE ($3T in 3 months)

Pattern: Every major crash is met with Fed intervention.

Where is the Fed Put Strike Price?

The Fed Put "strike price" = the level at which the Fed will intervene.

This isn't an exact number‚Äîit's a pain threshold:

  • -10% pullback: Fed watches, no action
  • -15% correction: Fed starts talking about "monitoring conditions"
  • -20% bear market: Fed pivots (cuts rates or restarts QE)

üí° Trading the Fed Put

Strategy: When the S&P 500 falls -15% to -20%, start buying.

Why: The Fed Put becomes more likely with every down day. Once triggered, markets rally hard.
