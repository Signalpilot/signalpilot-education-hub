Cross-Market Correlation: Reading The Full Picture

Stocks don't trade in a vacuum. Bonds, dollar, commodities—everything is connected.

When the 10-year yield spikes 20 basis points in a day, SPX will react. When the dollar breaks support, gold will rally. Professional traders monitor these relationships constantly because they predict equity moves BEFORE they happen.

Part 1: The Big Four Correlations

1. Stocks vs Bonds (SPY vs TLT)

Normal correlation: Inverse (stocks up → bonds down)

Why: Risk-on = investors sell bonds, buy stocks; Risk-off = opposite

Market State Stocks (SPY) Bonds (TLT) Interpretation
Risk-on ↑ ↓ Normal (money rotating stocks → bonds)
Risk-off ↓ ↑ Normal (flight to safety)
Both falling ↓ ↓ Danger: Forced liquidation (2008, March 2020)
Both rising ↑ ↑ Fed easing (QE environment)

⚠️ Critical Signal: When stocks AND bonds fall together = systemic risk (margin calls, forced selling). Exit all risk immediately.

2. Dollar vs Stocks (DXY vs SPX)

Normal correlation: Inverse (dollar up → stocks down)

Why: Strong dollar = tighter financial conditions, pressure on multinationals (70% of SPX revenue is international)

Why Dollar Strength Hurts Stocks

Mechanism #1: Tighter financial conditions

  • Strong dollar = global dollar liquidity contracts
  • International borrowers struggle to repay dollar-denominated debt
  • Risk of EM currency crises (2013 taper tantrum, 2015 China devaluation)

Mechanism #2: Earnings pressure

  • SPX companies earn 30% of revenue internationally
  • Strong dollar = foreign revenue worth less when converted back to USD
  • Example: Apple earns €100M in Europe. If EUR/USD falls 10%, that €100M is now worth $10M less

Trading rule: DXY breaking multi-month resistance = bearish for SPX (reduce long exposure)

3. Yields vs Stocks (10Y Yield vs SPX)

Relationship is non-linear:

  • Yields 0-2%: Rising yields = bullish (recovery from recession)
  • Yields 2-4%: Neutral (goldilocks)
  • Yields > 4%: Rising yields = bearish (bonds compete with stocks)

Rate of change matters: +10bps/day = manageable, +30bps/day = panic

4. Commodities vs Dollar (Gold, Oil vs DXY)

Gold vs Dollar: Strong inverse correlation (dollar down → gold up)

Oil vs Dollar: Moderate inverse correlation (oil priced in dollars)

Why: Commodities priced in USD → weaker dollar = commodity prices rise in dollar terms

Part 2: Intermarket Divergences (Leading Indicators)

What Is a Divergence?

Divergence: When correlated assets break their normal relationship

Signal: One asset is telling you something the other hasn't priced in yet

Key Divergences to Watch

Divergence #1: Stocks Up, Bonds Not Falling

Setup: SPX making new highs, but TLT not making new lows

Interpretation: Bond market skeptical of equity rally (maybe rally is low-quality, narrow breadth)

Trade: Bearish signal for stocks. Either bonds are wrong (rare) or stocks will follow bonds lower (common)

Signal Pilot tool: Use Janus Atlas to check if SPX rally has breadth or just a few mega-caps

Divergence #2: High-Yield Spreads Widening, Stocks Rallying

Setup: SPX making new highs, but HYG (junk bonds) falling / spreads widening

Interpretation: Credit market smells trouble (defaults coming, recession risk)

Why it leads: Credit investors are smarter than equity investors (bondholders get paid first in bankruptcy → more careful)

Trade: Major bearish divergence. Reduce equity exposure, buy puts.

Divergence #3: Copper Falling, Stocks Rallying

Setup: SPX making new highs, copper (Dr. Copper) falling

Interpretation: Industrial demand weakening (global growth slowing)

Why copper leads: Copper has a PhD in economics (used in construction, manufacturing, EVs)

Trade: Bearish for cyclical stocks (XLI, materials, energy). Defensive rotation coming.

Part 3: Correlation Regimes

High Correlation = Danger

When: Market stress, forced liquidations, systematic strategies unwinding

What happens: All assets move together (diversification fails)

Example: March 2020 — stocks, bonds, gold, crypto ALL fell together (only USD rallied)

Low Correlation = Opportunity

When: Normal markets, stock-picking environment

What happens: Individual assets trade on fundamentals (alpha opportunity)

Strategy: High correlation → reduce risk, wait for reset. Low correlation → increase position sizing, hunt alpha.

Part 4: Using Signal Pilot for Intermarket Analysis

Janus Atlas: Multi-Asset View

How to use: Overlay SPY, TLT, DXY, GLD on same chart

What to look for: Divergences (stocks making new high, dollar not making new low)

Pentarch Pilot Line: Cross-Asset Order Flow

How to use: Check if institutional order flow in bonds aligns with stocks

Signal: If bond order flow turns defensive while stocks rally → divergence warning

Plutus Flow: Unusual Cross-Asset Options Activity

Example: Massive TLT call buying + SPY put buying = institutions hedging for risk-off event

Quiz: Test Your Understanding

Q1: SPX rallies 2%, but TLT also rallies 1%. What does this signal?

Show Answer

Answer: Bullish for risk assets (Fed easing environment). Both stocks and bonds rising = liquidity injection (QE). This is a BUY signal for equities as long as it persists.

Q2: SPX making new highs, but HYG (high-yield bonds) falling. What action do you take?

Show Answer

Answer: Bearish divergence. Credit market pricing in recession risk while equity investors complacent. Reduce equity exposure, buy puts, or hedge. Credit leads equities—when they diverge, credit is usually right.

Q3: DXY (dollar index) breaks 6-month resistance. How do you adjust your SPX position?

Show Answer

Answer: Bearish for SPX. Strong dollar = tighter financial conditions + multinational earnings pressure. Reduce long exposure or add hedges. If DXY continues higher, SPX will face headwinds.

Practical Checklist

Daily Intermarket Review:

  • Check SPY vs TLT: Are they moving inversely (normal) or together (extreme)?
  • Check DXY: Breaking resistance (bearish SPX) or support (bullish SPX)?
  • Check 10Y yield rate of change: >20bps in single day = volatility incoming
  • Check HYG vs SPY: Credit diverging from equities?
  • Check copper: Falling while stocks rally = bearish divergence
  • Use Janus Atlas to overlay multi-asset charts for visual divergence detection
  • Check Plutus Flow for unusual TLT/GLD/DXY options activity (hedging signals)

Key Takeaways

  • Stocks & bonds falling together = systemic risk (2008, March 2020)
  • Strong dollar = bearish for stocks (tighter conditions + earnings pressure)
  • Credit divergences (HYG falling, SPX rallying) = major warning
  • Copper falling, stocks rallying = global growth slowing
  • High correlation regimes = reduce risk, diversification fails
