Stocks don't trade in a vacuum. When the 10-year yield spikes 30 basis points, SPX will react. When the dollar breaks support, gold will rally. Professional traders monitor these relationships constantly‚Äîbecause intermarket signals predict equity moves BEFORE they happen.

Retail traders stare at SPY charts all day, wondering why it suddenly dropped 2%. Meanwhile, the bond market was screaming "risk-off" for three hours. High-yield credit spreads were widening. The dollar was spiking. Copper was tanking. All the warnings were there‚Äîjust not on the SPY chart.

Professional traders watch bonds, dollar, commodities, credit spreads, and volatility alongside equities. These markets are interconnected through capital flows, correlation arbitrage, and systematic strategies. When correlations break‚Äîwhen stocks rally but bonds refuse to sell off‚Äîthat's a divergence signal worth trading.

üö® Real Talk

Intermarket analysis is how institutions think. When a hedge fund allocates capital, they're not just asking "are stocks cheap?" They're asking: "How do stocks price relative to bonds at current yields? Is the dollar's trajectory supportive? Are credit spreads confirming the equity rally?" This lesson teaches you to think like an institutional allocator‚Äîanalyzing markets in relation to each other, not in isolation.

Part 1: The Big Four Correlations

1. Stocks vs Bonds (SPY vs TLT)

Normal Relationship: Inverse correlation. Stocks up ‚Üí bonds down (yields up). Stocks down ‚Üí bonds up (yields down).

Why This Happens:

  • Risk-on environment: Investors sell "safe" bonds, buy higher-returning stocks. Bond prices fall (yields rise), stock prices rise.
  • Risk-off environment: Investors sell stocks, buy "safe" bonds. Bond prices rise (yields fall), stock prices fall.
  • Portfolio rebalancing: 60/40 funds constantly rebalance between stocks and bonds, creating negative correlation.
Market State Stocks (SPY) Bonds (TLT) 10Y Yield Interpretation
Risk-On ‚Üë Rising ‚Üì Falling ‚Üë Rising Normal rotation: money leaving bonds for stocks
Risk-Off ‚Üì Falling ‚Üë Rising ‚Üì Falling Normal flight to safety: selling stocks, buying bonds
Both Falling ‚Üì Falling ‚Üì Falling ‚Üë Yields spiking DANGER: Forced liquidation (margin calls, deleveraging)
Both Rising ‚Üë Rising ‚Üë Rising ‚Üì Yields falling Fed easing / QE environment (liquidity injection)

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Signal: Both Stocks AND Bonds Falling

When SPY AND TLT both fall on the same day, it signals systemic stress. Investors aren't rotating from stocks to bonds (normal risk-off). They're selling EVERYTHING to raise cash or meet margin calls.

Historical examples:

  • October 2008 (Lehman collapse): SPY -10%, TLT -5% same week
  • March 2020 (COVID crash): SPY -12%, TLT -3% same day (March 12)
  • September 2022 (UK pension crisis): Global bond/stock selloff

Trading rule: When this happens, exit ALL risk positions immediately. Only USD cash and short-term T-bills are safe. Wait for correlations to normalize before re-entering.

2. Dollar vs Stocks (DXY vs SPX)

Normal Relationship: Inverse correlation (moderate strength). Dollar up ‚Üí stocks down. Dollar down ‚Üí stocks up.

Why Dollar Strength Hurts Stocks:

Mechanism #1: Tighter Global Financial Conditions

  • Strong USD = global dollar liquidity contracts (dollar is world's reserve currency)
  • International borrowers struggle to service USD-denominated debt (emerging markets, corporates)
  • Risk of EM currency crises ‚Üí contagion to developed markets
  • Historical examples: 2013 taper tantrum, 2015 China devaluation, 2022 UK gilt crisis

Mechanism #2: Multinational Earnings Pressure

  • S&P 500 companies generate ~30% of revenue internationally
  • Strong USD = foreign revenue worth less when converted back to dollars
  • Example: Apple earns ‚Ç¨10B in Europe. EUR/USD at 1.10 = $11B revenue. EUR/USD falls to 1.00 = $10B revenue (10% haircut despite same European sales).
  • Mega-caps (AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL) have highest international exposure ‚Üí most sensitive to USD strength

Mechanism #3: Risk Asset Repricing

  • Strong USD often coincides with Fed tightening (raising rates)
  • Higher rates = lower present value of future earnings (discount rate effect)
  • Growth stocks (long-duration assets) most affected
DXY Move Typical SPX Reaction Duration Trading Implication
DXY breaks resistance (+2-3%) SPX pullback -2-5% 2-4 weeks Reduce long exposure, hedge with puts
DXY breaks support (-2-3%) SPX rally +3-7% 2-4 weeks Increase long exposure, buy dips
DXY spike >5% in month SPX correction -10-15% 1-3 months Go defensive, exit risk, buy TLT

3. Yields vs Stocks (10Y Treasury Yield vs SPX)

Relationship is Non-Linear (depends on level AND rate of change):

10Y Yield Level Rising Yields Impact on SPX Rationale
< 2% Bullish (rising yields good) Yield rise = economic recovery from recession. Growth beats rate headwind.
2-4% (Goldilocks) Neutral Moderate yields support growth without excessive drag. SPX can rally.
> 4% Bearish (rising yields bad) Bonds start competing with stocks. Risk-free 4-5% looks attractive vs equity risk.
> 5% Very Bearish Yield > long-term equity returns. Massive capital rotation from stocks ‚Üí bonds.

Rate of Change Matters More Than Level:

  • +5-10 bps/day: Manageable. Market digests gradually.
  • +15-20 bps/day: Elevated stress. Volatility increases.
  • +30+ bps/day: Panic. Forced deleveraging begins. SPX typically down 1-3% same day.

Real Example: October 2023 Yield Spike

Sept 30: 10Y yield at 4.57%
Oct 19: 10Y yield at 5.02% (+45 bps in 3 weeks)
SPX reaction: -5.2% over same period

Signal: Yield spike >4.5% + rapid rate of change
Trade: Sell SPX rallies, buy TLT (bonds oversold)
Outcome: Nov-Dec 2023 = bonds rallied 8%, SPX recovered
(Fed pivot expectations shifted, yields reversed)

4. Commodities vs Dollar (Gold, Oil, Copper vs DXY)

Gold vs Dollar: Strong inverse correlation (-0.7 to -0.9)

  • Gold priced in USD ‚Üí weak dollar = gold rises in dollar terms
  • Gold is anti-dollar hedge (central banks, international investors use gold to diversify from USD)
  • Trading rule: DXY breaks support (-3%) = buy GLD. DXY breaks resistance (+3%) = sell GLD.

Oil vs Dollar: Moderate inverse correlation (-0.4 to -0.6)

  • Oil priced globally in USD ‚Üí weak dollar = oil rises (international buyers get more purchasing power)
  • Correlation weaker than gold (oil driven more by supply/demand fundamentals)
  • Exception: Supply shocks override dollar correlation (2022 Russia/Ukraine ‚Üí oil spiked despite strong dollar)

Copper vs Stocks: Positive correlation (+0.6 to +0.8) with equities

  • Copper = "Dr. Copper" (has PhD in economics) ‚Üí industrial demand proxy
  • Used in construction, manufacturing, EVs, infrastructure
  • When copper falls while stocks rally = bearish divergence (global growth slowing, equity rally will fail)

Part 2: Intermarket Divergences‚ÄîLeading Indicators

What Is an Intermarket Divergence?

Divergence: When historically correlated assets break their normal relationship.

Why Divergences Matter: One asset is telling you something the other hasn't priced in yet. The divergence resolves by either (1) the lagging asset catching up, or (2) the leading asset reversing. Historically, the lagging asset catches up 70-80% of the time.

Divergence #1: Stocks Up, Bonds Refusing to Fall

Setup:

  • SPX making new 52-week highs
  • TLT NOT making new 52-week lows (or even rising slightly)
  • Yields NOT rising despite stock rally

Interpretation:

Bond market is skeptical of equity rally. Possible reasons:

  • Rally is narrow (only a few mega-cap stocks, no breadth)
  • Bond investors expect recession (buying bonds for safety despite equity strength)
  • Liquidity concerns (institutions parking cash in bonds, not chasing stocks)

Trading Signal: Bearish for stocks. When bonds and stocks disagree, bonds are usually right (bond market is "smarter"‚Äîmore institutional, less retail).

Trade Setup:

  • Entry: Short SPX when divergence lasts 2+ weeks
  • Confirmation: Use Signal Pilot Janus Atlas to check if SPX rally has breadth (all sectors up?) or just tech (AAPL, MSFT, NVDA)
  • Stop: If TLT starts falling (bonds confirm equity rally)
  • Target: SPX pullback 3-5% to close divergence

Divergence #2: High-Yield Credit Spreads Widening, Stocks Rallying

Setup:

  • SPX making new highs
  • HYG (high-yield bond ETF) falling
  • Credit spreads (HY bonds vs Treasuries) widening

What It Means:

Credit market is pricing in recession / default risk. Credit investors (bond investors) smell trouble‚Äîexpecting companies to struggle paying debt.

Why Credit Leads Equities:

  • Bondholders get paid before shareholders in bankruptcy ‚Üí more conservative, forward-looking
  • Credit analysts study company balance sheets, leverage, cash flow (more fundamental analysis than equity day traders)
  • Historical pattern: Credit sells off 2-6 weeks BEFORE major equity corrections

Real Example: January 2022

Dec 2021: SPX at 4800 (new all-time highs)
HYG: Peaked Dec 6, down -2% by Jan 1

Divergence Signal: Credit selling off despite equity ATH

Jan 2022: SPX drops -8.7% (Jan 3-24)
Credit was right. Equity investors caught offside.

Trade: If you shorted SPX when HYG broke support
(Dec 20), you captured 8% downside over next month.

Trading Rule:

  • Monitor HYG/SPY ratio daily
  • If HYG/SPY makes new 3-month low while SPX makes new 3-month high = major bearish divergence
  • Enter puts on SPX or reduce long exposure immediately

Divergence #3: Copper Falling, Stocks Rallying ("Dr. Copper" Divergence)

Setup:

  • SPX making new highs (or trending up)
  • Copper futures (HG) or copper ETF (COPX) falling -5-10%

Interpretation:

Industrial demand is weakening. Global growth is slowing. Copper is used in construction, manufacturing, EVs, infrastructure‚Äîif demand is falling, it signals economic slowdown ahead.

Trading Implications:

  • Bearish for cyclical stocks: Materials (XLB), Industrials (XLI), Energy (XLE)
  • Defensive rotation coming: Expect rotation into Utilities (XLU), Consumer Staples (XLP), Healthcare (XLV)
  • SPX correction likely: If copper keeps falling, SPX will eventually follow (cyclicals drag index lower)

Real Example: Summer 2022

June 2022: SPX rebounds from May lows (+8% rally)
Copper: Continues falling -15% from April highs

Divergence: Stocks rallying, copper (global growth proxy) tanking

July-Sept 2022: SPX drops -12% (new lows in October)
Copper was right. Global recession fears materialized.

Trade: Short cyclicals (XLI, XLB) when copper broke support
in June. Outperformed shorting SPX directly.

Divergence #4: VIX Staying Elevated Despite Stock Rally

Setup:

  • SPX rallying +3-5%
  • VIX refuses to fall (stays above 20-25)

Interpretation:

Options market is NOT buying the rally. Put buyers still hedging aggressively despite higher stock prices. This signals:

  • Rally is short-covering (forced buying), not conviction buying
  • Institutions using rally to hedge (buying puts while stocks up = cheap insurance)
  • Reversal likely once short-covering exhausts

Trading Rule:

  • SPX +5% in week, VIX still >22 = fade the rally (sell strength)
  • Wait for VIX <18 before trusting bullish continuation

Part 3: Correlation Regimes‚ÄîWhen Diversification Fails

High Correlation = Systemic Risk

Definition: When all asset classes move together (correlation ‚Üí +1 or -1).

When It Happens:

  • Market crashes / forced liquidations (March 2020, October 2008)
  • Systematic strategies unwinding (volatility targeting funds, risk parity funds)
  • Margin calls / deleveraging events

What Happens:

  • Stocks fall, bonds fall, gold falls, crypto falls, commodities fall
  • Only USD cash rallies (flight to ultimate liquidity)
  • Diversification FAILS (60/40 portfolios get crushed)

Example: March 2020 (COVID Crash)

March 9-12, 2020:
- SPY: -12%
- TLT (bonds): -3%
- GLD (gold): -4%
- BTC (crypto): -25%
- Oil: -30%

Correlation to SPY: Everything +0.9 correlation (all falling together)

Only winner: USD (DXY +4%)

Cause: Forced liquidation. Funds selling everything to raise cash.

Trading Rule for High Correlation Regimes:

  • Signal: SPY + TLT both down >1% same day
  • Action: Exit ALL risk positions. Go to cash. Wait for correlations to normalize (usually 1-4 weeks).
  • Re-entry: When SPY up, TLT down (normal inverse correlation returns) = safe to re-enter risk.

Low Correlation = Alpha Opportunity

Definition: When assets trade on individual fundamentals (correlation ‚Üí 0).

When It Happens:

  • Normal market environments (60-70% of time)
  • Low volatility regimes (VIX <18)
  • Stock-picker markets (2017, 2019, H2 2023)

What Happens:

  • Individual stocks trade on earnings, fundamentals, sector trends
  • Some stocks up +10%, others down -10% (dispersion high)
  • Active managers can generate alpha (outperform index)

Trading Strategy:

  • Low correlation = increase position sizing: Stock-picking edge is highest. Go aggressive on high-conviction trades.
  • High correlation = reduce position sizing: Everything moving together = no edge. Go defensive, wait for regime shift.

Part 4: Real Trade Examples Using Intermarket Analysis

Trade #1: Shorting SPX on Dollar Breakout (Sept 2022)

Setup (Sept 5, 2022):

  • DXY breaks 110 (multi-decade resistance)
  • 10Y yield breaks 3.5% (rising rapidly, +15 bps/week)
  • SPX at 3950 (attempting rally from August lows)

Intermarket Signals:

  • Dollar strength: DXY +5% in month (tightening financial conditions)
  • Yield spike: 10Y from 2.5% ‚Üí 3.5% in 6 weeks (repricing equity valuations lower)
  • Credit weakness: HYG making new lows (not confirming SPX rally attempt)

Trade:

  • Entry: Short SPX at 3950 (Sept 6)
  • Stop: 4050 (above recent high)
  • Target 1: 3750 (previous low)
  • Target 2: 3600 (measured move from DXY breakout)

Outcome:

Sept 12: SPX hits 3900 (Target 1 approaches)
Sept 30: SPX hits 3585 (Target 2 hit)

Trade Results:
- Entry: 3950
- Exit: 3600 (avg of partial exits)
- Profit: 350 points (8.9% in 24 days)

Why it worked: Dollar and yields screamed "sell risk."
Intermarket signals led SPX decline by days.

Trade #2: Buying Stocks on Credit Spread Compression (Nov 2023)

Setup (Nov 1, 2023):

  • SPX at 4200 (down -10% from July highs)
  • HYG (high-yield bonds) bottoming, spreads compressing
  • DXY breaking support (dollar weakness = bullish for equities)
  • 10Y yield peaked at 5%, now falling to 4.5%

Intermarket Signals:

  • Credit improving: HYG +2% while SPX flat (credit leading equities higher)
  • Dollar weakness: DXY -3% in 2 weeks (bullish for stocks)
  • Yield relief: 10Y falling -50 bps (discount rate tailwind for equities)

Trade:

  • Entry: Long SPX at 4225 (Nov 3)
  • Stop: 4100 (below recent low)
  • Target: 4500 (previous resistance)

Outcome:

Nov 30: SPX at 4550 (Target hit)
Dec 31: SPX at 4770 (extended rally)

Trade Results:
- Entry: 4225
- Exit: 4550 (target)
- Profit: 325 points (7.7% in 27 days)

Why it worked: Credit, dollar, yields ALL aligned bullish.
Intermarket signals confirmed bottom before SPX confirmed.

Part 5: Building Your Daily Intermarket Checklist

Daily Monitoring Routine (10-15 minutes)

Step 1: Check Core Correlations

  • SPY vs TLT: Moving inversely (normal) or together (stress)?
  • DXY: Breaking resistance (bearish SPX) or support (bullish SPX)?
  • 10Y yield: Rate of change >20bps in week? (Volatility warning)

Step 2: Check Credit Market

  • HYG vs SPY: Aligned or diverging?
  • HYG/SPY ratio: Making new 3-month low = bearish divergence

Step 3: Check Commodities

  • Copper (HG or COPX): Falling while stocks rally = bearish
  • Gold: Following dollar inverse correlation?
  • Oil: Supply shock overriding fundamentals?

Step 4: Check Volatility

  • VIX: Elevated despite stock rally = fade rally
  • CBOE Skew: >150 = extreme fear (potential reversal)

Step 5: Use Signal Pilot Tools

  • Janus Atlas: Overlay SPY, TLT, DXY, GLD on one chart ‚Üí visual divergence detection
  • Pentarch Pilot Line: Check institutional order flow in bonds vs stocks
  • Plutus Flow: Unusual options activity in TLT, GLD, DXY (hedging signals)

Key Takeaways

  • Stocks & bonds falling together = systemic risk‚Äîimmediate exit signal (2008, March 2020 pattern)
  • Dollar strength = bearish for stocks‚ÄîDXY breaking resistance is 2-4 week leading indicator for SPX weakness
  • Credit divergences are most reliable‚Äîwhen HYG falls while SPX rallies, credit is right 80% of time
  • Copper = global growth proxy‚Äî"Dr. Copper" falling + stocks rallying = bearish divergence
  • Yield rate of change matters‚Äî10Y spiking >30 bps/week triggers SPX volatility
  • High correlation = reduce risk‚Äîwhen all assets move together, diversification fails, go to cash

  5. Build Your Checklist: Create daily spreadsheet tracking: SPY, TLT, DXY, HYG, Copper, VIX. Note when divergences appear. Track how long until divergence resolves.
