Market Maker Algorithms: The Other Side of Your Trade

Every market order you place is filled by a market maker. And they profit from EVERY transaction.

Market makers aren't charities. They provide liquidity for profit. Understanding HOW they profit—and HOW they manipulate to maximize that profit—is critical to avoiding their traps.

Part 1: Spread Capture

Core strategy: Buy at bid, sell at ask, pocket the spread

Example:

  • Bid: $100.00, Ask: $100.05
  • MM buys from seller at $100.00
  • MM sells to buyer at $100.05
  • Profit: $0.05 per share (no directional risk)

Scale: Do this 10,000 times per day = $500,000 profit

Part 2: Adverse Selection

Problem: Smart traders only hit MMs when they have edge

Example of adverse selection:

  • Informed trader sees news, immediately buys at ask
  • MM fills at $100.05
  • News hits public, price jumps to $101.00
  • MM now holding inventory bought at $100.00, marked at $101 (loss avoided, but sold too early)

MM Defense: Widen spreads during volatility (higher compensation for adverse selection risk)

Part 3: Inventory Management

Goal: Stay delta-neutral (no directional exposure)

Tactics:

  • Skew quotes: If long inventory, lower bid to discourage more buying
  • Quote stuffing: Flood order book to slow down adverse selection
  • Layering: Place fake orders to manipulate price toward desired level

Part 4: How MMs Manipulate

Tactic #1: Spoofing

What: Place large fake orders to create false supply/demand, then cancel before fill

Example: Show 50,000 sell order at $100.10 → retail thinks resistance → price drops → MM buys cheaper → cancel fake order

Legality: Illegal, but hard to prove intent. Still happens.

Tactic #2: Quote Stuffing

What: Rapidly place and cancel orders to slow down competitor algos

Impact: Retail sees messy order book, hesitates, MMs get better fills

Tactic #3: Latency Arbitrage

What: MMs see your order BEFORE it hits exchange (PFOF), front-run it

Example:

  • You send market buy order for 1,000 shares
  • Broker sends to Citadel (PFOF)
  • Citadel buys 1,000 shares at $100.00
  • Citadel sells to you at $100.02
  • Citadel profit: $20 (risk-free, using latency advantage)

Key Takeaways

  • MMs profit from spread capture (buy bid, sell ask, thousands of times per day)
  • Adverse selection = informed traders hitting MMs when they have edge
  • Spoofing, quote stuffing, layering = MM manipulation tactics
  • PFOF enables latency arbitrage (Citadel sees your order first, front-runs)
  • Defense: Use limit orders, trade liquid hours, avoid market orders during news
