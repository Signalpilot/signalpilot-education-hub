Macro Regime Framework: Trading the Business Cycle

The strategies that work in expansion will bankrupt you in contraction. Know the regime, or get destroyed.

Markets cycle through four distinct macro regimes: Growth/Low Inflation (goldilocks), Growth/High Inflation (boom), Recession/Low Inflation (deflationary bust), Recession/High Inflation (stagflation). Each regime favors different assets and strategies. Professional traders identify the regime first, THEN decide what to trade.

Part 1: The Four Macro Regimes

Regime Growth Inflation Best Assets Worst Assets
Goldilocks + Low Stocks, Tech Gold, Commodities
Inflationary Boom + High Commodities, Real Estate Bonds
Stagflation - High Gold, Cash Stocks, Bonds
Deflationary Bust - Low/Negative Bonds, USD Commodities, Stocks

Regime #1: Goldilocks (Growth + Low Inflation)

Characteristics: GDP growing 2-3%, inflation 1-2%, Fed neutral

Why stocks rally: Earnings grow without inflation pressure ‚Üí multiple expansion

Best trades: Long tech, growth stocks, risk assets

Example period: 2010-2019 (longest bull market in history)

Regime #2: Inflationary Boom (Growth + High Inflation)

Characteristics: GDP >3%, inflation >3%, Fed tightening

Why commodities rally: Strong demand + supply constraints = price spikes

Best trades: Long commodities (oil, copper), short duration bonds

Example period: 2021-2022 (post-COVID reopening, inflation surge)

Regime #3: Stagflation (No Growth + High Inflation)

Characteristics: GDP <1%, inflation >4%, Fed stuck (can't ease or tighten)

Why gold rallies: Currency debasement + no growth = safe haven demand

Best trades: Long gold, short equities, cash heavy

Example period: 1970s (oil shocks, double-digit inflation, stagnant growth)

Regime #4: Deflationary Bust (Recession + Low Inflation)

Characteristics: GDP negative, inflation <1%, Fed easing aggressively

Why bonds rally: Flight to safety + falling yields = capital appreciation

Best trades: Long treasuries (TLT), USD, defensive stocks

Example period: 2008-2009 (financial crisis), March 2020 (COVID crash)

Part 2: How to Identify the Current Regime

Key Macro Indicators

Indicator #1: GDP Growth (ISM PMI as Proxy)

What to track: ISM Manufacturing PMI (monthly)

Interpretation:

  • PMI > 55: Strong growth (expansion)
  • PMI 50-55: Moderate growth (goldilocks)
  • PMI 45-50: Slowing growth (late cycle)
  • PMI < 45: Contraction (recession)

Trading rule: PMI trending down from >55 ‚Üí rotate out of cyclicals (XLI, XLE), into defensives (XLP, XLU)

Indicator #2: Inflation (CPI, PCE)

What to track: Core CPI (ex-food/energy) and Core PCE (Fed's preferred measure)

Interpretation:

  • Core inflation < 2%: Low inflation regime
  • Core inflation 2-4%: Moderate inflation (Fed watching)
  • Core inflation > 4%: High inflation (Fed tightening)

Trading rule: If inflation accelerating (e.g., 3% ‚Üí 5% in 6 months) ‚Üí long commodities, short bonds

Indicator #3: Yield Curve (2Y vs 10Y)

What to track: 10-year yield minus 2-year yield (slope of curve)

Interpretation:

  • Curve > +100bps: Steep (strong growth expectations)
  • Curve +20 to +100bps: Normal (healthy expansion)
  • Curve 0 to +20bps: Flattening (late cycle warning)
  • Curve negative (inversion): Recession signal (12-18 months lead time)

Trading rule: Yield curve inversion ‚Üí reduce equity exposure 30-50%, buy long-dated puts

Part 3: Regime-Based Portfolio Allocation

Goldilocks Portfolio (60/30/10)

  • 60% Equities: Large-cap growth, tech (QQQ, AAPL, MSFT)
  • 30% Bonds: Investment-grade corporates (LQD)
  • 10% Alternatives: Real estate (VNQ), small allocation gold (GLD)

Strategy: Buy dips, long bias, 0.5-1% stop losses

Inflationary Boom Portfolio (40/40/20)

  • 40% Commodities: Oil (USO), copper (COPX), agriculture (DBA)
  • 40% Cyclicals: Energy (XLE), materials (XLB), industrials (XLI)
  • 20% Short Duration Bonds: Short-term treasuries (SHY) or floating-rate (FLOT)

Strategy: Momentum, trend-following, avoid long-duration bonds

Stagflation Portfolio (50/30/20)

  • 50% Cash: T-bills, money market (preserve capital)
  • 30% Gold: Physical, GLD, miners (GDXJ)
  • 20% Defensive Stocks: Consumer staples (XLP), utilities (XLU), healthcare (XLV)

Strategy: Capital preservation, reduce risk, short-term tactical trades only

Deflationary Bust Portfolio (70/20/10)

  • 70% Long-Duration Bonds: 20+ year treasuries (TLT)
  • 20% Defensive Equities: Low-volatility (SPLV), dividend aristocrats (NOBL)
  • 10% USD: Cash or dollar futures (UUP)

Strategy: Flight to safety, sell rallies in equities, long treasuries

Part 4: Regime Transitions (Early Warning Signals)

Goldilocks ‚Üí Inflationary Boom

Signal: CPI accelerating (2% ‚Üí 3%+), PMI > 55, commodities breaking out

Action: Rotate from tech/growth ‚Üí cyclicals/commodities. Shorten bond duration.

Inflationary Boom ‚Üí Stagflation

Signal: PMI falling below 50 while inflation still > 4% (growth stalling, inflation persistent)

Action: Sell equities, buy gold, raise cash. Fed can't save you (cutting rates would worsen inflation).

Stagflation ‚Üí Deflationary Bust

Signal: Inflation collapsing (4% ‚Üí 1%), PMI < 45, credit spreads widening

Action: Buy long-duration bonds (TLT), sell gold, defensive equity hedge only.

Deflationary Bust ‚Üí Goldilocks

Signal: PMI rising above 50, Fed easing, credit spreads tightening

Action: Buy equities aggressively (especially small-caps, IWM), sell bonds, risk-on.

üîç Critical Insight: Regime transitions create the MOST profit opportunity (and risk). Missing a transition = 6-12 months of underperformance or catastrophic loss.

Part 5: Using Signal Pilot for Regime Analysis

Janus Atlas: Multi-Asset Regime Confirmation

Feature: Overlay SPY, TLT, GLD, DXY to visualize regime

Goldilocks signal: SPY up, TLT flat/down, GLD down, DXY neutral

Deflationary bust signal: SPY down, TLT up, GLD up, DXY up (all safe havens rallying)

Pentarch Pilot Line: Institutional Regime Positioning

Feature: Track institutional flow into defensive vs cyclical sectors

Warning signal: If institutions rotating INTO defensives (XLP, XLU) while SPY rallies ‚Üí late-cycle signal

Harmonic Oscillator: Regime Volatility Detection

Feature: Identify when volatility regime shifts (low ‚Üí high = regime transition)

Action: VIX spike from <15 to >25 = regime change imminent (review macro indicators immediately)

Quiz: Test Your Understanding

Q1: PMI drops from 58 to 48 in 3 months. Inflation is 5%. What regime are you entering?

  • Check Core CPI/PCE: <2% (low), 2-4% (moderate), >4% (high inflation)?
  • Check yield curve: Steepening (expansion) or inverting (recession warning)?
  • Plot regime on 2√ó2 matrix (Growth vs Inflation)
  • Adjust portfolio allocation based on current regime
  • Set alerts for regime transition signals (PMI crosses 50, CPI breaks 3-4%)

Key Takeaways

  • Four regimes: Goldilocks (best for stocks), Boom (commodities), Stagflation (gold/cash), Bust (bonds)
  • Track PMI + CPI + Yield Curve to identify current regime
  • Regime transitions create biggest opportunities (and risks)
  • Adjust portfolio allocation to match regime (60/30/10 goldilocks, 70/20/10 bust, etc.)
  • Yield curve inversion = recession in 12-18 months (reduce equity exposure)
