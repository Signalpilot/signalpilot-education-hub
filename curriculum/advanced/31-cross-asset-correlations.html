Cross-Asset Correlations: Intermarket Analysis

Reading time ~17 min • Intermarket Relationships

Assets don't trade in isolation. Bonds lead, dollar drives, gold warns. Learn intermarket correlations to anticipate moves before they happen and avoid trades doomed by macro headwinds.

The Four Pillars of Intermarket Analysis

1. US Dollar (DXY)

Dollar strength impacts everything (commodities, stocks, crypto).

DXY UP (strong dollar):
→ Commodities DOWN (gold, oil priced in $, more expensive)
→ Foreign stocks DOWN (earnings hurt by FX)
→ Crypto DOWN (risk-off, capital to USD)
→ US exporters DOWN (goods more expensive abroad)

DXY DOWN (weak dollar):
→ Commodities UP (cheaper in USD terms)
→ Foreign stocks UP (FX tailwind)
→ Crypto UP (risk-on, alternative to USD)
→ US exporters UP (competitive advantage)

2. Treasury Yields (TNX, 10-Year)

Yields show bond market's growth/inflation expectations.

Yields RISING:
→ Stocks DOWN (competing asset, discount rates up)
→ Growth stocks HIT HARDEST (ARKK, tech, high P/E)
→ Value stocks OUTPERFORM (banks, energy, lower duration)
→ Gold DOWN (opportunity cost of holding non-yielding asset)

Yields FALLING:
→ Stocks UP (TINA: There Is No Alternative)
→ Growth stocks OUTPERFORM (low rates = high valuations)
→ Gold UP (safe haven + low opportunity cost)

3. VIX (Volatility Index)

VIX measures fear (SPX options implied volatility).

VIX < 15: Complacency (risk-on)
→ Stocks grind higher
→ Low volatility = favorable for selling premium

VIX 15-20: Normal (neutral)
→ Regular volatility

VIX 20-30: Elevated fear (caution)
→ Stocks choppy, downside risk
→ Reduce position sizing

VIX > 30: Panic (extreme fear)
→ Stocks in correction/crash mode
→ Sit out OR buy deep dips (capitulation)

4. Commodities (Gold, Oil)

Gold = inflation hedge + safe haven. Oil = growth proxy.

GOLD rising:
→ Inflation fears OR risk-off (flight to safety)
→ USD typically falling
→ Real yields falling (10Y yield - inflation)

OIL rising:
→ Economic growth (demand) OR supply shock
→ Inflation pressures (energy costs pass through)
→ Energy stocks up, airlines/transport down (input costs)

Key Correlation Relationships

SPY vs. TNX (Stock-Bond Inverse)

Typical correlation: Negative (yields up = stocks down).

Scenario 1: Yields spike (TNX 4.0% → 4.5%)
Result: SPY drops 3-5% (valuation compression)

Why: Higher yields = bonds more attractive vs. stocks
     Discount rate up = future earnings worth less

Trade: If TNX breaking resistance → Short SPY or reduce longs

DXY vs. Gold (Inverse)

Correlation: -0.7 to -0.9 (strong inverse).

DXY rallies (strong dollar) → Gold falls
DXY drops (weak dollar) → Gold rallies

Why: Gold priced in USD. Strong USD = gold more expensive (less demand)

Trade Setup:
DXY breaks support → Go long GLD (gold ETF)
DXY breaks resistance → Short GLD or avoid longs

Crypto vs. Nasdaq (High Correlation)

BTC/ETH correlate 0.6-0.8 with QQQ (Nasdaq).

Nasdaq rallies → Crypto typically rallies
Nasdaq dumps → Crypto dumps (often 2-3x magnitude)

Why: Crypto treated as risk-on tech asset (not yet uncorrelated)

Leading Indicator: Nasdaq often leads crypto by 6-24 hours
Trade: If QQQ breaks down overnight → Expect BTC to follow

Oil vs. Energy Stocks (Positive)

XLE (Energy ETF) tracks oil with lag.

Oil (WTI) rallies 5% → XLE typically up 3-4% (levered)
Oil drops 5% → XLE down 3-4%

Trade: Watch oil for early signal
If WTI breaks resistance → Long XLE (equity leverage)
If WTI breaks support → Short XLE or avoid

Divergence Trading (High-Probability Setups)

What is Intermarket Divergence?

When correlated assets move opposite directions = WARNING.

Example 1: SPY up, TNX up (both rising)
Normal: SPY up usually = TNX down (inverse)
Divergence: Both rising = inflation fears (not bullish)

Interpretation: Stock rally may be short-lived
Trade: Fade the SPY rally (short on weakness)

SPY Up + VIX Up (Fear Rally)

Unusual: Stocks rising while fear increases.

Scenario:
SPY: $520 → $525 (+1%)
VIX: 18 → 22 (+22%, fear spiking)

Interpretation: Short covering (forced buying), not conviction
Likely: Rally fails, reversal coming

Trade: Fade rally above $525 (short with tight stop)

DXY Up + SPY Up (Risk-On Divergence)

Unusual: Strong dollar hurts stocks (negative correlation).

Scenario:
DXY: 103 → 105 (strong dollar)
SPY: $520 → $525 (stocks rallying)

Interpretation: Temporary, one will resolve
Likely: SPY drops to realign (dollar strength wins)

Trade: Short SPY when shows weakness

Gold Up + Real Yields Up (Illogical)

Gold typically falls when real yields rise.

Scenario:
10Y Yield - Inflation = 1.5% (real yield, rising)
Gold (GLD): Rising

Interpretation: Fear overriding fundamentals (crisis)
Likely: Unsustainable, gold corrects OR yields collapse

Trade: Depends on context (geopolitical vs. economic)

Using Leading Indicators

Example: TNX (10-Year Yield) Leading SPY

Day 1: TNX breaks resistance (4.20% → 4.35%)
Day 2: SPY still holding (no reaction yet)
Day 3: SPY starts dropping (catching up to yields)

Trade Setup (Day 2):
- TNX already broke (leading indicator fired)
- SPY hasn't reacted yet (lagging)
- Action: Short SPY in anticipation of catch-up

Tactical Sector Rotation Trade

Observation: TNX rising (yields up), SPY flat

Analysis:
- Rising yields hurt growth, help value
- Banks (XLF) benefit (net interest margin up)
- Tech (XLK) hurt (valuation compression)

Trade:
Long XLF (banks), Short XLK (tech)
Pairs trade (market neutral, capture rotation)

Exit: When yield spike reverses

Correlation Breakdown (Regime Shifts)

When Correlations Break

Crisis = correlations go to 1.0 (everything falls together).

Normal Market:
SPY/Gold correlation: -0.3 (slightly inverse)
SPY/TLT correlation: -0.5 (bonds hedge stocks)

Crisis (2008, 2020 March):
SPY/Gold correlation: +0.8 (both sold for cash)
SPY/TLT correlation: +0.6 (liquidation, even bonds sold)

Implication: Diversification fails in crisis (except cash)

Detecting Regime Shifts

Rolling 30-Day Correlation (SPY vs. TLT):

Normal: -0.4 to -0.6 (inverse, bonds hedge stocks)
Warning: -0.2 to 0 (decorrelating)
Crisis: +0.3 to +0.7 (selling everything)

Trade Action:
If correlation flips positive → Risk-off (exit all longs)
If correlation normalizes → Risk-on (resume trading)

Practical Intermarket Checklist

Pre-Market Intermarket Scan (5 min)

  • [ ] DXY: Up/Down/Flat (strong dollar = headwind for commodities)
  • [ ] TNX: Up/Down/Flat (rising yields = headwind for growth stocks)
  • [ ] VIX: < 15 (calm), 15-20 (normal), > 20 (caution)
  • [ ] Gold: Up/Down (safe haven demand?)
  • [ ] Oil: Up/Down (inflation signal?)
  • [ ] Futures: ES, NQ direction

Intermarket Confirmation Matrix

Before taking SPY LONG:

Bullish Confirmations:
✓ DXY falling or flat (weak dollar = tailwind)
✓ TNX falling or stable (yields not rising)
✓ VIX < 20 (low fear)
✓ Sector rotation into cyclicals (XLF, XLE up)

Bearish Warnings:
✗ DXY spiking (strong dollar)
✗ TNX breaking resistance (yield spike)
✗ VIX > 20 and rising (fear increasing)
✗ Sector rotation into defensives (XLU, XLP)

If 3+ bullish confirmations → High confidence long
If 2+ bearish warnings → Reduce size or skip

Key Takeaways

  • DXY up = commodities/crypto down (inverse)
  • TNX up = stocks down, especially growth (inverse)
  • VIX > 20 = reduce size, > 30 = sit out
  • Divergences = warnings (SPY up + VIX up = unsustainable)
  • Use macro as filter (don't fight macro headwinds)
