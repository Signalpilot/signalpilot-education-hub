Algorithmic Execution: Order Types & Slippage Optimization

Reading time ~17 min • Execution Algorithms

How you enter matters as much as what you enter. Bad execution costs 0.1-0.5% per trade—that's your edge, gone. Learn advanced order types, execution algorithms, and slippage minimization used by professionals.

Hidden Execution Costs

Example: 1000 trades/year, $10K avg position

Retail execution (market orders):
Slippage per trade: 0.15% = $15
Annual slippage: $15 × 1000 = $15,000

Professional execution (limit orders + algos):
Slippage per trade: 0.03% = $3
Annual slippage: $3 × 1000 = $3,000

Savings: $12,000/year (just from execution!)

Advanced Order Types

1. Limit Order (Price Priority)

Specify max price willing to pay (buy) or min price to accept (sell).

SPY at $520.05 (bid $520.04, ask $520.06)

Market Order BUY: Fills at $520.06 (pay spread)
Limit Order BUY $520.04: Join bid, wait for fill

Advantage: Save spread ($0.02 = $20 on 1000 shares)
Risk: May not fill if price runs away

2. Stop-Limit Order (Breakout Entry)

Converts to limit order when stop price hit.

SPY at $520, resistance at $522

Stop-Limit: Stop $522.00, Limit $522.10
- If SPY hits $522 → Limit buy order at $522.10 activated
- Fills if can get $522.10 or better

Advantage: Avoid buying breakout into vacuum (get reasonable fill)
Risk: If price gaps through $522.10, no fill (missed trade)

3. Iceberg Order (Hide Size)

Display small size, hide large order underneath.

Want to buy 10,000 SPY shares without moving market

Iceberg: Display 500 shares, hide 9,500
- Order book shows 500 bid
- When 500 fills → Another 500 appears (refreshes)
- Repeat until 10,000 filled

Advantage: Don't reveal intent (avoid front-running)
Use: Large positions, avoid market impact

4. Post-Only Order (Maker Rebate)

Only fills if adding liquidity (not taking).

Order book:
Bid: $520.04 (500 shares)
Ask: $520.06 (500 shares)

Post-Only BUY $520.05:
- Sits between bid/ask (adds liquidity)
- Earns maker rebate ($0.001/share)
- Doesn't cross spread (no taker fee)

If order would cross spread → Canceled (post-only constraint)

Advantage: Earn rebates, reduce costs
Risk: May not fill quickly

5. Fill-or-Kill (FOK)

Execute entire order immediately or cancel.

Want 1000 shares at $520.05 or better

FOK: Fill all 1000 immediately OR cancel entire order
- If 800 available → Order canceled (partial not accepted)
- If 1000+ available → Fills all 1000

Use: When partial fills unacceptable (want all or nothing)

6. Immediate-or-Cancel (IOC)

Fill what's available immediately, cancel rest.

IOC: Buy 1000 shares at $520.05
- If 600 available → Fills 600, cancels 400
- Immediately executed (no lingering order)

Use: Probing liquidity without leaving order in book

Execution Algorithms

VWAP Algo (Volume-Weighted Average Price)

Distribute order throughout day based on historical volume profile.

Order: Buy 50,000 SPY shares, 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM

Algo logic:
- Analyze historical volume (% of daily volume per hour)
- Slice order proportionally
- Example: 9:30-10:30 AM = 20% volume → Buy 10,000 shares

Execution:
- Small clips every 1-5 minutes
- Adjusts dynamically to real-time volume
- Goal: Match VWAP benchmark (minimize cost)

Best for: Large orders, liquid assets, normal days

TWAP Algo (Time-Weighted Average Price)

Equal slices at regular time intervals.

Order: Buy 50,000 shares over 4 hours

Algo logic:
- Divide into equal slices (e.g., 10,000 every 30 min)
- Execute on schedule (10:00, 10:30, 11:00...)
- Ignores volume (pure time-based)

Best for: Illiquid assets, avoid signaling intent

Implementation Shortfall (IS) Algo

Minimize difference between decision price and execution price.

Decision price: $520.00 (when you decided to buy)
Target: Execute 10,000 shares close to $520.00

Algo logic:
- Starts aggressive (large clips, capture price)
- If price moves away → Becomes more aggressive (urgency)
- If price favorable → Slows down (patient, save cost)

Best for: Time-sensitive trades, minimize opportunity cost

POV Algo (Percentage of Volume)

Execute as fixed % of market volume.

Order: Buy 50,000 shares, 20% POV target

Algo logic:
- If market volume = 10,000/min → Buy 2,000/min (20%)
- If volume increases → Algo speeds up
- If volume decreases → Algo slows down

Best for: Large orders, avoid dominating tape (< 30% volume)

Smart Order Routing (SOR)

What is SOR?

Algorithm routes order to multiple venues to find best execution.

Want to buy 1000 SPY shares at $520.05 or better

SOR scans:
- NYSE: 300 shares at $520.05
- NASDAQ: 400 shares at $520.04 (best price!)
- BATS: 500 shares at $520.06

SOR routes:
1. 400 shares to NASDAQ at $520.04
2. 300 shares to NYSE at $520.05
3. 300 shares to BATS at $520.05 (only takes < $520.06)

Result: Avg fill $520.047 (better than single venue)

Dark Pool Routing

SOR also checks dark pools for hidden liquidity.

Visible markets: Only 500 shares available at $520.05
Dark pools: 5,000 shares available at $520.03 (midpoint)

SOR routes to dark pool:
- Fills 1,000 at $520.03 (saved $0.02/share = $20!)
- No market impact (hidden execution)

Advantage: Better prices, anonymity
Risk: Dark pools have own fees, slower fills

Minimizing Slippage

Strategy 1: Scale Into Position

Don't enter full size at once (average in).

Want 1000 shares SPY at ~$520

Bad: Market order 1000 shares
Fill: $520.08 (paid ask + slippage)

Good: Scale in over 3-5 orders
Order 1: 300 shares at $520.05 (limit)
Order 2: 300 shares at $520.03 (dip)
Order 3: 400 shares at $520.06 (breakout confirmed)

Avg Fill: $520.047 (saved $0.033 = $33)

Strategy 2: Join the Bid/Ask

Place limit at bid (buy) or ask (sell), wait for fill.

SPY: Bid $520.04, Ask $520.06

Market Order: Pay $520.06 (take liquidity)
Limit at Ask: Join $520.06 (if price dips, you fill)
Limit at Mid: $520.05 (aggressive maker, may fill on spread tightening)

Advantage: Save spread cost (maker rebate possible)
Risk: May not fill if price runs away

Strategy 3: Avoid Predictable Times

Don't execute during high-slippage windows.

TimeSlippage RiskWhy
9:30-9:45 AMHighOpening volatility, wide spreads
10:30 AM-12 PMLowStable, liquid
12:00-2:00 PMMediumLow volume (lunch)
3:45-4:00 PMHighClosing volatility, algos finishing

Strategy 4: Use Conditional Orders

Enter on pullback, not chase.

Scenario: Breakout above $522, want to buy

Bad: Market order at $522.20 (chasing)
Slippage: 0.10% ($10 on 10K position)

Good: Conditional limit order
"If price > $522, then limit buy $522.05"
- Waits for slight pullback
- Fills at $522.05 vs. $522.20

Savings: $0.15/share = $150 on 1000 shares

Latency & Co-Location

What is Latency?

Time delay between decision and execution.

Retail Trader:
Decision → Internet → Broker → Exchange
Latency: 50-200 milliseconds

Professional (Co-Located):
Decision → Direct fiber → Exchange
Latency: 0.5-2 milliseconds (100x faster)

Impact:
Fast market moves $0.10 in 100ms
Retail: Order arrives, price now $520.10 (slippage)
Pro: Order arrives, price still $520.00 (minimal slippage)

Latency Arbitrage (Why HFTs Win)

HFT sees your large order coming:
1. Your order: Buy 10,000 shares at market (50ms away)
2. HFT algo detects order flow (0.5ms to react)
3. HFT buys 5,000 shares at $520.05
4. Your order arrives, pushes price to $520.15
5. HFT sells 5,000 shares at $520.15 to you

HFT profit: $0.10/share × 5,000 = $500 (your slippage!)

Defense: Limit orders, iceberg orders, algos (don't signal intent)

Order Flow Toxicity

Informed vs. Uninformed Flow

Market makers detect "toxic" order flow (informed traders).

Uninformed Flow (Retail):
- Random timing
- Small size
- Market orders
→ Market makers willing to trade (low risk)

Informed Flow (Institutional):
- Timed around events
- Large size
- Aggressive (pay spread)
→ Market makers widen spreads (adverse selection risk)

Result: If detected as informed → Worse execution (wider spreads)

Minimizing Order Toxicity

  • Randomize timing: Don't execute at same time daily (pattern detection)
  • Use iceberg orders: Hide true size
  • Route to dark pools: Avoid lit markets for large orders
  • Avoid aggressive market orders: Signal urgency (widens spreads)

Execution Quality Metrics

1. Effective Spread

How much you paid vs. midpoint.

Midpoint: $520.00
Your fill: $520.08

Effective Spread: $0.08 (0.015%)

Benchmark:
< 0.01% = Excellent
0.01-0.05% = Good
> 0.05% = Poor (improve execution)

2. Fill Rate

% of limit orders that filled.

100 limit orders placed
85 filled

Fill Rate: 85%

Benchmark:
> 80% = Good balance (getting filled without overpaying)
< 50% = Too passive (missing trades)

3. Implementation Shortfall

Cost from decision to execution.

Decision price: $520.00 (when you decided to buy)
Avg fill price: $520.12 (actual execution)

Implementation Shortfall: $0.12 (0.023%)

Target: < 0.05% (minimize opportunity cost + slippage)

Key Takeaways

  • Execution costs 0.1-0.5% per trade (use limits, not market orders)
  • Scale in (3-5 orders) to average better price
  • Iceberg orders hide size, avoid market impact
  • VWAP algos minimize cost on large orders
  • Avoid 9:30-9:45 AM and 3:45-4:00 PM (high slippage)
