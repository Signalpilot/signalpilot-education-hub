Alpha vs. Beta

Understanding Beta

Beta = sensitivity to market moves (systematic risk).

Beta = 1.0: Moves with market (SPY up 10% → you up 10%)
Beta = 1.5: 1.5x market (SPY up 10% → you up 15%)
Beta = 0.5: 0.5x market (SPY up 10% → you up 5%)
Beta = -0.5: Inverse (SPY up 10% → you down 5%)

Calculating Alpha

Alpha = return above expected (risk-adjusted excess return).

Your return: +40%
SPY return: +20%
Your beta: 1.5

Expected return: 1.5 × 20% = 30% (beta explains this)
Alpha: 40% - 30% = +10% (skill-based excess return!)

Interpretation: +10% alpha = genuine edge (not just riding market)

Sharpe, Sortino, & Information Ratio

Sharpe Ratio

Sharpe = (Return - Risk-Free) / Std Dev

Your stats:
Return: 40%
Risk-Free: 4%
Std Dev: 18%

Sharpe = (40 - 4) / 18 = 2.0 (excellent!)

Sortino Ratio (Downside-Focused)

Sortino = (Return - Risk-Free) / Downside Deviation

Advantage: Only penalizes bad volatility (losses), not gains

Your stats:
Return: 40%
Downside Dev: 12% (vs. total 18%)

Sortino = (40 - 4) / 12 = 3.0 (better than Sharpe!)

Information Ratio (Alpha per Risk)

IR = Alpha / Tracking Error

Alpha: +10%
Tracking Error: 8%

IR = 10 / 8 = 1.25

Benchmark:
< 0.5 = Poor (high risk for alpha)
0.5-1.0 = Good
> 1.0 = Excellent (efficient alpha generation)

Key Takeaways

  • Decompose returns: which strategies, assets, time periods
  • Alpha vs. Beta: separate skill from market moves
  • Top trades = most profit (let winners run!)

  • Time-of-day matters: avoid low-win-rate windows
