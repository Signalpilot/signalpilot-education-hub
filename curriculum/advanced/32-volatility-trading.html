Volatility Trading: VIX Strategies & Gamma Exposure

Reading time ~18 min • Volatility & Options Flow

Volatility isn't noise—it's tradeable. VIX predicts fear, gamma drives pinning. Learn how to trade volatility directly, exploit gamma exposure, and use options flow as a directional edge.

Understanding the VIX

What is VIX?

VIX = CBOE Volatility Index = Implied volatility of SPX 30-day options.

VIX measures: How much the market EXPECTS SPX to move

VIX 15 = Market expects 15% annualized vol
       = ~0.94% daily move (15% / √252 trading days)

VIX 30 = Market expects 30% annualized vol
       = ~1.88% daily move (2x the movement)

Important: VIX measures EXPECTED volatility, not actual

VIX Regime Classification

VIX LevelRegimeMarket State
< 12Extreme ComplacencyGrind higher, low vol (unsustainable)
12-15Low VolatilityCalm, bullish bias
15-20NormalHealthy volatility
20-30Elevated FearCorrective, choppy
30-40High FearSignificant decline
> 40PanicCrash/capitulation (buy signal)

VIX Mean Reversion

VIX spikes hard, then reverts to mean (~15-18).

Historical Pattern:
VIX average: ~16-17 (long-term mean)
VIX spikes: Rare, violent, SHORT-LIVED

2020 COVID: VIX 12 → 82 (6x spike), back to 25 in 3 weeks
2022 Bear: VIX 15 → 36 (2.4x spike), back to 20 in 6 weeks

Implication: VIX > 30 = buy opportunity (mean reversion trade)

Trading the VIX

Method 1: VIX Futures (Direct Exposure)

Futures trade forward expectations of VIX.

VIX Spot: 20 (current implied vol)
VIX 1-month future: 21.5 (contango, expect higher)
VIX 2-month future: 22.8

Contango = Futures > Spot (normal state, costs to hold long)
Backwardation = Futures < Spot (fear, VIX spiking)

Trade:
Long VIX futures when spot VIX < 15 (expecting spike)
Short VIX futures when spot VIX > 30 (expecting reversion)

Method 2: VIX ETFs (UVXY, VXX)

ETFs track VIX futures (with decay from contango).

UVXY: 1.5x leveraged VIX futures ETF

Structure: Rolls front-month VIX futures daily
Problem: Contango decay (loses ~5-10%/month in calm markets)

Use Case: SHORT-TERM hedge or spike trade
DO NOT hold long-term (decay kills)

Trade:
Buy UVXY when VIX < 15 AND market topping (hedge)
Short UVXY when VIX > 30 (mean reversion + decay)
Hold < 5 days (avoid decay)

Method 3: SPY Puts (Indirect Vol Trade)

Buying puts = Long volatility (put premium rises when VIX spikes).

Setup: VIX 14, SPY $520, expect volatility spike

Trade: Buy SPY $510 puts (2 weeks out)
Cost: $1.50/contract ($150)

Scenario 1: VIX spikes to 25, SPY drops to $515
Put value: $2.80 (profit from vol + directional)

Scenario 2: VIX stays 14, SPY flat at $520
Put value: $0.80 (decay loss)

Risk: Theta decay (time kills premium)
Reward: Gamma + Vega (move + vol expansion)

Gamma Exposure (GEX)

What is Gamma?

Gamma = rate of change of delta (option sensitivity acceleration).

Delta: How much option price moves per $1 move in underlying
Gamma: How much delta changes per $1 move

Example:
Call option: Delta 0.50, Gamma 0.05

SPY moves $1 up:
New Delta: 0.50 + 0.05 = 0.55
(Option becomes MORE sensitive to price moves)

Implication: Near expiry + ATM = HIGH gamma (explosive moves)

Market Maker Gamma Hedging

Market makers hedge gamma by buying/selling underlying.

Scenario: Dealer sold 10,000 SPY $520 calls (0-DTE)

Dealer Position: Short gamma (loses if SPY moves)

Hedging Behavior:
If SPY rises → Dealer buys SPY (delta hedge, creates buying pressure)
If SPY falls → Dealer sells SPY (delta hedge, creates selling pressure)

Result: Dealers AMPLIFY moves (buy high, sell low = momentum)

Gamma Pinning (Max Pain)

Price gravitates toward strike with most open interest (gamma neutral).

SPY options expiring today (0-DTE):
$515 strike: 5,000 contracts OI
$520 strike: 50,000 contracts OI ← MAX PAIN
$525 strike: 8,000 contracts OI

Implication: Dealers want SPY to close at $520 (max options expire worthless)

Hedging: As SPY approaches $520, dealers hedge less (reduce delta)
Result: Price "pins" to $520 into close (gravity effect)

Trade: Fade moves away from max pain strike (mean reversion)

0-DTE Options (Zero Days to Expiry)

The 0-DTE Phenomenon

0-DTE options = expire same day (extreme gamma, massive volume).

Statistics (2023-2024):
- 0-DTE = 50%+ of SPY options volume
- Most active: Monday, Wednesday, Friday expirations
- Drives intraday volatility (gamma flips)

Characteristics:
- High gamma (explosive delta changes)
- Rapid theta decay (premium evaporates by close)
- Dealer hedging = amplified moves

Gamma Flip Zones

When price crosses strikes with heavy OI, dealer hedging flips direction.

Setup: SPY at $519, 0-DTE $520 calls have 100K OI

Below $520:
- Calls OTM (low delta)
- Dealers short calls, delta-neutral (small hedge)

Crosses $520:
- Calls go ITM (delta increases rapidly)
- Dealers must buy MORE SPY (delta hedge)
- Buying pressure accelerates move to $521

Result: Gamma flip = momentum acceleration through strike

Trade: Buy breakout above high-OI strike (momentum trade)

0-DTE Trading Strategy

Pre-Market:
1. Identify max pain strike (highest OI)
2. Mark gamma flip zones (large OI strikes)
3. Note SPY open relative to these levels

Intraday:
IF price < max pain:
→ Fade weakness (expect reversion to max pain)

IF price breaks gamma flip zone:
→ Trade momentum (dealers amplify move)

Last Hour (3-4 PM):
→ Strongest pinning effect (avoid fighting max pain)

Options Flow as Directional Signal

Smart Money Options Indicators

Large, aggressive options trades = institutional intent.

Bullish Signals:
- Large call sweeps (market orders, paying ask)
- Far OTM calls (high conviction, low delta bets)
- Long-dated calls (3-6 months, position building)

Bearish Signals:
- Large put sweeps (paying ask for protection)
- ATM/ITM puts (direct bearish bet)
- Unusual put volume spike (hedging)

Call/Put Ratio Analysis

Ratio shows sentiment (contrarian indicator).

Put/Call Ratio = Put Volume / Call Volume

Interpretation:
< 0.7 = Extreme bullish sentiment (potential top)
0.7-1.0 = Neutral to bullish
1.0-1.3 = Neutral to bearish
> 1.3 = Extreme bearish sentiment (potential bottom)

Contrarian Trade:
P/C > 1.5 + VIX > 25 = Capitulation (BUY signal)
P/C < 0.6 + VIX < 15 = Complacency (SELL signal)

Unusual Options Activity (UOA)

Volume >> Open Interest = NEW positions (not rolling).

Example:
SPY $530 calls (1 month out)
Open Interest: 5,000
Today's Volume: 25,000 (5x OI)

Analysis:
- Large NEW positions opened
- Premium paid (not collected)
- Bullish bet on $530+ within 30 days

Trade: Align with flow (buy underlying or calls)

Volatility Surface & Skew

Volatility Smile/Skew

OTM puts more expensive than OTM calls (fear premium).

SPY at $520:
$500 put (OTM): IV = 22% (expensive, fear of crash)
$520 call (ATM): IV = 18% (baseline)
$540 call (OTM): IV = 16% (cheap, no fear of spike)

Put Skew = 22% - 16% = 6% premium for downside protection

Implication: Market prices tail risk (crashes > rallies)

Skew Trading

Normal Skew: 4-6% (puts more expensive)
High Skew: 8-12% (extreme fear, puts very expensive)

Trade:
High skew + VIX > 25 → Sell OTM puts (collect premium, mean reversion)
Low skew + complacency → Buy OTM puts (cheap protection)

Volatility Crush (Post-Event)

Earnings Volatility Crush

IV spikes before earnings, collapses after (regardless of move).

AAPL Earnings Tomorrow:
IV Today: 60% (elevated, pricing uncertainty)
IV After Earnings: 25% (drops to normal)

Option Trade:
Bought $180 call for $3.00 (60% IV)
AAPL moves from $175 → $182 (good directional call)

BUT: IV drops 60% → 25% (volatility crush)
Option value: $2.50 (LOSS despite correct direction!)

Lesson: Don't buy options into known events (vol crush kills)

Post-Event Trade

Better Strategy: SELL options before event (collect IV premium)

Sell iron condor before earnings:
Sell $170 put + Buy $165 put
Sell $185 call + Buy $190 call

Collect: $1.50 credit

After earnings: IV crushes, options decay
Keep credit if stock stays $170-$185 (high probability)

Key Takeaways

  • VIX mean reverts: < 15 expect spike, > 30 expect reversion
  • Gamma pinning: Price gravitates to max pain (high OI strike)
  • 0-DTE: Massive volume, drives intraday volatility (gamma flips)
  • Options flow: Large sweeps = smart money intent
  • Vol crush: Never buy options into known events (IV collapses)
