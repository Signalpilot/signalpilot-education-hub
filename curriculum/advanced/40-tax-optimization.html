Tax Optimization for Traders

Reading time ~13 min • Tax Efficiency

Taxes can cost 20-40% of gains. Optimize and keep more. Learn short vs. long-term gains, trader tax status, wash sales, tax-loss harvesting, and entity structuring for professionals.

Trader Tax Status (TTS)

Qualifying for TTS

IRS criteria (all must be met):

  • Substantial activity: Trade frequently (750+ trades/year guideline)
  • Regularity: Trade most days market open
  • Continuity: Trading is primary income source
  • Intent: Profit from short-term price movements (not dividends/interest)

TTS Benefits

Business Expense Deductions:
- Home office (dedicated trading space)
- Trading platform fees ($200/month × 12 = $2,400)
- Market data (Bloomberg, Reuters = $2,000/month)
- Computer equipment ($5,000)
- Education (courses, books)
- Professional fees (CPA, attorney)

Total deductions: $25K-50K/year (reduces taxable income!)

Mark-to-Market (MTM) Election

What is MTM?

Treat all positions as closed on Dec 31 (paper gains/losses count).

Without MTM:
- Only realized gains taxed
- Unrealized losses don't help (can't deduct)

With MTM:
- All positions marked to market (treated as sold Dec 31)
- Unrealized gains = taxable
- Unrealized losses = deductible

Benefit: Eliminates wash sale rule (see below)

When to Elect MTM

ScenarioMTM?
Day trader (close all positions daily)Yes (avoids wash sales)
Swing trader (hold 1-7 days)Maybe (consult CPA)
Position trader (hold weeks-months)No (lose long-term capital gains)

Wash Sale Rule

The Rule

Can't deduct loss if you rebuy "substantially identical" security within 30 days.

Example (Wash Sale):
Jan 1: Buy 100 SPY at $500 = $50,000
Jan 15: Sell 100 SPY at $480 = $48,000 (loss: -$2,000)
Jan 20: Buy 100 SPY at $485

Result: -$2,000 loss DISALLOWED (bought back within 30 days)
Instead: Loss added to cost basis of new position

Avoiding Wash Sales

Option 1: Wait 31 days before rebuying
Jan 15: Sell SPY (loss)
Feb 16: Can rebuy SPY (31+ days, loss deductible)

Option 2: Buy similar but not identical security
Sell SPY (S&P 500 ETF)
Buy IVV or VOO (also S&P 500 ETFs, NOT identical per IRS)

Option 3: MTM election (eliminates wash sale rule)

Tax-Loss Harvesting

Strategy

Realize losses to offset gains (reduce taxable income).

Your year-end positions:
- SPY: +$50,000 gain (sell, lock gain)
- QQQ: -$15,000 unrealized loss (sell, realize loss)
- IWM: +$5,000 gain (sell)

Net taxable gain: $50K + $5K - $15K = $40K

Tax saved: $15K loss × 37% rate = $5,550

Action: After 31 days, rebuy QQQ (maintain exposure, saved taxes)

Annual Strategy

December Tax-Loss Harvesting Checklist:
1. Review all open positions
2. Identify unrealized losses
3. If loss > 30 days old → Sell (harvest loss)
4. Wait 31 days, rebuy (or buy similar ETF)
5. Use losses to offset gains

Result: Lower tax bill, maintain market exposure

LLC vs. S-Corp for Traders

Scenario: $200K trading profit

Individual:
Income tax: $200K × 32% = $64,000

LLC (pass-through):
Income tax: $200K × 32% = $64,000
SE tax: $200K × 15.3% = $30,600
Total: $94,600 (ouch!)

S-Corp:
Salary: $80K (SE tax: $80K × 15.3% = $12,240)
Distribution: $120K (NO SE tax!)
Income tax: $200K × 32% = $64,000
Total: $76,240

Savings: $94,600 - $76,240 = $18,360/year!

Retirement Accounts for Traders

Tax-Advantaged Trading

  • IRA (Traditional): Tax-deferred growth, deduct contributions
  • Roth IRA: Tax-free growth (no tax on gains!)
  • Solo 401(k): $69K contribution limit (2024), self-employed

Roth IRA Strategy

Max contribution: $7,000/year (2024)

Year 1: Contribute $7,000
Trade aggressively (short-term gains = normally 37% tax)
Year 10: Grow to $70,000

Withdraw (age 59.5+): $70,000 tax-free!

Benefit: NO capital gains tax (vs. 37% short-term in taxable account)

Key Takeaways

  • Hold > 1 year for long-term gains (0-20% vs. 22-37%)
  • Trader Tax Status: deduct business expenses
  • MTM election: avoid wash sales (day traders)
  • Tax-loss harvest: offset gains with losses (Dec strategy)
  • S-Corp saves self-employment tax (vs. LLC)
  • Roth IRA: tax-free gains (max it out!)
