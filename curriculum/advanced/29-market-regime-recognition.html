Market Regime Recognition: Adaptive Trading Systems

Reading time ~17 min • Regime Detection & Adaptation

Markets shift between regimes—trending, mean-reverting, volatile. One strategy doesn't work everywhere. Learn to detect regime changes in real-time and adapt your approach before you get chopped up.

The Four Core Market Regimes

Regime 1: Trending (Directional)

Characteristics:

  • Clear higher highs + higher lows (uptrend) or lower lows + lower highs (downtrend)
  • Price sustains away from mean (EMA, VWAP)
  • Volume increases on trend moves, decreases on pullbacks
  • ADX > 25 (strong trend)
Best Strategies:
- Pullback entries (buy dips in uptrend, sell rallies in downtrend)
- Breakout continuations
- Trend following (ride the move)

Avoid:
- Fading trends (fighting momentum)
- Range-bound strategies (no mean reversion)

Regime 2: Mean-Reverting (Range-Bound)

Characteristics:

  • Price oscillates between defined support/resistance
  • Repeatedly touches and bounces from extremes
  • Volume spikes at range extremes (exhaustion)
  • ADX < 20 (weak trend)
Best Strategies:
- Fade extremes (sell highs, buy lows)
- VWAP reversions (price returns to VWAP)
- Oscillator extremes (RSI, Stochastic)

Avoid:
- Breakout trades (false breaks common)
- Trend following (no sustained direction)

Regime 3: High Volatility (Chaotic)

Characteristics:

  • Large price swings, whipsaws
  • ATR spikes (2-3x normal)
  • VIX > 25-30 (elevated fear)
  • News-driven (FOMC, earnings, geopolitical)
Best Strategies:
- Sit out (preserve capital)
- Reduce size 50-75% if trading
- Wider stops (avoid noise stopouts)
- Very short timeframes (scalp only)

Avoid:
- Normal position sizing (too risky)
- Swing trades (overnight risk extreme)

Regime 4: Low Volatility (Compression)

Characteristics:

  • Tight range, narrow candles
  • ATR at multi-week lows
  • Bollinger Bands squeeze
  • Precedes regime shift (expansion coming)
Best Strategies:
- Wait for breakout (compression → expansion)
- Prepare directional setups (don't trade the chop)
- Reduce size or frequency

Avoid:
- Forcing trades (low R:R in tight ranges)
- Scalping (spread costs eat profits)

Quantifying Regimes with Indicators

ADX (Average Directional Index)

Measures trend strength (NOT direction).

ADX ValueRegimeStrategy
< 20Weak/No trendMean reversion, fade extremes
20-25Developing trendEarly trend entries
25-40Strong trendTrend following, pullbacks
> 40Very strong trendRide trend, avoid fades
> 50Extreme (exhaustion risk)Trail stops, scale out

ATR (Average True Range)

Measures volatility (average range per candle).

ATR Regime Classification:
Current ATR: $2.50
20-period ATR avg: $2.00

ATR Ratio: $2.50 / $2.00 = 1.25 (25% above average)

Interpretation:
< 0.8x = Low volatility (compression)
0.8-1.2x = Normal volatility
1.2-1.5x = Elevated volatility
> 1.5x = High volatility (reduce size)

Bollinger Band Width

Measures range expansion/contraction.

BB Width = (Upper Band - Lower Band) / Middle Band

Interpretation:
BB Width < 4% = Tight range (compression, breakout pending)
BB Width 4-8% = Normal range
BB Width > 8% = Wide range (high volatility)

Linear Regression Slope

Measures directional bias over N periods.

20-period linear regression slope:

Slope > 0.5° = Uptrend (bullish bias)
Slope -0.5° to +0.5° = No trend (range-bound)
Slope < -0.5° = Downtrend (bearish bias)

R² (fit quality):
R² > 0.7 = Strong trend (price follows line)
R² < 0.3 = Weak trend (choppy)

Regime Detection Framework

Multi-Indicator Regime Score

Regime Detection Checklist:

1. ADX Score:
   ADX > 25 → +1 (trending)
   ADX < 20 → -1 (ranging)

2. ATR Score:
   ATR > 1.5x avg → +1 (volatile)
   ATR < 0.8x avg → -1 (compressed)

3. Bollinger Width:
   BB Width > 8% → +1 (expanded)
   BB Width < 4% → -1 (compressed)

4. Price vs. VWAP:
   Price > VWAP + 0.5% → +1 (trending up)
   Price < VWAP - 0.5% → -1 (trending down)
   Within 0.5% of VWAP → 0 (ranging)

5. Volume Trend:
   Volume increasing on directional moves → +1 (trend)
   Volume flat → 0 (range)

Total Score:
+3 to +5 = Strong Trending (trade pullbacks, breakouts)
0 to +2 = Weak Trend / Range (mean reversion)
-3 to -5 = Compressed (wait for breakout)

Regime Transition Patterns

Compression → Trending (Breakout)

Phase 1: Compression
- Low ATR, tight Bollinger Bands
- ADX < 20 (no trend)
- Price consolidates in narrow range

Phase 2: Breakout
- ATR spikes (volatility expansion)
- Volume increases dramatically
- Price breaks range high/low
- ADX starts rising (20 → 25+)

Phase 3: Trend Establishment
- ADX > 25, climbing
- Price sustains directional move
- Pullbacks shallow (higher lows in uptrend)

Trading:
- Enter on breakout confirmation (close above range + volume)
- Ride trend until ADX peaks and reverses

Trending → Mean Reversion (Exhaustion)

Phase 1: Strong Trend
- ADX > 40 (very strong)
- Extended move (> 3% from VWAP)
- Volume climaxing

Phase 2: Exhaustion Signals
- ADX peaks and starts declining
- Price fails to make new high/low
- Volume drops on continuation attempts
- Divergence (price up, ADX down)

Phase 3: Range Formation
- ADX drops below 25
- Price oscillates around VWAP
- Support/resistance defined

Trading:
- Exit trend trades when ADX peaks
- Switch to fade extremes (mean reversion)

Mean Reversion → Volatile (Shock)

Trigger: Unexpected news (FOMC, geopolitical)

Before: ADX 18, ATR $1.50, ranging between $519-521
After: ADX 35, ATR $3.50, violent swings $518-524

Action:
- Close all positions (risk spike)
- Wait for volatility to normalize
- Do NOT try to "buy the dip" in chaos

Position Sizing by Regime

Base Risk: 2% per trade

Regime Adjustments:
Strong Trending (ADX > 30): 2.0% (full size, high confidence)
Weak Trend (ADX 20-25): 1.5% (reduced, lower confidence)
Range-Bound (ADX < 20): 1.0% (tight targets, lower R)
High Volatility (ATR > 1.5x): 0.5-1.0% (risk management)
Compression: 0% (wait for regime shift)

Janus Atlas: Regime-Specific Interpretation

Trending Regime + Janus Sweep:
- Sweep = institutional accumulation
- High probability reversal (70%+ win rate)
- Trade aggressively (2% risk)

Ranging Regime + Janus Sweep:
- Sweep = stop hunt, range continuation
- Lower probability (50-60% win rate)
- Trade conservatively (1% risk) or skip

Volatile Regime + Janus Sweep:
- Ambiguous (could reverse OR continue violently)
- Skip (too risky)

Key Takeaways

  • Four regimes: Trending, range-bound, volatile, compressed
  • Use ADX + ATR + BB Width to classify regime
  • Trending: Trade pullbacks, breakouts (with trend)
  • Range: Fade extremes, mean reversion to VWAP
  • Volatile: Sit out or drastically reduce size
  • Backtest by regime to find your edge
