  1. Document your 6-month trading results starting now (builds credibility for prop firm applications) ‚Äî Whether you plan to join a prop firm, raise capital, or just improve, you need a verifiable track record. Starting tomorrow, keep detailed monthly performance records: (1) Create a simple spreadsheet with columns: Month, Starting Capital, Ending Capital, Profit/Loss $, Profit/Loss %, # of Trades, Win Rate, Largest Win, Largest Loss, Notes. (2) Record data at the end of each month (takes 5 minutes). (3) After 6 months, you'll have a complete track record showing consistency (or lack thereof). Example 6-month record: Month 1: $10K ‚Üí $10,850 (+8.5%, 23 trades, 61% win rate). Month 2: $10,850 ‚Üí $11,200 (+3.2%, 19 trades, 58% win rate). Month 3: $11,200 ‚Üí $10,900 (-2.7%, 21 trades, 52% win rate). Month 4: $10,900 ‚Üí $11,600 (+6.4%, 24 trades, 63% win rate). Month 5: $11,600 ‚Üí $12,100 (+4.3%, 22 trades, 59% win rate). Month 6: $12,100 ‚Üí $12,800 (+5.8%, 20 trades, 61% win rate). Total: +28% over 6 months, avg 4.7%/month, 129 trades, 59% avg win rate. Why this matters: When you apply to prop firms (FTMO, TopStepTrader, etc.), they ask: "Show us your track record." If you say "I made money but didn't track it," you're done. If you show 6-12 months of consistent profitability with detailed stats, you're credible. Example: Marcus applied to a prop firm. They asked for his track record. He showed 8 months of data averaging +6.2%/month with 62% win rate. They funded him with $50K immediately. His friend Jake applied with no track record ("I'm profitable, trust me"). Rejected instantly. Why this works: Prop firms, investors, and even your own psychology require proof of consistency. A 6-12 month track record proves you're not lucky‚Äîyou have a repeatable edge. It also forces discipline (you can't hide bad months if you're tracking). Action: Tonight, create a spreadsheet titled "Trading Track Record." Add columns for Month, Capital, P/L, Win Rate, Trades. Starting December 1, record your first month. Do this for 6 months minimum before applying to any prop firm or considering trading full-time.
  2. Research 3 prop firms and their evaluation requirements tonight (know what's required before you apply) ‚Äî If you're considering prop trading as a career path, you need to understand the landscape. Tonight, spend 30 minutes researching these 3 major prop firms: (1) FTMO (most popular, $10K-$200K funding, 80% profit split, $155-$1,080 evaluation fee depending on account size). (2) TopStepTrader (futures-focused, $25K-$150K funding, 90% profit split after first payout, $165/month subscription during eval). (3) MyForexFunds (forex-focused, $5K-$300K funding, 80-90% profit split, $39-$999 one-time eval fee). For each, note: (A) Evaluation requirements. Example: FTMO requires 10% profit in phase 1 (30 days max), 5% profit in phase 2 (60 days), max 10% total drawdown, max 5% daily loss. (B) Profit split (what % you keep vs firm). (C) Evaluation cost (what you pay upfront to attempt the challenge). (D) Rules (can you hold overnight? Trade news? Max lot size?). Example comparison: FTMO $100K account: $540 eval fee, need +10% ($10K profit) in 30 days, max -10% ($10K) drawdown, 80% profit split (you keep $8K of every $10K you make). TopStepTrader $50K account: $165/month eval, need +$3K profit (6%), max -$2K daily loss, max -$3K total loss, 90% split after first $5K payout. Why this matters: Most traders fail prop firm evals because they don't understand the rules. They over-leverage trying to hit profit targets quickly, violate the 5% daily loss rule, and fail. By researching requirements BEFORE you pay, you can practice those exact constraints in your personal account first. Example: David paid $540 for FTMO $100K eval without researching. He didn't realize there's a 5% daily loss limit ($5K). On day 3, he took 3 aggressive trades in a row trying to hit the 10% profit target fast. Lost -$6,200 in one day. Instantly failed the eval. Lost his $540 fee. If he'd researched first, he'd know: "I need strict risk management, max 2-3% risk per trade, can't blow past $5K daily loss." Action: Tonight, go to FTMO.com, TopStepTrader.com, and MyForexFunds.com. Read their evaluation rules. Write down: profit targets, drawdown limits, daily loss limits, time limits. Then ask yourself: "Can I hit these targets with my current strategy and risk management?" If no, adjust your strategy BEFORE paying for an eval.
  3. Set a realistic 'full-time trading' income goal and calculate the capital needed (prevents premature quitting your job) ‚Äî Most traders quit their job too early and blow up because they underestimate capital requirements. Tonight, calculate how much capital you REALLY need to go full-time. Simple formula: Monthly income needed √∑ 0.03 = Minimum capital required. Why 3% per month? Because consistent traders average 3-5% monthly returns. 10%+ monthly is unsustainable long-term (you'll have drawdown months). Example calculation: You need $6K/month to cover living expenses (rent, food, bills, etc.). $6K √∑ 0.03 = $200K minimum capital. At 3% monthly return, $200K generates $6K/month. But some months you'll make 5% ($10K), other months -2% (-$4K). Over time, it averages 3-4%. Reality check: If you have $30K capital and need $6K/month, you'd need 20% monthly returns to survive. That's impossible to sustain. You'd blow up in 3-6 months. Example: Luis quit his $75K/year job with $40K trading capital, thinking "I'm making 12% per month, I'll be fine!" His monthly expenses were $5,500. For 3 months, he made $4,800/month (12% of $40K). Month 4: He had a -15% drawdown (-$6K). His capital dropped to $34K. Now he's making 12% of $34K = $4,080/month, not enough to cover $5,500 expenses. He had to dip into savings. Month 6: Another drawdown. Capital at $28K. Can't sustain lifestyle. Had to get a job again, but now he's job-hunting while stressed about money. What he should have done: Keep his job until he had $180K capital ($5,500/month √∑ 0.03 = $183K). Then quit. Or reduce living expenses to $3K/month (needs only $100K capital). Why this works: Going full-time too early is the #1 reason traders fail. They need to extract unsustainable returns to pay rent, which forces over-leveraging, which causes blowups. By calculating realistic capital needs FIRST, you avoid this trap. Action: Tonight, calculate: (1) Monthly expenses (rent, food, bills, insurance, etc.). (2) Desired monthly income (expenses + savings). (3) Capital needed (monthly income √∑ 0.03). Write this number down: "I need $[__]K capital before I can consider full-time trading." Then set a realistic timeline to reach that amount (2-5 years for most traders).

The Trader Progression Path

Stage 1: Retail Beginner ($1K-10K Capital)

Duration: 6-12 months

Focus:
- Learn basics (price action, indicators, risk management)
- Paper trade first (3 months minimum)
- Start small ($1K-5K live)
- Survive (don't blow account!)

Realistic Expectations:
- Expected performance: High probability (learning curve)
- Monthly return: -5% to +5% (break-even goal)
- Income: $0 (still learning, don't quit job)

Success Metric: Consistent execution (follow plan, no revenge trading)

Stage 2: Consistent Retail ($10K-50K)

Duration: 12-24 months

Focus:
- Refine edge (backtest, journal, iterate)
- Increase capital (save, compound)
- Consistent profitability (3-6 months green)

Realistic Expectations:
- Expected performance: High probability
- Monthly return: 5-15%
- Income: $500-$3K/month (supplement, not replace job)

Success Metric: 6 consecutive profitable months

Stage 3: Advanced Retail ($50K-250K)

Duration: 24-48 months

Focus:
- Scale capital (compound + deposits)
- Systematize (automation, multiple strategies)
- Professional infrastructure (setup, software)

Realistic Expectations:
- Expected performance: High probability
- Monthly return: 5-10% (lower as capital grows)
- Income: $2.5K-$25K/month (approaching full-time income)

Success Metric: 12 months profitability, Sharpe > 1.5

Stage 4: Full-Time Independent ($250K+)

Duration: 48+ months

Focus:
- Trade full-time (quit job)
- Diversify strategies (reduce single-strategy risk)
- Manage drawdowns (conservative sizing)

Realistic Expectations:
- Expected performance: High probability
- Monthly return: 3-8% (lower risk, higher capital)
- Income: $7.5K-$20K+/month ($90K-$240K/year)

Success Metric: 24 months profitability, support lifestyle from trading

Alternative Career Paths

Path 1: Prop Trading Firm

Trade firm capital, keep 50-80% of profits.

Structure:
- Firm provides capital ($50K-$500K)
- You keep 50-80% of profits
- Firm covers losses (up to limit)

Pros:
- No personal capital required
- Large buying power
- Risk shared with firm

Cons:
- Profit split (keep 50-80%, not 100%)
- Daily/monthly loss limits
- Firm rules (restricted strategies)

Top Prop Firms:
- SMB Capital (equity day trading)
- Jane Street (market making, quant)
- TopstepTrader (futures, evaluation model)
- FTMO (forex, evaluation model)

Path 2: Hedge Fund / Asset Manager

Manage money for clients, charge 2/20 fees.

Structure:
- 2% annual management fee (on AUM)
- 20% performance fee (on profits)

Example: $10M AUM, +15% return
Management fee: $10M √ó 2% = $200K
Performance fee: ($10M √ó 15%) √ó 20% = $300K
Total income: $500K

Pros:
- Manage large capital (leverage expertise)
- Predictable income (management fees)
- Institutional resources

Cons:
- Requires track record (2-3 years proven returns)
- Regulatory (Series 65, RIA registration)
- Client management (reporting, redemptions)

Path 3: Trading Educator / Content Creator

Teach trading, sell courses/signals/tools.

Income Streams:
- Courses: $100-$2,000 (one-time or subscription)
- Membership: $50-$200/month (recurring)
- YouTube ad revenue: $2-$10 per 1K views
- Affiliate commissions: Broker referrals

Example:
- 500 course sales/year √ó $500 = $250K
- 1,000 members √ó $100/month = $1.2M/year
- YouTube (1M views/month) = $5K-$20K/month

Pros:
- Scalable (teach once, sell forever)
- Lower risk (vs. trading own capital)
- Build audience/brand

Cons:
- Must be credible (track record required)
- Competitive (many scammers, hard to stand out)
- Time-intensive (content creation)

Path 4: Quant Developer / Algo Trader

Build systematic strategies for firms or self.

Role:
- Develop algorithms (Python, C++)
- Backtest strategies (historical data)
- Deploy systems (live trading)

Salary (Institutional):
- Junior: $100K-$150K
- Mid-level: $150K-$300K
- Senior / Quant PM: $300K-$1M+

Pros:
- High income (predictable salary)
- Intellectual challenge
- No emotional trading (code executes)

Cons:
- Requires programming skills (Python, stats, ML)
- Competitive hiring (top firms = PhD preferred)
- Long hours (finance culture)

Common Pitfalls

Pitfall 1: Quitting Job Too Early

Mistake: 3 good months ‚Üí quit job ‚Üí pressure ‚Üí blow account

Reality: Need 12-24 months consistent profitability BEFORE quitting

Safe Approach:
1. Trade part-time (2-3 years)
2. Build capital ($100K+)
3. Prove consistency (12+ months green)
4. Save 12 months expenses (safety net)
5. THEN quit job

Pitfall 2: Overleveraging Small Accounts

Mistake: $5K account, risk 10%/trade to "grow faster"
Result: 3 losses = -30% = $3.5K left (hard to recover)

Correct: $5K account, risk 2%/trade
- Slower growth, but sustainable
- Focus on skill-building, not account growth

Pitfall 3: Ignoring Taxes & Expenses

Mistake: "I made $100K trading!" (gross, not net)

Reality:
- Taxes (30-40%): -$35K
- Software/data: -$5K
- Commissions: -$3K
- Net: $57K (43% reduction!)

Plan: Set aside 40% for taxes quarterly

Success Factors

Top 5 Traits of Successful Traders

  1. Discipline: Follow plan (no FOMO, no revenge trades)
  2. Patience: Wait for A-grade setups (quality > quantity)
  3. Risk management: Never risk > 2% (survive to compound)
  4. Adaptability: Adjust to regime changes (don't fight market)
  5. Continuous learning: Journal, review, improve (never stop)

The 90/90/90 Rule

90% of traders lose 90% of their capital in 90 days.

Why Most Fail:
1. Overleveraging (risk > 5%)
2. No plan (random entries)
3. No risk management (no stops)
4. Emotional trading (revenge, FOMO)
5. Insufficient capital (can't withstand drawdowns)

How to Join the 10%:
1. Risk 1-2% max per trade
2. Written trading plan (potential entry/potential exit rules)
3. Always use stops (never hope)
4. Journal trades (learn from mistakes)
5. Start with $10K+ (adequate capital)

  • Don't quit job until 12-24 months profitable + 12 months savings
  • Success = discipline + risk management + adaptability
