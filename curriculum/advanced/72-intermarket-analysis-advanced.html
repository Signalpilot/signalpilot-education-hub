Part 1: The Intermarket Causality Chain

How Assets Drive Each Other

Primary driver ‚Üí Secondary effect ‚Üí Equity impact

Example Chain: Fed cuts rates ‚Üí Dollar weakens ‚Üí Commodities rally ‚Üí Inflation expectations rise ‚Üí Growth stocks underperform (higher discount rate)

Intermarket causality chains allow you to predict equity moves before they happen. DXY is the leading indicator, equities are the lagging effect.

Key Causality Chains

Chain #1: Dollar ‚Üí Commodities ‚Üí Inflation ‚Üí Equities

Mechanism:

  • Dollar falls (DXY down) ‚Üí Commodities priced in USD rise (oil, copper, gold)
  • Commodity prices rise ‚Üí Input costs increase ‚Üí Inflation accelerates
  • Inflation rises ‚Üí Fed tightens ‚Üí Equity valuations compress

Leading indicator: DXY breaking support ‚Üí expect commodity rally in 2-4 weeks ‚Üí inflation data confirms in 1-2 months ‚Üí equity pressure follows

Trade: If DXY breaks down, rotate OUT of long-duration tech (ARKK, QQQ) INTO commodities (XLE, XLB)

Chain #2: Credit Spreads ‚Üí Risk Appetite ‚Üí Equities

Mechanism:

  • High-yield spreads (HYG vs TLT) widen ‚Üí Credit markets pricing in recession risk
  • Credit tightening ‚Üí Companies can't refinance debt cheaply ‚Üí Defaults rise
  • Risk-off ‚Üí Equities sell off (credit leads equities by 1-3 months)

Leading indicator: HYG/TLT ratio breaking down ‚Üí credit stress ‚Üí SPX follows lower within weeks

Trade: If credit spreads widening while SPX making new highs ‚Üí SELL SPX (divergence = warning)

Chain #3: Yields ‚Üí Growth vs Value ‚Üí Sector Rotation

Mechanism:

  • 10-year yield rises ‚Üí Discount rate for future earnings increases
  • Growth stocks (high PE, distant cash flows) hurt more than value (low PE, near-term cash flows)
  • Rotation: OUT of growth (QQQ, ARKK) INTO value (XLF, XLE, XLI)

Threshold: 10-year yield > 4% historically triggers growth-to-value rotation

Trade: If 10Y breaking 4%, short QQQ / long XLF (relative value trade)

Part 2: Advanced Correlation Analysis

Rolling Correlation (Dynamic Relationships)

Problem: Correlations are NOT static (SPX vs TLT correlation changes based on regime)

Regime SPX vs TLT Correlation Interpretation
Normal (2010-2019) -0.4 to -0.6 (inverse) Risk-on = stocks up, bonds down
Crisis (2008, 2020) -0.8 to -0.9 (strong inverse) Flight to safety extreme
QE Environment (2020-2021) +0.2 to +0.4 (POSITIVE!) Fed buying both ‚Üí both rise

Key insight: When SPX and TLT BOTH rise (positive correlation) = Fed easing (bullish). When BOTH fall (correlation still positive but negative direction) = forced liquidation (panic).

Lead-Lag Relationships

Concept: One asset leads, another follows (with time lag)

Example: Copper leads equities by 1-3 months

  • Copper rises ‚Üí Industrial demand strong ‚Üí Global growth accelerating
  • Equities rally 1-3 months later as earnings reflect stronger economy

Trade: If copper breaks out (new 6-month high) while SPX flat ‚Üí expect SPX to follow higher (buy early, before crowd notices)

Part 4: Commodity Complex Analysis

The Commodity Curve: Backwardation vs Contango

Contango (normal): Future prices > spot prices (storage costs priced in)

Backwardation (demand surge): Spot prices > future prices (immediate shortage)

Trading implication:

  • Oil in backwardation ‚Üí Supply shortage, demand strong ‚Üí Bullish for energy stocks (XLE)
  • Oil in contango ‚Üí Oversupply ‚Üí Bearish for energy (avoid or short)

Cross-Commodity Relationships

Gold vs Real Yields (Inverse Correlation)

Formula: Real Yield = 10Y Nominal Yield - Inflation Expectations

Relationship: Real yields UP ‚Üí Gold DOWN (opportunity cost of holding gold increases)

Real yields DOWN ‚Üí Gold UP (negative real yields = gold becomes attractive)

Trade: If real yields break below -1%, buy gold (GLD, miners GDXJ)

Copper/Gold Ratio (Growth Indicator)

Ratio rising: Copper outperforming gold ‚Üí Growth accelerating (copper = industrial demand, gold = safe haven)

Ratio falling: Gold outperforming copper ‚Üí Growth slowing, defensive rotation

Trade: Copper/gold ratio breaking out ‚Üí buy cyclicals (XLI, XLB), sell defensives (XLP, XLU)

EUR/USD as Risk Barometer

EUR/USD rising: Dollar weakness ‚Üí Commodities rally, EM outperforms, SPX benefits (weaker USD = multinational earnings boost)

EUR/USD falling: Dollar strength ‚Üí Commodities fall, EM underperforms, SPX headwinds

JPY as Safe Haven

USD/JPY falling (JPY strengthening): Risk-off, flight to safety ‚Üí SPX likely declining

USD/JPY rising (JPY weakening): Risk-on ‚Üí SPX likely rallying

Carry Trade Unwind Detection

Carry trade: Borrow in low-yield currency (JPY), invest in high-yield assets (equities, EM bonds)

Unwind signal: JPY suddenly strengthens + VIX spikes + EM currencies fall ‚Üí Massive liquidation (2008, Aug 2015, March 2020)

Action: Exit all risk assets immediately (carry unwinds are violent and fast)
