Advanced Auction Theory: Opening Range & Fair Value Gaps

Reading time ~16 min • Market Microstructure

Markets are continuous auctions searching for fair value. Openings reveal intent, gaps show imbalance. Learn to trade opening auctions, fair value gaps, and single prints for high-probability setups.

Market Profile Basics

The Auction Concept

Markets facilitate price discovery through auction.

Auction Process:
1. Price offered too high → No buyers → Price drops
2. Price offered too low → No sellers → Price rises
3. Fair value found → Maximum volume transacts (POC)

Market Profile: Visualizes where price spent time (volume at price)

Opening Auction Dynamics

The Opening Range (First 30-60 min)

Opening range establishes day's value area and often predicts direction.

Market open: 9:30 AM
Opening Range: High/Low of 9:30-10:00 AM period

Example:
9:30-10:00 AM: High $520.50, Low $519.20
Opening Range: $1.30

Significance:
- If price breaks above $520.50 → Bullish day (70% of time)
- If price breaks below $519.20 → Bearish day (70% of time)
- If price stays inside range → Range-bound day

Opening Type Classification

Type 1: Open-Drive (Directional Intent)

Market opens, immediately moves directional (no balance)

Example:
Open: $520.00
9:35 AM: $520.50 (no pullback)
9:45 AM: $521.20 (continued drive)
10:00 AM: $522.00 (strong momentum)

Interpretation: Strong institutional buying (trend day likely)
Strategy: Buy first pullback (don't chase, wait for dip)

Type 2: Open-Test-Drive (Probe then Move)

Market opens, tests opposite direction, then reverses

Example:
Open: $520.00
9:35 AM: $519.50 (quick dip, "test")
9:40 AM: $520.00 (back to open)
9:50 AM: $521.00 (drive up)

Interpretation: Tested for sellers (found none), buying takes over
Strategy: Long after test fails (reversal pattern)

Type 3: Open-Rejection-Reverse (False Direction)

Market opens strong one way, gets rejected, reverses hard

Example:
Open: $520.00
9:40 AM: $521.00 (rallies)
9:50 AM: $520.80 (stalls at resistance)
10:00 AM: $520.00 (back to open)
10:30 AM: $519.00 (reverses lower)

Interpretation: Early buyers trapped, stop cascade
Strategy: Fade the failed breakout (short below open)

Type 4: Open-Balance (Range Day)

Market opens, oscillates in narrow range

Example:
Open: $520.00
9:30-10:30 AM: Oscillates $519.50 - $520.50

Interpretation: Balance, no directional conviction
Strategy: Fade range extremes OR wait for breakout

Opening Auction Trading Rules

Rule 1: Wait 15-30 min (let opening volatility settle)

Rule 2: Identify opening type (Drive vs. Test vs. Balance)

Rule 3: Trade WITH opening direction IF:
- Open-Drive continues (buy pullback in uptrend)
- Open-Test-Drive confirms (long after test fails)

Rule 4: Trade AGAINST opening IF:
- Open-Rejection-Reverse (fade failed breakout)
- Open at extreme, rejected (fade back to value)

Rule 5: AVOID during Open-Balance (wait for breakout)

Fair Value Gaps (FVG)

What is a Fair Value Gap?

Price jumps without transacting (gap in candle wicks = imbalance).

3-Candle Pattern:

Candle 1: High $519.80
Candle 2: Low $520.20, High $521.50 (gaps up, no overlap)
Candle 3: Low $521.00

Fair Value Gap: $519.80 - $520.20 (unfilled gap)

Why it matters: Price skipped $519.80-$520.20 (imbalance)
Expected: Price returns to fill gap (balance auction)

FVG Trading Framework

Gaps act as magnets (price returns to fill imbalance).

Bullish FVG (Gap up):
- Price gaps from $519.80 to $520.20
- Continues rally to $522.00
- Pullback to gap ($520.00 area)
- Trade: Long at gap fill ($520.10), Stop $519.50, Target $522.50

Bearish FVG (Gap down):
- Price gaps from $521.00 to $520.60
- Continues drop to $519.00
- Bounces to gap ($520.80 area)
- Trade: Short at gap fill ($520.70), Stop $521.30, Target $518.50

FVG Fill Probability

Time Since FVGFill Probability
Same session70-80%
Within 24 hours85-90%
Within 3 days95%+

Implication: Most gaps fill. Trade the reversion.

Partial vs. Full Gap Fill

Gap: $519.80 - $520.20

Partial Fill: Price touches $520.15 (fills 75% of gap)
- Often sufficient (price may reverse here)

Full Fill: Price touches $519.85 (fills 100% of gap)
- Stronger confirmation (complete rebalancing)

Trading:
- Enter on 50% fill ($520.00), tight stop below full fill
- If rejects at 50% → Gap respected (strong)
- If fills 100% → Wait for acceptance beyond gap

Single Prints (Low Volume Nodes)

What are Single Prints?

Price traded briefly (one TPO period, ~30 min), low volume = imbalance.

Market Profile Example:
$521.00: ######### (9 TPO periods, high volume, POC)
$520.50: ########## (10 periods)
$520.00: ## (2 periods, SINGLE PRINT area)
$519.50: ######### (9 periods)

Interpretation: $520.00 = imbalance (quickly passed through)
Expected: Price avoids returning OR fills and breaks through

Single Print Trading

Single prints act as support/resistance.

Scenario 1: Single Print Support
Price drops from $521 → $520 (single print) → bounces to $521.50

Trade: Buy at $520.20 (above single print)
Logic: Single print respected = support confirmed

Scenario 2: Single Print Broken
Price drops from $521 → $520 → continues to $519

Trade: Short on break of $519.80 (single print failed)
Logic: Single print break = continuation, no support

Value Area Migration

Day-Over-Day Value Shifts

Value area shifts reveal institutional repositioning.

Day 1 Value Area: $519.00 - $521.00 (POC $520.00)
Day 2 Value Area: $520.50 - $522.50 (POC $521.50)

Migration: Value shifted UP by $1.50

Interpretation:
- Institutional buyers lifting offers
- New fair value = $521.50 (was $520.00)
- Bullish trend forming

Strategy: Buy pullbacks to old POC ($520.00, now support)

Value Area Relationship Trading

Price LocationInterpretationStrategy
Above VAHExpensive (above value)Fade or wait for acceptance
Inside Value AreaFair value (balanced)Range trade or wait
Below VALCheap (below value)Buy for reversion to POC
Example Trade: Value Area Reversion
VAH: $521.00, POC: $520.00, VAL: $519.00
Current Price: $518.50 (below VAL, "cheap")

Trade: Long $518.60
Target: $520.00 (POC, fair value)
Stop: $518.00 (below VAL, invalidation)
R:R: ($520 - $518.60) / ($518.60 - $518) = 2.3R

Overnight Inventory & Gap Rules

Gap Types

Gap Up (Open > Previous Close)

Previous Close: $520.00
Open: $521.00 (gap up $1.00)

Scenarios:
1. Gap and Go: Continues higher (strong demand)
   → Trade: Buy first pullback to gap fill ($520.80)

2. Gap Fill: Returns to $520.00 (neutral demand)
   → Trade: Long at fill IF holds, Short if breaks through

3. Gap Reversal: Fills and continues lower (weak)
   → Trade: Short below previous close ($519.80)

Gap Down (Open < Previous Close)

Previous Close: $520.00
Open: $519.00 (gap down $1.00)

Scenarios:
1. Gap and Go: Continues lower (strong selling)
   → Trade: Short first bounce to gap fill ($519.20)

2. Gap Fill: Returns to $520.00 (neutral)
   → Trade: Short at fill IF rejects, Long if breaks higher

3. Gap Reversal: Fills and continues higher (strong buying)
   → Trade: Long above previous close ($520.20)

Gap Fill Statistics

Gap Size < 0.5%: 90% fill same day
Gap Size 0.5-1%: 75% fill same day
Gap Size > 1%: 50% fill same day (often takes days/weeks)

Trading Rule:
Small gaps (<0.5%) = Trade fill aggressively
Large gaps (>1%) = Wait for acceptance, don't assume fill

Complete Opening Auction Framework

Pre-Market Checklist

  • [ ] Previous day Value Area (VAH, POC, VAL)
  • [ ] Overnight range (high/low during off-hours)
  • [ ] Gap size and direction (vs. previous close)
  • [ ] Pre-market volume (high = strong intent)

Opening 30-Min Process

  • [ ] Observe opening type (Drive, Test, Rejection, Balance)
  • [ ] Mark opening range high/low
  • [ ] Identify any FVGs or single prints
  • [ ] Wait for first meaningful setup (don't force)

Trade Execution Rules

IF Open-Drive + Minimal Flow Trending:
→ Buy first pullback to OR low or VWAP

IF Open-Test-Drive + failed test:
→ Long on reclaim above test low

IF Open-Rejection-Reverse:
→ Short below opening range low (breakdown)

IF Open-Balance:
→ Wait for OR breakout, trade direction of break

IF FVG within OR:
→ Trade reversion to gap fill

IF price below VAL:
→ Long for reversion to POC (2-3R setup)

Key Takeaways

  • Opening range (first 30-60 min) sets tone for day
  • Fair Value Gaps fill 90%+ of the time (trade reversion)
  • Single prints = imbalance (act as support/resistance)
  • Value Area: Above VAH = expensive, below VAL = cheap
  • POC = fair value (price reverts to POC)
