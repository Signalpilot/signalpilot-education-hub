Part 1: Displacement‚ÄîReading Institutional Urgency

Not All Candles Are Created Equal

Displacement is when price moves aggressively in one direction, leaving gaps (Fair Value Gaps) and showing institutional conviction.

Key characteristics of true displacement:

  • Multiple consecutive candles in same direction (3-7+ candles)
  • Large candle bodies with small wicks (conviction, not indecision)
  • Fair Value Gaps created (price skipped zones due to speed)
  • High volume on Plutus Flow (institutional participation confirmed)

Fake Momentum (Retail Chasing)

Pattern: One or two large green candles after a period of consolidation

Volume: Low to moderate (retail FOMO, not smart money)

Follow-through: Price stalls, reverses, or chops sideways

Why it fails: No institutional conviction. Retail bought the move, but institutions aren't participating. Without smart money, there's no fuel for continuation.

Common trap: Retail buys the second or third green candle, gets trapped at the top, then watches price reverse.

True Displacement (Smart Money Flow)

Pattern: 5-7+ consecutive candles with large bodies and minimal wicks

Volume: High and sustained (Plutus Flow shows institutional accumulation/distribution)

Follow-through: After retracement (mitigation), price continues in displacement direction

Why it works: Institutions don't move like this unless they're positioning for a major move. They have information (fundamentals, order flow, positioning data) that retail doesn't.

Pro insight: When you see displacement, don't chase it. Wait for the retracement to the mitigation block, THEN enter with institutions.

Types of Displacement

Bullish Displacement (Institutional Buying)

Characteristics:

  • 5+ consecutive bullish candles with large bodies
  • Price moves from $100 ‚Üí $105 with minimal pullbacks
  • Multiple Fair Value Gaps created (skipped price levels)
  • High volume throughout the move

What it means: Institutions are aggressively accumulating. They're not nibbling‚Äîthey're loading the truck. This urgency suggests they expect significant upside.

What happens next:

  1. Price retraces 30-60% of the displacement (mitigation)
  2. Returns to a Fair Value Gap or mitigation block
  3. Institutions add to positions (more buying)
  4. Price resumes upward, often breaking recent highs
Bearish Displacement (Institutional Selling)

Characteristics:

  • 5+ consecutive bearish candles with large bodies
  • Price moves from $200 ‚Üí $190 rapidly
  • Creates bearish Fair Value Gaps (imbalances)
  • High selling volume (institutions distributing)

What it means: Institutions are dumping positions. This isn't profit-taking‚Äîit's full distribution. They see something bearish coming.

What happens next:

  1. Price retraces 30-60% upward (mitigation rally)
  2. Returns to a bearish FVG or mitigation block
  3. Institutions add shorts (more selling)
  4. Price continues lower, often breaking recent lows

üí° The Critical Insight

Displacement = Direction. When institutions move with urgency, they're telling you where price is GOING. Don't fight it. Wait for the retracement, then join them.

Part 2: Mitigation Blocks‚ÄîInstitutional Rebalancing

Where Institutions Add to Winners

After displacement, price doesn't just continue forever. It retraces. And that retracement to a specific zone is called mitigation.

Mitigation block: The zone where displacement originated, often containing a Fair Value Gap. This is where institutions RE-ENTER to add to their position before the next leg.

Mitigation vs Order Blocks (The Difference)

Order Block

Definition: Last opposite-colored candle BEFORE displacement

Example (Bullish): Last red candle before 7 green candles (displacement)

Purpose: Initial entry zone‚Äîwhere institutions first entered

When to trade: First retracement after BOS or ChoCH

Mitigation Block

Definition: Zone where displacement STARTED, often contains unfilled FVG

Example (Bullish): The $100-$101 zone where the 7-candle rally began

Purpose: Rebalancing zone‚Äîwhere institutions add to existing positions

When to trade: After displacement, during retracement to the origin

Simple rule: Order blocks are for INITIAL entries. Mitigation blocks are for ADDING to winners.

How to Identify Mitigation Blocks

Step 1: Identify Displacement

Look for 5-7+ consecutive candles moving in one direction with:

  • Large candle bodies (conviction)
  • Fair Value Gaps (imbalances left behind)
  • High volume on Plutus Flow

Example: Price displaces from $100 ‚Üí $107 in 6 candles

Step 2: Mark the Origin Zone

The mitigation block is where the displacement STARTED:

  • Identify the first candle of the displacement move
  • Mark from its low to its high (bullish) or high to low (bearish)
  • This zone often contains a Fair Value Gap

Example: Displacement started at candle with low=$100.00, high=$101.50. Mitigation block = $100.00-$101.50

Step 3: Wait for Retracement

Price will retrace 30-60% of the displacement move:

  • Shallow retracement: 30-40% (strong trend, institutions very bullish)
  • Standard retracement: 50% (typical institutional rebalancing)
  • Deep retracement: 60-70% (may reach mitigation block or deeper FVG)

Example: Displacement: $100 ‚Üí $107 (7 points). 50% retracement = $103.50. Mitigation block at $100-$101.50 is deeper, but higher probability.

Step 4: Enter at Mitigation + FVG Confluence

Ideal potential entry setup:

  • ‚úì Price retraces into mitigation block
  • ‚úì Mitigation block contains unfilled Fair Value Gap
  • ‚úì Janus Atlas confirms the zone
  • ‚úì Plutus Flow shows absorption (buying), not exhaustion
  • ‚úì Higher timeframe supports direction (daily uptrend for bullish mitigation)

Entry: Limit order within mitigation block. Stop: Below mitigation block low. Target: Recent high + 1.5-2R.

Part 3: Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)‚ÄîThe Sweet Spot

The 61.8%-78.6% Retracement Zone

Institutions don't just enter anywhere. They target specific Fibonacci levels for retracements: 61.8% (golden ratio) to 78.6%.

This is called the Optimal Trade Entry (OTE) zone. It's where the probability is highest that institutions will defend their position and add more.

Why OTE Works

Mathematical basis: 61.8% and 78.6% are derived from the Fibonacci sequence‚Äîratios that appear throughout nature, markets, and institutional algo trading.

Institutional behavior: Algos are programmed to rebalance at these levels. When price hits 61.8%-78.6% retracement after displacement, institutional buy/sell algorithms trigger.

  • 78.6% retracement: $110 - (10 √ó 0.786) = $102.14

Don't blindly buy the OTE zone. Wait for confirmation:

  • Janus Atlas: Marks the OTE/mitigation zone as high-probability
  • Plutus Flow: Shows absorption (buying volume increasing as price enters OTE)
  • Price action: Bullish engulfing or hammer candle within OTE zone
