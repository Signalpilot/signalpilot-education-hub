Swing Trading Framework: Multi-Day Structure

16-19 min read ‚Ä¢ Position Trading

Intraday noise vanishes on the daily chart. Swing trading is where technical analysis actually works.

Day trading fights HFT algos and market makers in their domain. Swing trading operates on the timeframe where fundamentals meet technicals‚Äîwhere institutional positioning takes days to build and unwind.

üö® Real Talk

Swing trading isn't slower day trading. It's a different game. Daily order blocks, weekly structure, and position sizing changes. Overnight risk matters. But the edge? Massive. You're trading with institutions, not against them.

In this lesson:

  • Daily timeframe structure (where swing traders live)
  • Multi-day order blocks and mitigation zones
  • Position sizing for overnight holds (risk management differences)
  • Swing trade management (entries, scaling, exits)

Part 1: Daily Timeframe Structure

Why daily charts? They filter out intraday noise while capturing institutional flows that take 3-10 days to complete.

Market structure on daily:

  • Higher highs, higher lows = Daily uptrend (swing long bias)
  • Lower highs, lower lows = Daily downtrend (swing short bias)
  • BOS on daily = Major trend continuation signal
  • ChoCH on daily = Trend reversal (huge move incoming)

Daily Order Blocks

What's different from intraday? Daily order blocks represent institutional positioning over multiple days, not hours. They're more reliable but require patience.

Example:

  • Daily chart shows bullish displacement from $100 ‚Üí $115 over 5 days
  • Daily order block: $100-$102 (origin of displacement)
  • Price retraces to $103 over 2-3 days (mitigation)
  • Enter swing long at $102, stop at $98, target $120
  • Hold time: 5-15 days

Part 2: Position Sizing for Swing Trades

Key difference: Overnight risk requires smaller position sizes than day trading.

Day trading: 1-2% risk per trade (close before market close)

Swing trading: 0.5-1% risk per trade (overnight gaps, news risk)

Why smaller?

  • Gap risk (earnings, news, geopolitical events)
  • Holding through sessions (different volatility)
  • Larger stop distances (daily ATR > intraday ATR)

Stop Placement for Swing Trades

Use structure, not arbitrary %:

  • Below daily order block low (for longs)
  • Below weekly swing low (for major positions)
  • Below key daily support (previous week low, monthly open)

ATR-based: 1.5-2x daily ATR beyond entry (gives room for volatility)

Part 3: Swing Trade Management

Entry Strategies

Strategy 1: Daily order block retest

  • Wait for daily displacement
  • Mark order block
  • Enter on retracement to order block (may take 3-5 days)

Strategy 2: Weekly structure break

  • Weekly BOS occurs (breaks previous week high)
  • Enter on daily pullback to weekly order block
  • Target: Next weekly high or +20% move

Scaling & Exits

Scaling in:

  • 50% position at order block entry
  • 25% at OTE zone (if price reaches 70% retracement)
  • 25% at deep retracement (if price tests 78.6%)

Scaling out:

  • 33% at 1.5R (recent swing high)
  • 33% at 3R (major structure level)
  • 33% trail with daily ATR stop

Part 4: Overnight Risk Management

Gap risk mitigation:

  • Avoid holding through earnings (unless that's the thesis)
  • Check economic calendar (FOMC, CPI, NFP)
  • Reduce size before major news
  • Use options to hedge (protective puts for long positions)

Weekend holds:

  • Reduce position size by 30-50% before Friday close if concerned about weekend news
  • Only hold full size if weekly structure strongly supports direction

Key Takeaways

  • Daily timeframe filters noise and captures institutional multi-day flows
  • Position size smaller for swing trades (0.5-1% vs 1-2% for day trades)
  • Daily order blocks more reliable than intraday (institutions positioning)
  • Overnight risk requires wider stops, smaller size, news awareness
