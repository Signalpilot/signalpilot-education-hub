Footprint Charts Advanced: Reading Institutional Intent

17-20 min read ‚Ä¢ Order Flow Mastery

Candlesticks show you WHAT happened. Footprint charts show you WHO made it happen.

Every price level has two sides: buyers and sellers. Footprint charts show you the exact volume traded at each price, broken down by aggressor side. When you see 5,000 contracts bought at $100.00 but only 500 sold, that's institutional absorption‚Äîsmart money accumulating.

üö® Real Talk

Footprint charts are the closest thing retail gets to seeing institutional order flow. Stacked imbalances reveal absorption. POC migration shows repositioning. Divergence screams trap. This is next-level market reading.

In this lesson:

  • Reading footprint charts (bid vs ask volume at each price)
  • Stacked imbalances and absorption vs exhaustion
  • POC (Point of Control) migration patterns
  • Footprint divergence with price (institutional vs retail disagreement)

Part 1: Footprint Chart Basics

What is a footprint chart? Each candle is split into price levels, showing bid and ask volume traded at that specific price.

Example footprint candle:

Price    Ask    Bid    Delta
$100.50  1200 | 300  = +900  (buying aggression)
$100.25  2500 | 400  = +2100 (STRONG buying)
$100.00  800  | 3200 = -2400 (selling into support)
$99.75   500  | 1800 = -1300 (selling pressure)

Reading this:

  • At $100.25: 2,500 ask volume (buyers hitting ask) vs 400 bid = institutions BUYING
  • At $100.00: 800 ask vs 3,200 bid = institutions SELLING into that "support"
  • Delta shows net aggressor volume (positive = more buying, negative = more selling)

Part 2: Stacked Imbalances

Imbalance: When one side overwhelms the other (e.g., 3,000 ask vs 200 bid)

Stacked imbalance: Multiple consecutive price levels showing same-sided imbalances

Example (Bullish):

$101.00  2800 | 300   Imbalance ‚úì
$100.75  3200 | 450   Imbalance ‚úì
$100.50  2900 | 380   Imbalance ‚úì
$100.25  2100 | 500   Imbalance ‚úì

Translation: Institutions absorbing ALL selling across 4 price levels. This is accumulation. Expect continuation higher.

Absorption vs Exhaustion

Absorption (bullish): High volume at bid side gets absorbed by even HIGHER volume on ask side. Price holds or moves up.

Exhaustion (bearish): High volume on ask side, but NO bid absorption. Price drops.

Part 3: POC Migration

POC (Point of Control): Price level with highest volume in the candle/session.

POC Migration: As price moves, the POC shifts. Direction of shift reveals institutional intent.

Bullish POC migration: POC moves UP as candles progress (institutions accumulating higher)

Bearish POC migration: POC moves DOWN (institutions distributing lower)

Part 4: Footprint Divergence

Setup: Price makes new high, but footprint shows LESS buying volume than previous high

What it means: Retail pushing price up with small orders. Institutions NOT participating. Trap incoming.

How to trade it:

  • Price new high: $105.00
  • Footprint: Only 800 contracts vs 2,500 at previous high ($104.50)
  • Institutions absent = reversal likely
  • Enter short on breakdown below $104.50

Key Takeaways

  • Footprint charts show bid/ask volume at each price level
  • Stacked imbalances = institutional absorption or exhaustion
  • POC migration reveals where institutions are repositioning
  • Footprint divergence = retail vs institutional disagreement (fade retail)
