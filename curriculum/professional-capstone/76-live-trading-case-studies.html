Case Study #1: Breakout Trade (SPY, March 2023)

Setup: Daily consolidation at resistance ($400) for 15 days, ascending triangle forming

Confirmation signals:

  • Volume declining during consolidation (coiling)
  • 4-hour higher lows (buying pressure building)
  • Pentarch Pilot Line showing institutional accumulation
  • No major resistance above until $410

Entry: 15-min breakout above $400.50 with volume surge (2× average)

Position sizing: Risk $1 (stop at $399.50), target $405 (5:1 R/R)

Management: Took 50% at $404 (+3.5R), trailed stop to $402 on remainder

Result: +4.2R average (50% at 3.5R, 50% at 5R)

Case Study #2: Mean Reversion (AAPL, Earnings Overreaction)

Setup: AAPL drops 8% on earnings miss (overreaction)

Analysis:

  • Revenue beat, only EPS miss by 2 cents (not material)
  • Dropped to weekly 200 EMA support ($145)
  • Put/call ratio spiked to 1.8 (panic)
  • Minimal Flow showing 50K+ share buys at $145 (institutions buying dip)

Entry: Buy at $146 (after 1-hour bullish engulfing at weekly support)

Risk: Stop $143 (below weekly 200 EMA), target $152 (gap fill)

Result: +2R in 3 days (closed $152.50)

Case Study #3: Failed Trade (Learning from Losses)

Setup: TSLA breakout above $250 resistance

Entry: $252 on 5-min chart (MISTAKE: didn't wait for hourly confirmation)

What went wrong:

  • Lower timeframe breakout, but hourly still below resistance (false signal)
  • No institutional flow confirmation (Pilot Line neutral)
  • Entered on FOMO, not systematic criteria

Result: -1R loss (stopped at $250)

Lesson: Multi-timeframe confluence is non-negotiable. 5-min breakouts fail 70%+ without hourly/daily confirmation.

Case Study Lessons

  • Winning trades: Multiple signals aligned (price action + volume + order flow + timeframe confluence)
  • Position management: Take partial profits at 2-3R, trail remainder
  • Losing trades: Usually violated multi-timeframe rule or entered emotionally
  • Keep detailed journal: Every trade is a case study for improvement
