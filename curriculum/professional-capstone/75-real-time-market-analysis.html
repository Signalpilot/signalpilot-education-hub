Tyler's 7-Step Real-Time Synthesis System

After losing $53K, Tyler rebuilt with a simplified real-time execution system designed for SPEED + ACCURACY (not exhaustive analysis):

Step 1: Pre-Market 3-Minute Scan (Not 30 Minutes)

Rule: 9:27-9:30 AM, check ONLY these 4 things (3 mins max): (1) Futures overnight: /ES and /NQ up or down? (30 sec), (2) Major catalysts: Any Fed speech, CPI, NFP, or mega-cap earnings TODAY? (60 sec), (3) Intermarkets: SPY, TLT, GLD aligned (risk-on/off) or diverging? (60 sec), (4) Key levels: Yesterday's high/low, overnight range, VWAP (30 sec). That's it. No reading analyst reports. No checking 20 technical indicators. No reviewing historical patterns. Just 4 things in 3 minutes.

Why this works: These 4 inputs tell you the day's MACRO context (bullish/bearish bias, major catalysts, institutional positioning). Everything else is noise that delays execution. Tyler's old system checked 15+ things for 20-30 minutes. New system: 4 things in 3 minutes. Result: ready to trade at 9:30, not 9:45.

Step 2: Opening 30 Minutes‚Äî3-Signal Confirmation (90 Seconds Max)

Rule: When a setup appears (gap, potential breakout, potential breakdown), check ONLY these 3 signals in 90 seconds: (1) Price action (gap holding/filling? potential breakout on volume?), (2) Order flow (Pentarch Pilot Line: cumulative delta positive or negative?), (3) Volume (3√ó average for that time? Enter at $523 by 9:33 AM. Old Tyler would've spent 33 minutes reading analyst reports. New Tyler: 3 minutes, entered, captured the move.

Why this works: These 3 signals are LEADING (order flow, volume) and CONFIRMING (price action). They predict the next 10-30 minutes. Charts, RSI, MACD are LAGGING‚Äîthey describe the last 10-30 minutes (useless for real-time). 3 signals = enough for 70-80% accuracy, fast enough to catch the move.

Step 3: No Research During Live Market (Save for Post-Market)

Rule: NO reading analyst reports, earnings transcripts, news articles, or checking historical patterns while the market is open. If he needs that info, trade is SKIPPED. Research happens BEFORE 9:30 AM or AFTER 4 PM, never during live hours.

Why this works: Tyler's Oct 16 mistake was reading analyst reports from 9:25-9:58 AM while NVDA was moving. That research didn't change the trade thesis (earnings beat = bullish was obvious at 9:25 AM). It just delayed potential entry 33 minutes. Research is for thesis development (before market) or review (after market), not real-time execution.

Step 4: 2-Minute Entry Rule (From Signal to Execution)

Rule: From the moment a setup appears to potential entry execution must be ‚â§ 2 minutes. If he can't decide in 2 minutes, the signal isn't clear enough ‚Üí SKIP. This forces rapid synthesis, prevents analysis paralysis.

How it works: 9:30 AM signal appears ‚Üí 90 sec to check 3 signals ‚Üí 30 sec to calculate position size + place order = 2 min total. Compare to old Tyler: 20-30 min analysis ‚Üí entered at TOP of move ‚Üí stopped out. New Tyler: 2 min synthesis ‚Üí entered at START of move ‚Üí captured meat of the move.

Why this works: Intraday moves last 10-45 minutes on average. If you take 20+ minutes to "confirm," you've missed 50-80% of the move. 2-minute rule ensures you catch the BEGINNING, not the END.

Step 5: Order Flow Overrides Chart Patterns

Rule: If order flow (cumulative delta, tape prints) disagrees with chart pattern (potential breakout, support/resistance), TRUST THE FLOW. Order flow is institutional intent. Charts are retail pattern recognition. Institutions move markets, not retail.

Example (from Nov 7 loss): SPY at $452, chart showed bullish setup (above MAs, RSI 58). Tyler entered long. But order flow showed -8M cumulative delta (institutions selling). SPY dropped to $449.50 in 15 minutes. Chart said buy. Flow said sell. Flow won. New rule: if flow disagrees with chart, SKIP the trade (or trade WITH the flow, not against it).

Why this works: Charts show what retail sees (everyone has TradingView). Order flow shows what institutions DO (privileged info from tape, delta, block prints). Institutions move price. Trade with them, not against them.

Step 6: No FOMO Entries (Checklist Must Be Completed)

Rule: Even with 2-minute rule, must COMPLETE the 3-signal check before potential entry. No skipping steps to "catch the move." If the move happens before the 2-minute check is done, it's MISSED ‚Üí move on to next setup.

Why this works: Tyler's Dec disaster came from swinging to opposite extreme: zero analysis, pure FOMO. Part 1: The 5-Minute Pre-Market Scan

What Professionals Check Before the Open

Between 9:25-9:30 AM, professional traders run a systematic scan to understand the macro backdrop and institutional positioning BEFORE the first trade.

The Pre-Market Checklist

Macro Context (60 seconds)

Overnight Futures:

  • /ES (S&P 500 futures): Up or down from yesterday's close?
  • /NQ (Nasdaq futures): Leading or lagging /ES?
  • /RTY (Russell 2000): Risk-on (outperforming) or risk-off (underperforming)?

Example:

  • /ES: +0.8% (bullish)
  • /NQ: +1.2% (outperforming = tech strength)
  • /RTY: +0.3% (underperforming = defensive bias)
  • Read: Bullish, but selective (large cap tech leading, small caps lagging)

Global Markets:

  • Asia: Nikkei, Hang Seng (did they rally or selloff overnight?)
  • Europe: DAX, FTSE (are they confirming or diverging from US futures?)

Key Indicators:

  • DXY (Dollar Index): Rising dollar = bearish for stocks/commodities
  • 10-Year Yield: Rising yields = pressure on growth stocks
  • VIX: Above 20 = elevated fear, below 15 = complacency

Catalysts & News (90 seconds)

Economic Calendar:

  • Any Fed speakers today? (Check federalreserve.gov)
  • Major data releases? (CPI, NFP, GDP, PMI)
  • FOMC meeting this week?

Earnings:

  • Which major companies reported overnight or pre-market?
  • Any mega-cap earnings today (AAPL, MSFT, AMZN, NVDA, GOOGL)?
  • Sector trends (e.g., multiple banks beating = financials strength)

Sector News:

  • Any sector-specific catalysts? (Oil price spike, chip shortage update, etc.)
  • Geopolitical events? (War, sanctions, trade deals)

üí° Pro Tip: News Hierarchy

Tier 1 (Market Moving): Fed policy, CPI, NFP, major geopolitical events

Tier 2 (Sector Moving): Mega-cap earnings, sector-specific data

Tier 3 (Noise): Random analyst upgrades/downgrades, individual stock news

Focus on Tier 1 first. Ignore Tier 3 unless trading that specific stock.

Intermarket Alignment (60 seconds)

The Big Three: SPY, TLT, GLD

Risk-On Pattern:

  • SPY: ‚Üë (stocks rallying)
  • TLT: ‚Üì (bonds selling off, yields rising)
  • GLD: ‚Üì (gold selling off, risk appetite high)

Risk-Off Pattern:

  • SPY: ‚Üì (stocks selling off)
  • TLT: ‚Üë (bonds rallying, flight to safety)
  • GLD: ‚Üë (gold rallying, safe haven bid)

Divergence (Dangerous):

  • SPY: ‚Üë but TLT: ‚Üë and GLD: ‚Üë (stocks rallying but bonds/gold also rallying = confusion, likely reversal soon)

‚ö†Ô∏è When Intermarkets Diverge, Fade the Move

Example: S&P futures +1%, but bonds and gold also rallying.

Read: Fake rally. Institutions are hedging (buying bonds/gold) while retail chases stocks. Likely reversal within hours.

Trade: Don't buy the rally. Wait for confirmation or short on first sign of weakness.

Key Levels (90 seconds)

Yesterday's High/Low:

  • Mark these levels on your chart
  • If we gap above yesterday's high ‚Üí bullish continuation likely
  • If we gap below yesterday's low ‚Üí bearish continuation likely

Overnight Range:

  • High and low from overnight futures session (6 PM - 9:30 AM)
  • Breakout above overnight high = bullish
  • Breakout below overnight low = bearish

VWAP:

  • Volume-Weighted Average Price (resets daily at 9:30 AM)
  • Price above VWAP = bullish, below = bearish
  • VWAP acts as magnet (price tends to revert to it)

Major Support/Resistance:

  • Round numbers (SPY 500, 450, 400)
  • Previous week's high/low
  • 50-day and 200-day moving averages
Part 2: Opening 30 Minutes ‚Äî Reading Institutional Intent

9:30-10:00 AM: The Most Important 30 Minutes

üî¥ CHECKPOINT (5 minutes)

You now understand the core concepts.

Take a 30-second breath before continuing...

The opening 30 minutes reveal institutional intent. Are they accumulating (buying dips) or distributing (selling rips)?

The Opening Auction (9:28-9:30 AM)

9:28 AM: NYSE publishes the Opening Auction Imbalance

  • Buy imbalance > $500M: Institutions want to buy ‚Üí bullish open likely
  • Sell imbalance > $500M: Institutions want to sell ‚Üí bearish open likely
  • Imbalance < $200M: Balanced, no strong institutional bias

How to use it:

  • Large buy imbalance + gap up = strong continuation (don't fade)
  • Large sell imbalance + gap down = strong continuation (don't buy dip)
  • Large buy imbalance but gap down = reversal setup (institutions buying weakness)

Gap Behavior (First 15 Minutes)

Gap Up ‚Üí Immediate Fill = Fade the Rally

Pattern:

  1. Market gaps up +0.5% at 9:30 AM
  2. Within first 15 minutes, price falls back to yesterday's close (gap fills)
  3. Volume on the selloff is higher than volume on the rally

Read: Retail chased the gap up. Institutions sold into strength. Bearish reversal.

Trade:

  • Short once gap is 50% filled (or buy puts)
  • Target: Yesterday's low
  • Stop: Above the 9:30 AM high

‚ö†Ô∏è Real Example: Feb 2, 2024 (NFP Day)

Setup: Strong NFP report ‚Üí SPY gaps up +0.8% at 9:30 AM

9:35 AM: SPY starts selling off, gap 50% filled by 9:45 AM

9:50 AM: Gap fully filled, SPY back at yesterday's close

10:15 AM: SPY breaks yesterday's low, down -1.2% on the day

Lesson: When retail buys the gap and institutions fade it, the reversal is violent.

Gap Up ‚Üí Extension = Trend Continuation

Pattern:

  1. Market gaps up +0.5% at 9:30 AM
  2. Price holds above the gap (doesn't fill)
  3. By 9:45 AM, price breaks above 9:30 AM high on volume

Read: Institutions are buying. Retail FOMO hasn't even started yet. Bullish continuation.

Trade:

  • Buy breakout above 9:30 AM high (or buy calls)
  • Target: Next resistance level (round number, previous week high, etc.)
  • Stop: Below VWAP or yesterday's close

üí° Real Example: Jan 12, 2024 (CPI Day)

Setup: Lower-than-expected CPI ‚Üí SPY gaps up +1.2% at 9:30 AM

9:35 AM: SPY holds above gap, consolidates

9:50 AM: SPY breaks above 9:30 AM high (+1.5% on day)

11:00 AM: SPY +2.3% (rally extends all morning)

Lesson: When institutions confirm the gap with buying, the trend is strong.

Rule: The first 30-minute bar should have 3-5x average volume.

Why: High volume = institutional participation. Low volume = retail-only (unreliable).

Example:

  • SPY average 30-min volume: 20 million shares
  • Today's 9:30-10:00 AM volume: 80 million shares (4x average)
  • Read: Institutions are active. Trust the direction.
Part 3: Real-Time Order Flow Synthesis

Combining Tape + Flow + Price Action

This is where Signal Pilot's tools (Minimal Flow, Pentarch Pilot Line) become critical. You're synthesizing three data streams in real-time:

  1. Tape (Time & Sales): Large prints, aggressive buying/selling
  2. Order Flow (Cumulative Delta): Institutional accumulation/distribution
  3. Price Action: Breakouts, breakdowns, VWAP reactions

The 3-Signal Confirmation Framework

Only trade when ALL THREE signals align:

Bullish Setup (All 3 Signals Confirm)

Signal #1: Tape (Minimal Flow)

  • Large buy prints (10K+ shares at ask)
  • Aggressive buying (market orders, not limit orders)
  • Example: 50K share buy at $500.05 (lifting the offer)

Signal #2: Order Flow (Pentarch Pilot Line)

  • Cumulative delta trending UP
  • More buy volume than sell volume over last 15-30 minutes
  • Example: +5 million cumulative delta (institutions accumulating)

Signal #3: Price Action

  • Price breaks above resistance (overnight high, VWAP, previous day high)
  • Breakout on volume (3x average)
  • Example: SPY breaks $500 on 100M volume

Trade:

  • Entry: Buy potential breakout (or buy calls)
  • Target: Next resistance (+1-2%)
  • Stop: Below potential breakout level or VWAP
  • Win Rate: ~70% when all 3 signals confirm

Bearish Setup (All 3 Signals Confirm)

Signal #1: Tape (Minimal Flow)

  • Large sell prints (10K+ shares at bid)
  • Aggressive selling (market orders hitting bids)
  • Example: 75K share sell at $499.95 (hitting the bid)

Signal #2: Order Flow (Pentarch Pilot Line)

  • Cumulative delta trending DOWN
  • More sell volume than buy volume over last 15-30 minutes
  • Example: -7 million cumulative delta (institutions distributing)

Signal #3: Price Action

  • Price breaks below support (overnight low, VWAP, previous day low)
  • Breakdown on volume (3x average)
  • Example: SPY breaks $495 on 120M volume

Trade:

  • Entry: Short potential breakdown (or buy puts)
  • Target: Next support (-1-2%)
  • Stop: Above potential breakdown level or VWAP
  • Win Rate: ~70% when all 3 signals confirm

‚ö†Ô∏è What If Only 1 or 2 Signals Confirm?

Don't trade. Wait for full alignment.

Example: Price breaks out, but order flow is negative ‚Üí fake potential breakout, likely reversal.

Example: Order flow is bullish, but price can't break resistance ‚Üí distribution, likely selloff.

Patience = Edge. Pros wait for high-probability setups.

Part 4: Intraday Session Pivots

The 4 Key Decision Points

üü¢ CHECKPOINT (15 minutes)

Almost done! You've mastered the complete framework.

Final stretch - you're doing great...

Professional traders know that market behavior changes at specific times each day. Here's how to trade each session:

10:00 AM Pivot ‚Äî The First Institutional Wave Completes

What Happens:

  • Opening range (9:30-10:00 AM) establishes the day's bias
  • Institutions who wanted to position have done so
  • Market either continues the trend or reverses

How to Trade:

  • If 9:30-10:00 AM was strong trending: Look for pullback to VWAP, then continuation
  • If 9:30-10:00 AM was choppy/weak: Likely range-bound until 2 PM, avoid or scalp only

12:00-2:00 PM ‚Äî Lunch Doldrums

What Happens:

  • Volume drops 40-60%
  • Institutional traders at lunch, only algos active
  • Price tends to chop in a narrow range

How to Trade:

  • Best strategy: Don't trade. Take a break.
  • If you must trade: Scalp the range (buy support, sell resistance, tight stops)
  • Avoid: Directional bets during lunch (low probability)

2:00-3:00 PM ‚Äî Late-Day Momentum

What Happens:

  • Institutional traders return from lunch
  • Volume picks up again
  • Market either resumes morning trend or reverses

How to Trade:

  • If morning was bullish + 2 PM confirms: Buy continuation to 4 PM
  • If morning was bullish but 2 PM shows weakness: Fade the rally (institutions distributing)

3:50-4:00 PM ‚Äî MOC Imbalance Trading

What Happens:

  • NYSE publishes Market-On-Close (MOC) order imbalance at 3:50 PM
  • Institutions rebalance portfolios at the close
  • Large imbalances create predictable price moves in final 10 minutes

How to Trade:

  • Buy imbalance > $500M: Buy at 3:50 PM, sell at 3:59 PM (ride the buying pressure)
  • Sell imbalance > $500M: Short at 3:50 PM, cover at 3:59 PM (ride the selling pressure)
  • See Lesson 45 for full MOC imbalance strategies

Correct! When stocks rally but bonds AND gold also rally, it's a fake rally. Institutions are hedging (buying safe havens) while retail chases stocks. Fade the move.

3. It's 2:15 PM. Morning was bullish (+1.5% by 10 AM). Now SPY is testing VWAP from above. Order flow just turned negative. What do you do?

B) Short if SPY breaks below VWAP ‚Äî late-day reversal setup
C) Close all positions ‚Äî too risky

Correct! Morning trend + negative afternoon flow = distribution. Institutions sold the rally. If VWAP breaks, it's a late-day reversal (target: overnight low or yesterday's close).
