Multi-Timeframe Mastery: HTF Guides, LTF Executes

Reading time ~15 min • Multi-Timeframe Analysis

Trading on one timeframe = gambling. Professional multi-timeframe alignment = edge. Learn the 3-timeframe rule, how to avoid conflicting signals, and complete frameworks for HTF/MTF/LTF integration with Signal Pilot.

The 3-Timeframe Rule

Framework: Higher guides, Medium confirms, Lower executes.

HTF (Higher Timeframe): Trend & Context

  • Timeframe: Daily or Weekly
  • Purpose: Identify trend, major support/resistance, market regime
  • Question: "Are we in uptrend, downtrend, or range?"
  • Signal Pilot: Minimal Flow regime (trending/ranging/volatile)

MTF (Medium Timeframe): Structure

  • Timeframe: 4H or 1H
  • Purpose: Swing structure, sweep zones, entry areas
  • Question: "Where are the key levels?"
  • Signal Pilot: Janus Atlas sweeps, Plutus Flow POC

LTF (Lower Timeframe): Execution

  • Timeframe: 15min or 5min
  • Purpose: Precise entry timing, confirmation
  • Question: "What's the exact entry price?"
  • Signal Pilot: Real-time order flow, footprint confirmation

RULE: NEVER trade against HTF. Only execute LTF setups aligned with HTF trend.

Why Single-Timeframe Trading Fails

Problem 1: Conflicting Signals

15min chart: Bullish breakout (BUY signal)
Daily chart: Downtrend, at resistance (SELL context)

Result: Breakout fails, you get stopped out

Solution: Check Daily FIRST. If bearish, don't take 15min longs.

Problem 2: No Context

5min chart: Perfect sweep setup at $100
BUT you don't know: $100 is major HTF resistance

Result: Entry looks great, but HTF sellers crush it

Solution: Mark HTF levels on LTF chart.

Problem 3: Poor Risk:Reward

1min scalp: Entry $100, stop $99.90, target $100.20
R:R = 1:2 (looks okay)

BUT Daily shows resistance at $100.15
Real R:R = 1:1.5 (terrible)

Solution: Use HTF for targets, LTF for entries.

Complete Multi-Timeframe Framework

Step 1: HTF Analysis (Daily)

Questions to answer:

  • [ ] Trend direction? (Up/Down/Range)
  • [ ] Price above/below major EMAs?
  • [ ] Minimal Flow regime? (Trending/Ranging/Volatile)
  • [ ] Major support/resistance nearby?
  • [ ] Bias: Long, Short, or Neutral?

Step 2: MTF Structure (4H)

Questions to answer:

  • [ ] Where are swing highs/lows?
  • [ ] Janus Atlas sweep zones identified?
  • [ ] Plutus Flow POC/HVN/LVN marked?
  • [ ] Structure supports HTF bias?
  • [ ] Entry zones defined?

Step 3: LTF Execution (15min)

Questions to answer:

  • [ ] Setup aligns with HTF + MTF?
  • [ ] Janus sweep confirmed (if reversal)?
  • [ ] Footprint shows absorption/imbalance?
  • [ ] Order book supports direction?
  • [ ] Entry candle closes in favor?

Step 4: Manage on MTF

Use MTF for management:

  • Targets: MTF swing highs/lows
  • Trailing stop: MTF structure breaks
  • Partial exits: MTF resistance/support

Alignment Scenarios

Scenario 1: Perfect Alignment (Trade This)

HTF (Daily): Uptrend, above 50 EMA, Minimal Flow trending up
MTF (4H): Pullback to support, Janus sweep completed
LTF (15min): Reversal candle, footprint absorption

Action: LONG with high conviction
Why: All timeframes aligned bullish

Win Rate: 65-75%

Scenario 2: Partial Alignment (Trade Carefully)

HTF (Daily): Uptrend, but approaching resistance
MTF (4H): Bullish structure
LTF (15min): Long setup

Action: LONG with reduced size, tight target
Why: HTF resistance creates risk

Win Rate: 50-60%

Scenario 3: Conflicting (SKIP)

HTF (Daily): Downtrend, below EMAs
MTF (4H): Bearish structure
LTF (15min): Bullish breakout setup

Action: SKIP (do not fight HTF)
Why: LTF longs against HTF downtrend = low win rate

Win Rate: 30-40% (avoid)

Risk: $200, Reward 1: $600, Reward 2: $1,100

Outcome: 70% win rate on setups like this.

Example 2: HTF Range, MTF Structure, LTF Fade

LTF (15min): Exhaustion at $455, negative delta despite bullish candle

Setup:
Entry: $454.80 (short on rejection)
Stop: $455.40 (above range high + buffer)

Common Multi-Timeframe Mistakes

Mistake 1: Analyzing HTF but Trading LTF Without Context

Bad: "Daily is uptrend, but this 1min short looks good!"

Fix: Only trade LTF setups aligned with HTF bias.

Mistake 2: Using LTF for Targets

Bad: HTF resistance at $105, but taking profit at $100.50 (5min level).

Fix: Use HTF/MTF for targets, LTF for entries.

Mistake 3: Too Many Timeframes

Bad: Analyzing Monthly, Weekly, Daily, 4H, 1H, 15min, 5min, 1min.

Fix: Stick to 3 timeframes maximum (Daily, 4H, 15min).

Signal Pilot Multi-Timeframe Integration

Minimal Flow (HTF Regime)

Use on Daily/4H to determine overall regime:

  • Trending: Trade WITH pullbacks, avoid counter-trend
  • Ranging: Fade extremes, target mid
  • Volatile: Reduce size, widen stops

Janus Atlas (MTF Structure)

Use on 4H/1H to identify entry zones:

  • Sweep zones (reversal areas)
  • Breakout levels
  • Liquidity pools

Plutus Flow (MTF + LTF Confirmation)

Use on 4H for POC, LTF for real-time delta:

  • 4H: Volume Profile POC (fair value)
  • 15min: CVD alignment, absorption/exhaustion

Omnideck (Multi-Timeframe Confluence)

Omnideck shows all indicators across timeframes:

  • HTF: Trend direction, regime
  • MTF: Structure, sweeps
  • LTF: Entry timing
  • When all align → Highest conviction trades
