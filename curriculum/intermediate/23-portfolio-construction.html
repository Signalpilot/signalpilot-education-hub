Portfolio Construction: Beyond Single Trades

Reading time ~14 min • Risk & Position Management

Portfolio risk ≠ sum of individual trade risks. Learn correlation, portfolio heat management, sector exposure limits, and professional portfolio construction frameworks.

Portfolio Heat (Total Risk)

Portfolio Heat = Total $ at risk across all open positions.

Trade 1: $200 risk (2%)
Trade 2: $150 risk (1.5%)
Trade 3: $100 risk (1%)

Total Heat: $450 (4.5%)

Max Heat Rule

Never exceed 6-8% total portfolio heat.

Why: If all positions hit stops simultaneously (market crash), you lose max 6-8% (survivable).

Correlation Risk

Problem: Correlated Positions

Trade 1: Long SPY (S&P 500 ETF)
Trade 2: Long QQQ (Nasdaq ETF)
Trade 3: Long AAPL (tech stock)

Correlation: ~0.85+ (highly correlated)
Problem: If market crashes, ALL 3 stop out together

Solution: Diversify Correlation

Trade 1: Long SPY (equities)
Trade 2: Short USD/JPY (forex, inverse correlation)
Trade 3: Long Gold (safe haven, low correlation)

Correlation: <0.3 (diversified)
Benefit: Market crash → SPY stops, but Gold/JPY may profit

Sector Exposure Limits

Bad: Concentrated Sector Risk

All positions in tech:
- AAPL long
- NVDA long
- TSLA long
- QQQ long

If tech sector crashes → 100% of portfolio at risk

Good: Sector Diversification

Max 30-40% per sector:
- Tech: 30% (SPY, AAPL)
- Energy: 20% (XLE)
- Crypto: 25% (BTC)
- Forex: 25% (EUR/USD)

If tech crashes → Only 30% exposed

Portfolio Construction Strategies

Strategy 1: Core + Satellite

Core (60-70%): Long-term, low-risk positions (indexes, large caps)

Satellite (30-40%): Active trades, higher risk/reward

$10,000 account:
Core: $6,000 in SPY (long-term hold)
Satellite: $4,000 active trading capital

Max risk on satellites: 6% = $240/trade

Strategy 2: Equal Weight

Each position gets equal allocation.

$10,000 account, 5 positions
Each position: $2,000 (20%)

Pros: Simple, balanced
Cons: Doesn't account for setup quality

Strategy 3: Kelly-Based Dynamic

Allocate based on edge (win rate × R:R).

A-grade (60% WR, 3R avg): 2% risk (high edge)
B-grade (50% WR, 2R avg): 1% risk (moderate edge)

Better setups get more size

Portfolio Rebalancing

When to Rebalance

  • Position hits target: Close, free up capital
  • Regime shift: Minimal Flow changes (trending → ranging)
  • Correlation spike: Positions become too correlated
  • Heat exceeds 6%: Close lowest-conviction position

Rebalancing Framework

Daily Check:
1. Total heat < 6%? (If no, reduce)
2. Correlation < 0.5? (If no, close most correlated)
3. Sector exposure < 40% each? (If no, reduce concentrated sector)
4. All positions still aligned with regime? (If no, exit misaligned)

Advanced: Hedging Strategies

Hedge 1: Inverse ETF

Core: Long SPY ($5,000)
Hedge: Short SPY via SH or SPXU (inverse ETF, $1,000)

If market crashes: SPY loses $500, SH gains $200+ (net loss reduced)

Hedge 2: Options (Defined Risk)

Core: Long QQQ ($5,000)
Hedge: Buy QQQ puts ($200 premium, protection below $400)

Max loss: $200 (premium paid)

Hedge 3: Safe Haven Assets

Portfolio: 70% equities
Hedge: 30% gold, treasuries (inverse correlation)

Market crash → Equities fall, gold rises (balance)

Complete Portfolio Framework

Step 1: Define Allocation

  • Core: 60-70% (long-term)
  • Satellite: 30-40% (active)

Step 2: Set Limits

  • Max heat: 6-8%
  • Max sector: 30-40%
  • Max correlation: 0.5

Step 3: Grade Setups

  • A-grade: 2% risk
  • B-grade: 1% risk

Step 4: Monitor & Rebalance

  • Daily heat check
  • Weekly correlation review
  • Monthly sector rebalance

Key Takeaways

  • Portfolio heat < 6-8% (total risk across positions)
  • Diversify correlation (avoid all positions moving together)
  • Limit sector exposure (max 30-40% per sector)
  • Grade-based sizing (A=2%, B=1%)
  • Rebalance regularly (daily heat, weekly correlation)

Exercises

Exercise 2: Check correlation
