Part 1: Advanced Sweep Patterns

Pattern #1: The Double Sweep (Highest Probability)

Here's what happens:

📖 War Story: The $100 Double Sweep

Asset: BTC

Level: $100 support (obvious level, everyone watching)

First sweep (9:00 AM):

  • Price at $100.20
  • Drops to $99.50 (sweep below $100)
  • Reverses to $100.50
  • Traders a long position might be considered: "Sweep complete, going up!"

The fake-out (10:00 AM):

  • Price drops back to $100
  • Longs stopped out at $99.90 (tight stops)
  • Frustration sets in

Second sweep (10:30 AM):

  • Price drops to $99.00 (deeper sweep!)
  • Footprint: +12,000 delta (massive absorption)
  • Reverses aggressively to $100.30

The move (11:00 AM - 2:00 PM):

Price rallies from $100.30 → $102.50 in 3 hours.

Why it worked:

  1. First sweep grabbed obvious stops ($99.50)
  2. Bounce to $100.50 created new longs with tight stops at $99.90
  3. Second sweep grabbed BOTH sets of stops ($99.50 + $99.90 stops)
  4. Now all stops cleared → Institutions loaded → Price free to move

Expected performance on double sweeps: Highest probability

💡 The Aha Moment

First sweep clears obvious stops. Second sweep clears the traders who bought the first sweep.

After the second sweep, there's nobody left to stop out. That's when the real move begins.

Pattern #2: Multi-Timeframe Sweep Confluence

Picture this: $100 is support on Daily, 4H, AND 1H charts.

That's not one liquidity pool. That's three pools converging.

Daily chart: Support at $100 (swing low from 3 weeks ago) 4H chart: Support at $100 (swing low from 4 days ago) 1H chart: Support at $100 (swing low from yesterday) Implication: MASSIVE liquidity cluster - Daily stops: Long-term traders (millions in size) - 4H stops: Swing traders (hundreds of thousands) - 1H stops: Day traders (thousands) Sweep result: $50M+ in stops triggered at $99.50 What happens after: Institutions absorb ALL of it Potential Reversal power: Extreme (highest expectancy)

Janus integration: When Janus marks confluence across multiple timeframes, that's your highest-conviction setup.

Pattern #3: Sweep and Reclaim (Immediate Confirmation)

The fastest potential reversal pattern in order flow trading.

Sweep-and-Reclaim Mechanics

12:00 PM: Price at $100.50

12:05 PM: Sweep to $99.40 (below $100 support)

12:06 PM: IMMEDIATE reclaim to $100.20 (within 1-3 candles)

12:07 PM: Volume spike + positive delta

What this means:

  • Sweep cleared stops
  • Immediate reclaim = absorption indicated
  • Failed potential breakdown (buyers defended aggressively)

Your Trade

Example entry area: $100.30 (above reclaim level)

Example stop: $99.30 (below sweep + buffer)

Potential target: $102.50 (previous high) = 2.2R

Janus integration: Janus marks sweep, then potential reclaim signal triggers entry area.

Expected performance: High probability

Key detail: Reclaim must happen within 1-3 candles. If it takes 10+ candles, it's not a sweep-and-reclaim—it's just noise.

Pattern #4: Failed Sweep (Trade Opposite)

Not all sweeps reverse. Some break through.

Here's how to spot the difference:

Failed Sweep Characteristics

What happens:

  1. Price sweeps $100 to $99.50
  2. Expected: Potential Reversal UP
  3. Reality: No reclaim within 5 candles
  4. Continues through to $98.50, $98.00 (potential breakdown)

Implication: Support was truly broken (not just swept).

Common approach: Consider short on continuation below swept level.

Trading Failed Sweeps

Setup:

  • Sweep below $100 to $99.50
  • NO reclaim within 5 candles
  • Continues to $98.50 (potential breakdown indicated)
  • Janus marks failed sweep

Your trade:

  • Example entry area: $98.30 (potential breakdown indicated)
  • Example stop: $99.80 (above failed sweep)
  • Potential target: $95.00 (next support) = 2.2R

Expected performance: Moderate to high probability

⚠️ Common Janus Mistakes

Mistake #1: Trading Every Sweep

"Janus showed sweep, entering immediately!"

Fix: Require reclaim + volume + order flow confirmation. Not all sweeps reverse.

Mistake #2: Ignoring Failed Sweeps

Holding long after sweep failed to reclaim.

Fix: If no reclaim within 5 candles, exit. Consider potential reversal trade (short).

Mistake #3: Fighting HTF After Sweep

LTF sweep potential reversal long, but HTF downtrend.

Fix: Only trade sweeps aligned with HTF bias. Don't fight the trend.
