Position Sizing: The Only Edge That Matters

Reading time ~11 min • Risk Reality

Win rate is a vanity metric. Position sizing is everything. Learn why fixed percentage is amateur hour, Kelly Criterion simplified, and ATR-based position calculators.

Why Win Rate Doesn't Matter

Trader A: 80% win rate, 1:1 R:R

  • 10 trades: 8 wins × +$100 = +$800
  • 2 losses × -$100 = -$200
  • Net: +$600

Trader B: 40% win rate, 3:1 R:R

  • 10 trades: 4 wins × +$300 = +$1,200
  • 6 losses × -$100 = -$600
  • Net: +$600 (same!)

Same profit, completely different stats. Win rate is IRRELEVANT without R:R.

The Fixed Percentage Trap

Retail approach: "I risk 1% per trade, always."

Problem: Not all setups are equal. High-quality sweep with 3R potential ≠ low-quality breakout with 1R potential.

Sizing should match setup quality, not be fixed.

Kelly Criterion Simplified

Formula: f* = (bp - q) / b

  • f* = % of bankroll to risk
  • b = Odds (R:R, e.g., 2 for 2:1)
  • p = Win rate (e.g., 0.55 for 55%)
  • q = Loss rate (1 - p)

Example: 55% win rate, 2:1 R:R

f* = (2 × 0.55 - 0.45) / 2 = 0.325 = 32.5%

Wait, 32.5% per trade?! That's insane.

Quarter Kelly (Realistic)

Full Kelly = too aggressive (huge drawdowns). Use Quarter Kelly (divide by 4):

Quarter Kelly = 32.5% / 4 = 8.1% per trade

Still aggressive, but survivable.

ATR-Based Position Sizing

Formula:

Position Size = (Account × Risk%) / Stop Distance

Example:

  • Account: $10,000
  • Risk: 2% = $200
  • Stop Distance: $2.00 (ATR-based, 2× ATR)
  • Position Size: $200 / $2 = 100 shares

If ATR is wider (volatile), position size is smaller. If ATR is tighter, position size is larger.

This scales risk to market conditions.

Portfolio Heat Management

Portfolio heat = total risk across all open positions.

Example:

  • Trade 1: Risking $200 (2%)
  • Trade 2: Risking $150 (1.5%)
  • Trade 3: Risking $100 (1%)
  • Total heat: $450 (4.5%)

Max heat rule: Never exceed 6% total portfolio risk.

If you're at 5.5% heat, DO NOT take new trade until one closes.

Variable Position Sizing Based on Setup Quality

Grade setups A/B/C:

  • A-grade: Sweep + absorption + HTF alignment + Omnideck confluence → Risk 2%
  • B-grade: Clean structure, 2/3 confirmations → Risk 1%
  • C-grade: Marginal setup, 1/2 confirmations → Risk 0.5% (or skip)

Never trade C-grade setups. Only A/B. Scale size to quality.

Complete Position Sizing Framework

Step 1: Grade Setup

  • A/B/C based on confluence (Janus, Plutus, Minimal Flow, Harmonic Oscillator)

Step 2: Calculate Stop Distance

  • ATR-based: 1.5-2× ATR beyond structure

Step 3: Calculate Position Size

  • Size = (Account × Risk%) / Stop Distance
  • A-grade: 2% risk, B-grade: 1% risk

Step 4: Check Portfolio Heat

  • Total risk across open positions < 6%
  • If >6%, wait for trade to close

Step 5: Execute

  • Enter with calculated size
  • Set stop at calculated distance
  • Target minimum 2R (adjust based on structure)

Common Mistakes

Mistake 1: Fixed 1% on every trade. Fix: Scale to setup quality (A=2%, B=1%, C=skip).

Mistake 2: Ignoring ATR. Fix: Wider ATR = smaller size, tighter ATR = larger size.

Mistake 3: No portfolio heat limit. Fix: Max 6% total risk across all positions.

Key Takeaways

  • Win rate is vanity, R:R + sizing = profit
  • Fixed % is amateur (scale to setup quality)
  • Kelly Criterion = optimal sizing (use Quarter Kelly)
  • ATR-based stops = dynamic sizing (adapts to volatility)
  • Portfolio heat < 6% (total risk across positions)
  • Grade setups A/B/C, size accordingly
