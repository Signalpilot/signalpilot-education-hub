If you can handle overnight volatility, swing trading is the highest win-rate strategy retail has access to.

Part 1: Daily Timeframe Structure Analysis

Why Daily Charts?

Daily charts occupy the sweet spot between noise and lag:

Timeframe Signal Quality Edge Source Problem
1-min, 5-min Low (HFT noise) Speed execution Retail always slowest player
15-min, 1-hour Medium (intraday structure) Order flow + structure Still requires all-day monitoring
Daily High (institutional flows) Multi-day positioning, structure, fundamentals alignment Overnight risk (manageable)
Weekly Very high (macro trends) Long-term fundamentals Too slow for trading, better for investing

Daily charts filter intraday noise while capturing institutional flows that take 3-10 days to complete. A hedge fund building a 5,000-share position in AAPL doesn't do it in one 5-minute candle‚Äîthey accumulate over days. That accumulation shows up as daily order blocks.

Daily Market Structure: The Foundation

Market structure rules apply identically to daily charts, but signals carry more weight:

Bullish Daily Structure (Long Bias):

  • Higher highs, higher lows on daily chart
  • Each pullback finds support at higher level than previous
  • Daily BOS (Break of Structure) = major trend continuation signal
  • Daily ChoCH (Change of Character) = potential multi-week reversal

Bearish Daily Structure (Short Bias):

  • Lower highs, lower lows on daily chart
  • Each rally meets resistance at lower level than previous
  • Daily BOS downward = strong bearish continuation
  • Daily ChoCH upward = reversal (cover shorts, consider longs)

Example: SPY Daily Uptrend (Oct-Nov 2024)

Oct 10: Low at $560 (higher low vs Sept)
Oct 18: High at $585 (higher high)
Oct 25: Pullback to $572 (higher low ‚úì)
Nov 1:  Break to $595 (BOS = continuation confirmed)
Nov 8:  Pullback to $580 (higher low ‚úì)
Nov 15: Break to $605 (BOS again)

= Daily uptrend intact (HH, HL pattern)
= Swing long bias at every pullback
= Target next BOS higher

Daily Order Blocks: Institutional Positioning Zones

What makes daily order blocks different?

  • They represent multi-day accumulation/distribution (not single-candle algos)
  • Institutions have large positions there (they'll defend these levels)
  • They're visible to all market participants (self-fulfilling support/resistance)
  • They last weeks or months (vs intraday blocks lasting hours)

Identifying Daily Order Blocks:

  1. Find strong displacement on daily chart (big move, 5-15% in 3-7 days)
  2. Identify last opposite-colored candle before displacement
  3. Mark that candle's body as order block zone
  4. Wait for price to retrace back to zone (may take 1-3 weeks)
  5. Enter on retest with tight stop below/above zone

Part 2: Position Sizing for Swing Trades

The Overnight Risk Problem

Day traders close positions before market close, eliminating overnight gap risk. Swing traders hold through multiple sessions, accepting gaps, earnings, news events. This requires different position sizing.

Day Trading Risk:

  • 1-2% account risk per trade
  • No overnight gaps
  • Can potential exit immediately if setup fails
  • Tight stops (5-20 ticks on ES)

Swing Trading Risk:

  • 0.5-1% account risk per trade (HALF of day trading)
  • Overnight gap risk (can gap through stop)
  • Must hold through intraday noise
  • Wider stops (daily ATR = 2-5% for stocks)

Stop Placement Rules for Swing Trades

Use structure, not arbitrary percentages:

  • For longs: Stop below daily order block low (or previous daily swing low)
  • For shorts: Stop above daily order block high (or previous daily swing high)
  • Weekly holds: Stop below/above weekly swing point
  • Earnings risk: Tighten stop or reduce size before earnings

ATR-Based Stops (Alternative Method):

Calculate 14-day ATR (Average True Range)
Stop Distance = 1.5√ó to 2√ó ATR

Example: SPY daily ATR = $8
         Entry: $600
         Stop: $600 - (2 √ó $8) = $584

This gives price room to move without stopping you out on normal volatility

New swing traders use day-trading position sizes (2% risk) with wider daily stops. Result: One bad trade wipes out 5-8% of account. Cut position size in HALF when transitioning from day trading to swing trading. Overnight risk is real‚Äîgaps can blow through stops. Smaller positions = longer survival.

Strategy 3: Gap Fill Reversion

When stocks gap up/down on news but structure doesn't support it, gaps often fill. This creates mean-reversion swing setups.

Bullish Gap Fill Setup:

  • Stock gaps down 5-15% on earnings or news
  • BUT daily structure remains bullish (no ChoCH)
  • Gap creates "fair value gap" (unfilled price range)
  • Enter long at daily order block BELOW gap
  • Target: Gap fill + previous high

Bearish Gap Fill Setup:

  • Stock gaps up 5-15% on news
  • BUT daily structure remains bearish (or hitting resistance)
  • Enter short at daily order block ABOVE gap
  • Target: Gap fill + previous low

üí° Gap Statistics (Why This Works)

According to studies of S&P 500 stocks: 72% of gaps over 3% fill within 10 trading days. Institutions use gaps to engineer better entry prices‚Äîretail panic sells on gap downs (institutions accumulate), retail FOMO buys on gap ups (institutions distribute). Swing traders profit by fading the retail reaction.

Tier 1 40% Initial order block touch Get positioned early with lower risk
Tier 2 30% 4H BOS or strong daily candle reaction Add on confirmation
Tier 3 30% Deep retest (78.6% Fib or OTE zone) Maximize position if price gives deeper discount

Trailing Stop Strategies

Method 1: Daily 8 EMA Trailing Stop

  • Once trade is 2R+ profitable, trail stop below daily 8 EMA
  • Exit when daily close breaks below 8 EMA
  • Pros: Catches most of trend, simple to implement
  • Cons: Can give back 5-10% on whipsaw

Method 2: ATR Trailing Stop

  • Trail stop at Entry + (2√ó daily ATR)
  • As price rises, stop rises but never falls
  • Exit when price hits trailing stop
  • Pros: Volatility-adjusted, tighter than EMA
  • Cons: More likely to potential exit early on volatile swings

Method 3: Structure-Based Trail

  • Trail stop below each new daily higher low
  • Exit when daily structure breaks (makes lower low)
  • Pros: Stays in trade until structure breaks
  • Cons: Requires daily chart monitoring

When to Exit Early (Cut Winners Short)

Sometimes structure changes before hitting targets. Exit immediately if:

  • Daily ChoCH against your position (trend reversal confirmed)
  • Weekly structure breaks (higher timeframe trend change)
  • Major news event (FOMC, earnings, geopolitical shock)
  • Volatility spike (VIX +20% in single day = potential exit risk-on positions)
  • Target already hit 80% of expected move (diminishing returns)

The 4 Types of Overnight Risk

Risk Type Description Mitigation Strategy
Gap Risk Price gaps through your stop (no fill at stop price) Use smaller position size (0.5-1% risk vs 2%)
Earnings Risk Company reports earnings, stock gaps 10-30% Close or reduce position before earnings, or use options hedge
News Risk Unexpected news (Fed, geopolitical, regulation) Check economic calendar, reduce size before FOMC/CPI
Weekend Risk News over weekend when markets closed Reduce position 30-50% before Friday close if concerned

Earnings Risk Management

Never hold through earnings unless that's your thesis. Earnings gaps are 50/50 coin flips. Your edge is structure, not guessing earnings.

Options for Managing Earnings:

  1. Close Position Entirely (1-2 days before earnings)
    • Safest approach
    • Lock in gains, re-enter after earnings if structure holds
  2. Reduce Position by 50-75%
    • Keep small position for upside capture
    • Minimize downside risk
  3. Hedge with Options
    • Long stock? Buy ATM or slightly OTM put (protective put)
    • Short stock? Buy ATM or slightly OTM call (protective call)
    • Cost: 2-5% of position, but caps risk to that cost

Example: NVDA Earnings Hedge

Position: Long 100 shares NVDA at $500 (swing trade in profit)
Earnings: Tomorrow after close
Risk: Stock could gap down 10-20% on earnings miss

Hedge Option:
Buy 1√ó NVDA $500 put expiring in 2 weeks
Cost: $15 per share = $1,500 total

Outcome if gap down: Put gains $50+ per share, offsets stock loss
Outcome if gap up: Lose $1,500 on put, but stock gains $50-100 per share
Net result: Downside capped at -$1,500, upside unlimited

News & Economic Event Management

High-Impact Events (Reduce Size or Exit):

  • FOMC meetings (Fed rate decisions)
  • CPI / PPI (inflation data)
  • NFP (Non-Farm Payrolls, jobs report)
  • Geopolitical crises (war, sanctions, political instability)
  • Major tech earnings (AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN move entire market)

Event Management Rules:

  1. Check economic calendar every Sunday (mark FOMC, CPI, NFP dates)
  2. Reduce swing positions by 30-50% before major events
  3. Don't enter new swing trades 1-2 days before FOMC
  4. After event: Re-enter if structure holds, potential exit if structure breaks

Weekend Hold Strategy

Full Weekend Hold (100% position):

  • Weekly structure strongly supports direction
  • No major news expected over weekend
  • Trade already 2R+ profitable (house money)

Partial Weekend Hold (50% position):

  • Trade is profitable but less than 2R
  • Structure is intact but not confirmed on weekly
  • Geopolitical uncertainty present

No Weekend Hold (close Friday):

  • Trade in drawdown (below potential entry)
  • Major geopolitical risk (war, elections, crisis)
  • Weekly structure showing weakness

‚ö†Ô∏è The "Sunday Night Disaster" Scenario

Futures open Sunday 6 PM ET. If major news breaks over weekend, stock futures can gap 3-5% before cash market opens Monday 9:30 AM. If you're holding a full-size swing position against that gap, you're stopped out at worst price with no control. This is why swing traders use 0.5-1% risk instead of 2%.
