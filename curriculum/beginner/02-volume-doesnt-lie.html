Volume Doesn't Lie, But You're Reading It Wrong

Reading time ~20 min • Market Structure Reality • Plutus Flow

Hook: Volume bars show quantity, not quality. Two candles with identical volume can have completely opposite implications. One signals absorption (continuation), the other exhaustion (reversal). Traditional volume analysis can't tell you which—but delta analysis can.

Part 1: Why Traditional Volume Analysis Fails

The Standard Volume Bar Problem

Most traders look at volume bars under the chart:

  • High volume = "Strong move"
  • Low volume = "Weak move"
  • Volume spike = "Something important"

But this tells you NOTHING about direction or quality.

The Identical Volume Paradox

Scenario A: Bullish candle, 100,000 volume
Breakdown: 80,000 buy volume, 20,000 sell volume
Delta: +60,000 (strong buying)
Implication: Continuation UP likely

Scenario B: Bullish candle, 100,000 volume
Breakdown: 40,000 buy volume, 60,000 sell volume
Delta: -20,000 (selling pressure)
Implication: Reversal DOWN likely

Same candle shape, same volume, opposite outcomes. Traditional volume bars can't differentiate—but delta can.

Part 2: Delta Analysis — Buy Volume vs. Sell Volume

What IS Delta?

Delta = Buy Volume - Sell Volume

  • Positive Delta (+): More buying than selling (bullish pressure)
  • Negative Delta (-): More selling than buying (bearish pressure)
  • Cumulative Volume Delta (CVD): Running total of delta (trend of pressure)

How Delta Works

Buy Volume: Aggressive market buy orders (lift the ask)
Sell Volume: Aggressive market sell orders (hit the bid)

Example:

Price at $100.00 / $100.01 (bid/ask)

Trade 1: 1,000 shares bought at $100.01 (ask lifted) → +1,000 buy volume
Trade 2: 500 shares sold at $100.00 (bid hit) → +500 sell volume
Trade 3: 2,000 shares bought at $100.02 (ask lifted) → +2,000 buy volume

Delta = (1,000 + 2,000) - 500 = +2,500 (bullish)

Part 3: Absorption vs. Exhaustion

Absorption (Strength)

Definition: High volume + price holds level = Strong hand absorbing pressure

Bullish Absorption Example:

Price at $100 support
Volume spike: 200,000 shares
Delta: -80,000 (heavy selling)
Price action: Closes at $100.05 (held support)

Interpretation: Strong buyer absorbed all selling → Reversal UP likely

Signal Pilot: Plutus Flow shows volume spike with negative delta, but price holds → Absorption confirmed.

Exhaustion (Weakness)

Definition: High volume + price rejects level = Buyers/sellers exhausted

Bullish Exhaustion Example:

Price rallies to $105 resistance
Volume spike: 180,000 shares
Delta: +30,000 (moderate buying)
Price action: Closes at $104.50 (rejected resistance)

Interpretation: Buyers exhausted, couldn't break $105 → Reversal DOWN likely

Signal Pilot: Plutus Flow shows volume spike with weak positive delta at resistance → Exhaustion confirmed.

Part 4: Volume Profile — POC, HVN, LVN

What IS Volume Profile?

Instead of showing volume over TIME (traditional bars), Volume Profile shows volume at each PRICE level.

Key Components

  • POC (Point of Control): Price level with highest volume (fair value)
  • Value Area: Range containing 70% of volume (accepted prices)
  • HVN (High Volume Node): Price clusters with heavy trading (support/resistance)
  • LVN (Low Volume Node): Price gaps with little trading (rejection zones)

Using Volume Profile for Trading

POC as Magnet:

  • Price tends to revert to POC (fair value)
  • Above POC = potential pullback target
  • Below POC = potential rally target

HVN as Support/Resistance:

  • HVN = accepted price = more likely to hold
  • Price approaching HVN = watch for absorption

LVN as Breakout Zones:

  • LVN = rejected price = less likely to hold
  • Breakout through LVN = fast move (little resistance)

Part 6: Common Mistakes

Mistake 1: High Volume = Bullish

Bad: "Volume is high, price must go up!"
Reality: High volume can be selling pressure (distribution).
Fix: Check delta—positive or negative?

Mistake 2: Ignoring Delta Divergence

Bad: "Price is rising, so it's bullish."
Reality: CVD falling while price rises = Distribution.
Fix: Require CVD and price alignment (or trade the divergence).

Mistake 3: Trading Every Volume Spike

Bad: "Plutus Flow spike, entering now!"
Reality: Spikes can be absorption OR exhaustion—context matters.
Fix: Require structure (Janus Atlas), regime (Minimal Flow), and price action confirmation.

Part 7: Key Takeaways

Core Concepts

  • Delta = Buy Volume - Sell Volume (directional pressure)
  • CVD = Cumulative delta (trend of pressure)
  • Absorption = High volume, price holds (strength)
  • Exhaustion = High volume, price rejects (weakness)
  • Volume Profile POC = Fair value (magnet)
