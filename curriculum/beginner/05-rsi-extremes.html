RSI Extremes Are Not Reversal Signals

Reading time ~12 min • Indicator Truth • Harmonic Oscillator

RSI >70 is often a BUY signal in trending markets. Learn regime-based RSI interpretation, when overbought means continuation vs. reversal, and Harmonic Oscillator voting system.

The RSI Myth

What you were taught: RSI >70 = Overbought = Sell. RSI <30 = Oversold = Buy.

Reality: In strong uptrends, RSI stays >70 for weeks. Selling at 70 = missing massive rallies.

Regime-Based RSI Interpretation

Trending Market (Use RSI as Filter, Not Trigger)

Uptrend:

  • RSI >50 = Bullish regime (stay long biased)
  • RSI >70 = Strong momentum (DO NOT fade)
  • RSI pullback to 40-50 = BUY opportunity

Downtrend:

  • RSI <50 = Bearish regime (stay short biased)
  • RSI <30 = Strong momentum DOWN (DO NOT fade)
  • RSI rally to 50-60 = SELL opportunity

Ranging Market (Use RSI as Mean Reversion)

Range-bound:

  • RSI >70 = Overbought (fade to range mid)
  • RSI <30 = Oversold (buy to range mid)
  • RSI 40-60 = Neutral (wait for extremes)

The key: REGIME determines interpretation. Use Minimal Flow to detect regime (trending, ranging, volatile).

Harmonic Oscillator: 5-Indicator Voting System

Components:

  1. RSI (momentum)
  2. Stochastic (price location in range)
  3. CCI (Commodity Channel Index)
  4. Williams %R (overbought/oversold)
  5. MFI (Money Flow Index—volume-weighted)

Voting Logic:

  • 5/5 agreement: Extreme signal (high probability reversal in RANGE)
  • 4/5 agreement: Strong signal
  • 3/5 agreement: Moderate signal
  • 2/5 or less: Conflicting (wait)

When RSI Extremes DO Matter

Scenario 1: Ranging Market + 5/5 Harmonic Oscillator

Setup: Price in $100-$105 range for 2 weeks. RSI hits 75, all 5 Harmonic Oscillator components agree (overbought).

Action: Short at $104.50, target $100 (range mid), stop $105.50.

Scenario 2: Divergence in Trend

Setup: Price makes new high ($110), but RSI makes lower high (divergence). Uptrend weakening.

Action: Watch for breakdown, exit longs, prepare short.

Scenario 3: Extreme Exhaustion (RSI >85 or <15)

Setup: RSI hits 92 (rare extreme). Even in strong trend, short-term pullback likely.

Action: Take partial profits on longs, tighten stops.

Complete RSI Framework

Step 1: Identify Regime (Minimal Flow)

  • Trending: Use RSI as trend filter (>50 bullish, <50 bearish)
  • Ranging: Use RSI for mean reversion (>70 short, <30 long)
  • Volatile: Ignore RSI (too noisy)

Step 2: Check Harmonic Oscillator Confluence

  • 4-5/5 agreement = High probability signal
  • <2/5 agreement = Wait

Step 3: Require Structural Confirmation

  • Janus Atlas: Sweep or breakout
  • Plutus Flow: Delta alignment
  • Price action: Reversal or continuation candle

Common Mistakes

Mistake 1: Fading RSI 70 in uptrends. Fix: Only fade in RANGING regime.

Mistake 2: Ignoring regime. Fix: Use Minimal Flow to determine market state.

Mistake 3: Trading RSI alone. Fix: Require Harmonic Oscillator + structure (Janus, Plutus).

Key Takeaways

  • RSI >70 in uptrend = continuation (not reversal)
  • RSI <30 in downtrend = continuation (not reversal)
  • Regime determines interpretation (trending vs. ranging)
  • Harmonic Oscillator = voting system (4-5/5 agreement required)
  • RSI is a filter, not a trigger (require structure confirmation)
