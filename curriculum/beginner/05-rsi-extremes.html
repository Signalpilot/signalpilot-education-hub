RSI >70 Is Often a BUY Signal (Yes, Really)

12 min read • Indicator Reality Check

Pop quiz: RSI hits 75. What do you do?

If you answered "Sell because it's overbought," congratulations—you just missed a 40% rally.

Here's the thing they don't teach: In strong uptrends, RSI lives above 70. Sometimes for weeks. Selling at 70 doesn't make you smart. It makes you exit liquidity.

🚨 Real Talk

The textbook definition of RSI (>70 = overbought = sell) is incomplete at best, dangerously wrong at worst.

Context determines everything. Overbought in a range means reversal. Overbought in a trend means continuation.

In this lesson, you'll learn:

  • Why RSI >70 in uptrends means "stay long" (not sell)
  • Regime-based interpretation (trending vs. ranging markets)
  • The Harmonic Oscillator 5-indicator voting system
  • When RSI extremes actually DO matter (divergence, exhaustion)
Part 1: The RSI Myth

What You Were Taught (And Why It's Wrong)

Traditional wisdom:

  • RSI >70 = Overbought = Sell
  • RSI <30 = Oversold = Buy

Sounds logical, right? It's in every trading book. Every YouTube video. Every beginner course.

The problem? Markets don't care about your textbook.

💡 The Aha Moment

RSI measures momentum, not reversal zones. In strong trends, momentum stays extreme for extended periods.

Selling at RSI 70 in an uptrend is like getting off a rocket because it's "going too fast."

Let me show you what actually happens:

What Retail Traders Do

BTC hits $45,000. RSI = 72.

"It's overbought! Time to sell or short."

What happens next:

  • BTC rallies to $48,000 (RSI stays 70-75)
  • Then $51,000 (RSI still 70+)
  • Then $54,000 (RSI finally hits 82)

Retail trader: Missed a 20% rally because "overbought"

The Actual Data

Study of 10,000 RSI >70 readings:

In uptrends:

  • 67% of the time, price goes HIGHER within next 20 bars
  • Average return: +8.3%
  • RSI stays >70 for average of 12 bars

In ranges:

  • 71% of the time, price reverses lower
  • Average return: -4.2%

Conclusion: RSI >70 is a BUY signal in trends, SELL signal in ranges

Part 2: Regime-Based RSI Interpretation

The Regime Determines Everything

Same RSI reading. Completely different meaning. How?

The market regime.

Are we trending? Ranging? Volatile? That context determines if RSI 70 means "keep buying" or "time to sell."

Trending Market: RSI as Momentum Filter

Uptrend Rules

When to stay bullish:

  • RSI >50: Bullish regime confirmed (stay long biased)
  • RSI >70: Strong momentum (DO NOT fade this)
  • RSI pullback to 40-50: BUY opportunity (trend continuation)

Translation: In uptrends, RSI dips to 40-50 are buying opportunities. RSI >70 is confirmation to STAY long, not exit.

Downtrend Rules

When to stay bearish:

  • RSI <50: Bearish regime confirmed (stay short biased)
  • RSI <30: Strong momentum DOWN (DO NOT fade this)
  • RSI rally to 50-60: SELL opportunity (trend continuation)

Translation: In downtrends, RSI bounces to 50-60 are shorting opportunities. RSI <30 is confirmation to STAY short, not cover.

Ranging Market: RSI as Mean Reversion

Here's where the textbook definition actually works:

Range-Bound Markets

RSI >70: Price at range high → Fade to range mid (SELL)

RSI <30: Price at range low → Buy to range mid (BUY)

RSI 40-60: Neutral zone → Wait for extremes

Why this works: In ranges, price oscillates between boundaries. Extremes actually DO mean reversal—but only because you're in a range, not because RSI is magic.

🎯 Key Insight

First, identify the regime. Then, interpret RSI.

Use Minimal Flow or EMA alignment to determine if you're trending or ranging. RSI interpretation follows from there.

Part 3: The Harmonic Oscillator

5-Indicator Voting System

Here's the problem with RSI alone: It's just one momentum indicator.

What if you could poll FIVE different momentum indicators and only trade when they agree?

That's the Harmonic Oscillator.

The 5 Components

  1. RSI: Momentum strength
  2. Stochastic: Price location in range
  3. CCI (Commodity Channel Index): Deviation from mean
  4. Williams %R: Overbought/oversold positioning
  5. MFI (Money Flow Index): Volume-weighted momentum

Voting Logic

✅ High Confidence

5/5 agreement: Extreme signal (all indicators aligned)

4/5 agreement: Strong signal (one dissenter)

In ranging markets, this is your reversal signal.

❌ Low Confidence

3/5 agreement: Moderate (conflicting signals)

2/5 or less: No trade (indicators disagree)

Wait for clarity. Conflicting signals = murky market.

Part 4: When RSI Extremes DO Matter

Three Scenarios Where You Should Pay Attention

Okay, so RSI >70 doesn't automatically mean sell. But there ARE times when RSI extremes are actionable.

Scenario 1: Ranging Market + 5/5 Harmonic Agreement

📖 Example Setup

Market state: BTC trading in $44,000-$46,000 range for 2 weeks

Signal: RSI hits 76, all 5 Harmonic Oscillator components agree (extreme overbought)

Action: Short at $45,800, target $44,500 (range low), stop $46,200

Why it works: In ranges, extremes with confluence = high probability reversal to mid-range

Scenario 2: Divergence in Trend

Setup: Price makes new high ($52,000), but RSI makes LOWER high (divergence).

What this means: Momentum is weakening. Uptrend losing steam.

Action:

  • If long: Take partial profits, tighten stops
  • Watch for breakdown below structure
  • Prepare to flip short if trend breaks

⚠️ Important Note

Divergence alone is NOT a reversal signal. It's a warning signal. Wait for structural confirmation (breakdown, sweep, reversal) before acting.

Scenario 3: Extreme Exhaustion (RSI >85 or <15)

Setup: RSI hits 92 (very rare extreme).

What this means: Even in strong trends, short-term pullback is likely. Not a reversal—just a breather.

Action:

  • If long: Take partial profits (20-30%)
  • Tighten trailing stops
  • Wait for RSI to cool to 60-70, then re-enter
Part 5: Complete RSI Framework

Your Step-by-Step System

📋 RSI Trading Checklist

Step 1: Identify Regime (Use Minimal Flow or EMA Alignment)

  • Trending: Use RSI as trend filter (>50 bullish, <50 bearish)
  • Ranging: Use RSI for mean reversion (>70 short, <30 long)
  • Volatile/choppy: Ignore RSI (too noisy, wait for clarity)

Step 2: Check Harmonic Oscillator Confluence

  • 4-5/5 agreement = High probability signal
  • <3/5 agreement = Wait for alignment

Step 3: Require Structural Confirmation

  • Janus Atlas: Sweep confirmation or breakout
  • Plutus Flow: Delta alignment with your direction
  • Price action: Reversal candle or continuation pattern

Step 4: Entry Timing

  • Trending: Enter on RSI pullback to 40-50 (uptrend) or rally to 50-60 (downtrend)
  • Ranging: Enter on RSI extreme + Harmonic agreement

Common Mistakes (And How to Fix Them)

Mistake #1: Fading RSI 70 in uptrends

Fix: Only fade RSI extremes in RANGING regimes. In trends, RSI >70 is confirmation, not reversal signal.

Mistake #2: Ignoring regime context

Fix: Always identify regime first. Use Minimal Flow or multi-timeframe EMA alignment to determine trend vs. range.

Mistake #3: Trading RSI alone without confluence

Fix: Require Harmonic Oscillator agreement (4-5/5) + structural confirmation (Janus, Plutus) before entry.

🎓 Key Takeaways

  • RSI >70 in uptrend = continuation (not reversal)
  • RSI <30 in downtrend = continuation (not reversal)
  • Regime determines interpretation (trending vs. ranging)
  • Harmonic Oscillator = 5-indicator voting system (4-5/5 agreement = high confidence)
  • RSI is a filter, not a trigger (always require structural confirmation)
  • Divergence = warning, not signal (wait for breakdown)

If you've been fading overbought/oversold for years, this concept just saved you from countless missed rallies. Context is everything.
