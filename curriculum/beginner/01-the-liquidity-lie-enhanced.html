Part 1: The Retail Myth

What You Were Taught

"When price reaches support, buyers step in and push it higher."
"When price reaches resistance, sellers step in and push it lower."

This sounds logical. It's taught in every trading book, YouTube video, and free course. The problem? It's incomplete at best, dangerously wrong at worst.

üí° Why This Mental Model Fails

The retail model assumes:

  • Markets are fair (they're not)
  • Everyone sees the same levels (we do, that's the problem)
  • Price respects these levels organically (it doesn't, it's engineered)

Reality check: When EVERYONE sees the same support level, it becomes a target‚Äînot a defense.

Part 2: What Support Actually Represents

Support = Clustered Stop Losses Below Price

üéØ Key Concept: Liquidity Engineering

Support isn't broken randomly‚Äîit's targeted for the stop-loss liquidity below it. This is how institutions accumulate positions at better prices.

Scenario: BTC is trading at $45,000. Previous low ("support") is at $44,500.

What Retail Traders Do

  • 1,000 long traders enter near $45,000
  • They all place stops at $44,450 (just below support)
  • Total liquidity: $10,000,000 in stop losses
  • Result: Predictable cluster of sell orders

What Institutions See

  • $10M in guaranteed sell orders at $44,450
  • Triggerable by pushing price down $550
  • Cost to trigger: $2-3M in sell pressure
  • Profit: Buy $10M+ worth at better prices, ride it back up

The Liquidity Sweep Mechanic

Step 1: Retail Clusters

Price approaches obvious support ($100). Retail traders:

  • Go long at $100.50
  • Place stops at $99.50 (below support)
  • Everyone uses the same textbook approach
Why this happens: Retail traders all read the same books and watch the same YouTube videos. Stop placement becomes predictable.
Step 2: Institutional Sweep

Large player executes coordinated sell pressure:

  • Price pushed from $100 to $99.40 (sweeps the stops)
  • Stop losses trigger (forced selling)
  • Institutions buy AT those sell orders (accumulate cheap)

This creates a volume spike‚Äîbut it's not organic selling, it's engineered liquidity grab.

Step 3: Reversal

With retail stopped out and institutions positioned:

  • Buy pressure resumes (institutions are now long)
  • Price rallies back above $100
  • Retail: "I knew support would hold! But I got stopped out..."

üéì The Trap

Support DID hold (eventually). But not before sweeping YOUR stop. This is why "being right" doesn't equal profits.

Key Takeaways

  • Support/resistance are liquidity pools, not magical price levels
  • Obvious levels get swept because retail stops cluster below them
  • Wait for the sweep, then enter on the reversal (with institutions)
