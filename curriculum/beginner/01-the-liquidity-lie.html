Part 1: What You Were Taught (And Why It's Wrong)

The Support/Resistance Fairy Tale

"When price reaches support, buyers step in and push it higher."

"When price reaches resistance, sellers step in and push it lower."

Sounds logical, right? It's in every trading book. Every YouTube video. Every free course.

The problem? It's incomplete at best. Dangerously wrong at worst.

üí° Why This Mental Model Fails

The retail story assumes:

  • Markets are fair ‚Äî They're not
  • Everyone sees the same levels ‚Äî We do. That's the problem
  • Price respects these levels naturally ‚Äî It doesn't. It's engineered

Here's the thing: When EVERYONE sees the same support level, it becomes a target‚Äînot a defense.

Why? Because predictable behavior creates opportunity. And institutions are paid to exploit opportunity.

Part 2: What Support Actually Represents

Support = Clustered Stop Losses

Picture this:

BTC is trading at $45,000. The previous low ("support") is at $44,500.

What does retail do?

The Retail Perspective

1,000 traders observe long opportunities near $45,000

They all place stops at $44,450 (just below that "strong support")

Total liquidity sitting there: $10,000,000 in stop losses

"I'm protected if support breaks!"

Result: Predictable cluster of guaranteed sell orders

The Institutional Perspective

$10M in guaranteed sell orders at $44,450

Triggerable by pushing price down just $550

Cost to trigger: $2-3M in coordinated sell pressure

Reward: Buy $10M+ at better prices, then ride it back up

"Free liquidity. Let's take it."

Result: Profitable trade using retail as liquidity

üéØ The Aha Moment

Support isn't broken randomly. It's targeted for the stop-loss liquidity below it.

This is liquidity engineering. And it happens every single day.

How The Sweep Works

Let's break down the mechanics. It's actually simple:

Step 1: Retail Clusters

Price approaches obvious support at $100.

Retail traders (following the textbook):

  • Long positions commonly observed at $100.50
  • Place stops at $99.50 (below support)
  • Everyone learned the same strategy

Why this happens: We all read the same books. Watch the same videos. Take the same courses.

Stop placement becomes predictable. Predictable behavior creates opportunity.

Step 2: The Sweep

A large player executes coordinated sell pressure:

  • Price pushed from $100 to $99.40 (below the stop cluster)
  • Stop losses trigger automatically (forced selling)
  • Institutions buy AT those forced sell orders

This creates a volume spike‚Äîbut it's not organic selling. It's engineered liquidity.

Retail sees: "Support broke! Dump incoming!"

Institutions see: "Accumulation complete. Time to rally."

Step 3: The Potential Reversal

With retail stopped out and institutions positioned:

  • Buy pressure resumes (institutions are now long)
  • Price rallies back above $100
  • Often pushes significantly higher ($102, $103+)

Retail reaction: "I KNEW support would hold! But I got stopped out... Bad luck I guess."

üéì The Trap Explained

Support DID hold (you were right about direction!)

But not before sweeping YOUR stop first.

This is why being "right" about the market doesn't equal profits.

Part 5: Types of Sweeps

Common Sweep Patterns

1. Obvious Support/Resistance Sweep

What: Price breaks clearly visible horizontal level

Why: Everyone sees it ‚Üí Clustered stops ‚Üí High liquidity

Example: $100 psychological level, previous day low, previous week high

2. Equal Lows/Highs Sweep

What: Multiple swing lows/highs at same price

Why: Retail sees "triple bottom = strong support" ‚Üí More stops below

Example: Three swing lows at $44,500 = Massive stop cluster at $44,400

3. Trendline Sweep

What: Price breaks ascending/descending trendline

Why: Trendline traders place stops just beyond the line

Example: Uptrend support line ‚Üí Stops clustered below it

4. Round Number Sweep

What: Price sweeps psychological levels

Why: Retail loves round numbers ($100, $1000, $50,000)

Example: BTC sweeps $50,000 to $49,800, reverses to $51,500

Part 6: Common Mistakes

How To NOT Get Swept

Mistake #1: Buying Support Blindly

Bad: "$100 is support, I'm buying here with stop at $99.50."

Why it fails: You're providing the liquidity for the sweep

Fix: Wait for the sweep, THEN watch for potential reversal

Mistake #2: Tight Stops Below "Strong" Support

Bad: "Support is so strong, I'll use a 0.5% stop."

Why it fails: Tighter stops = Easier to sweep

Fix: Use 1.5-2x ATR stops beyond swept low

Mistake #3: Ignoring Volume

Bad: "Price touched support and bounced, going long!"

Why it fails: Without volume spike, it's not a sweep (just a test)

Fix: Require volume confirmation via Janus Atlas

Mistake #4: Trading Every Sweep

Bad: "Sweep detected, entering immediately!"

Why it fails: Not all sweeps reverse (some break through)

Fix: Require higher timeframe alignment and potential reversal confirmation
