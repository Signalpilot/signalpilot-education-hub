Support/Resistance & swing structure

Reading time ~10–12 min • Educational only

Working theory: structure is the map. We trade at structure, not in the void. Use fresh swing points to define zones, then require evidence.

Draw zones, not lines

Mark the last pivot highs/lows that led to a meaningful impulse. Expand them into zones that capture the reaction area (wick/body), not a 1-px line.

BOS/CHOCH in plain English

  • BOS (Break of Structure): continuation. In an uptrend, a clean break above the prior swing high.
  • CHOCH (Change of Character): first meaningful break against the sequence — the earliest sign of regime shift.

Confluence that upgrades a zone

  • Anchors: AVWAP (session/event) or prior open runs through the zone.
  • Acceptance: range mid overlaps the zone.
  • Participation: PVA/OBV print on the reclaim/touch.

Rules of engagement

  • Trade the reclaim (close back above demand / below supply) or the first pullback after BOS.
  • Require close-confirmed timing. Wick taps without a close don’t count.
  • Freshness beats age. First two touches matter most; after three, discount the zone.

Worked example

HTF uptrend. Demand zone from the last sell-to-buy impulse overlaps session AVWAP. Price dips into zone, prints PVA Bull on close → entry with stop 1.3×ATR below zone, target mid → prior high.

Checklist

  • Mark clean swing zones; label BOS/CHOCH.
  • Look for anchor overlap (AVWAP/open).
  • Require participation on reclaim/touch.
  • Stop outside zone (≥ k×ATR), size to fixed R.
