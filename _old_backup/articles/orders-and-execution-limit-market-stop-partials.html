Orders & execution (limit/market/stop, partials)

Reading time ~12–15 min • Educational only

Order types

  • Market: certain fill, uncertain price (pay spread + slippage).
  • Limit: certain price, uncertain fill (great for pullbacks/fades).
  • Stop-market: becomes market on trigger (best for breakouts when missing is worse than slippage).
  • Stop-limit: avoids runaway slippage but risks no fill in fast moves.

Slippage math

Average slippage 0.05R → 10 trades = 0.5R lost to friction. Bake into **expectancy** or pick entries that avoid it.

Partials vs trailing

  • Scale-out (trim 1/2 at 1R, trail rest): lower variance, capped upside.
  • All-in/out: higher variance, captures fat tails better.

Execution checklist

  • Decide order type before the setup prints.
  • Use limit at structure (demand/supply/anchor). Use stop-market through key breaks with RVOL.
  • On thin books: prefer limits; accept missed trades over terrible fills.
